Course Icons

Road signs let you know what to expect as you drive. Similarly, there will be some symbols or icons found throughout this course. Icons are used in the course to draw your attention to some specific things. Here are icons you may see.

Resources

Throughout the course, you will need support of various resources, such as a vocabulary summary or a grammar explanation. This icon indicates that you have some resources you will find useful for the lesson/assessment you are currently working on. You are in luck!

Collaboration

This icon indicates that there are collaboration opportunities available for you, on which you can work together with your classmates, using the knowledge you have obtained at that point. Collaboration opportunities are often provided with choices, and you usually will have till the end of a segment to work on these collaboration projects.

Notes

This icon usually appears right after some new vocabulary is introduced, and the notes provide supplemental information that you should know about some of the new words. Remember that you may be assessed on the content introduced in the “notes” sections.

Helpful Hint

In this course, this icon usually appears before you are about to start reading long paragraphs or watching a video. It provides reading or listening strategies you can use to assist your upcoming reading or listening activities with efficiency.

Self-Check

This icon indicates there are some practices you can do on your own. Self-check practices in this course provide clear and instant feedback for your practice. They can help immediately reinforce your learning in an effective way.

Toolbox

It's where you keep the extra tools you need to help you complete the task ahead. Your toolbox has special Chinese tools to help you complete this course. We encourage you to reach out to your teacher, but always check the toolbox first when you have questions. You will find the dialogue videos and extended explanations about the grammar or other concepts that you might need extra help with. In the toolbox section, you will also find vocabulary lists that will help you succeed on your tests. Make sure you print it before you start the lesson. There are templates to help complete the assignments. Please take some time to explore all the tools at your fingertips.

It’s Also Said

In English, some people say pop, and others say soda. There are similar variations in Chinese, due to its vast use in different regions. In this course, you will also be introduced to some common terms and expressions used in different Chinese-speaking regions that bear the same meanings as the formal term in Mandarin Chinese that you are learning. You won't be required to use these on the assessments, but please feel free to keep a list of them with your vocabulary for your personal use or even to show them off in your presentational speaking and writing assignments.

Borrowed Words

Did you know that some words can have the same definition, as well as look and sound similar? This happens in most languages. A cognate is a word that comes from the same origin as a word from a different language. Cognates between languages usually have similarities in spelling, pronunciation, and meaning. In this course, you will learn some cognates between Chinese and English.

Think and Apply

Want to show off your Chinese skills? Whenever you see the Think and Apply icon, you will have the opportunity to practice what you have learned in the lesson with family and friends. Take advantage of practicing your skills.

Assessment

At the end of each lesson, you will see the assessment icon and the instructions for turning in your assessment. You will be asked to complete a quiz or exam or submit an assignment for your instructor to grade.

Course Interactives

Watch Your Tones Practice

This interactive provides you an opportunity to verbally practice your pronunciations and self-check the accuracy of your work.

Checkpoint Practice

This interactive helps you review the vocabulary you have learned by providing multi-media questions in various complexity levels.

Flip Cards

This interactive presents you with culture lessons in a clear, dynamic, and efficient manner.

Self-check Practice

This interactive helps you review the grammar lessons you just learned by practicing and giving immediate feedback.

Talks of Friends for Life

This interactive present culture insights through the perspectives of two best friends from different backgrounds and the comparisons between them.

First Person Narration

This interactive provides information on cultures and lives of local people through the eyes of students or teenagers from different Chinese-speaking regions in the world.

Drag & Drop

This interactive helps you review various knowledge points by dragging and dropping verbal or written items to the appropriate places on a graph or image, using the knowledge you have learned.

Vocabulary Tiles

This interactive helps you study new vocabulary words by presenting them in multiple forms and providing tools for you to verbally practice them.
