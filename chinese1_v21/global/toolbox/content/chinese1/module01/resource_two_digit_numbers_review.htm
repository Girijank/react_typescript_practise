How to say Two-Digit Numbers in Chinese

To say two-digit numbers in Chinese is simple. Just grab two digits, put a 十shí between then, voilà! You are done.

For example, 31 would be 三十一sānshíyī; 64 would be 六十四liùshísì; and 99 would be 九十九jiǔshíjiǔ.

There are two exceptions:

  1. If the first digit is “one,” you don’t have to say it. For example, use 十二shièr for “12”; 十六shíliù for “16.”
  2. If the second digit is “zero,” you don’t have to say it. For example. 二十èrshí for “20”; and 五十wǔshí for “50.”
