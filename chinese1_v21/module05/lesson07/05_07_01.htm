演讲时间 Yǎnjiǎng shíjiān Presentational Speaking

It is time to showcase your verbal skills on what you have learned. For this presentation, you will record an audio or a video, speaking in complete sentences in Chinese. Your audio or video should include the grammar and vocabulary from the lessonlessons.

Using complete sentences in Chinese, talk about a Chinese home and compare it with a home in the United States. Research and describe one of the five types of Chinese homes you learned about previously in this module. Talk about it as if it were the home of your friend living in China. Then, talk about the home in the United States, based on the dream house plan you have created in the workshop for this module. You will compare the two homes regarding their types, compositions, and interior furniture items.

Note

  • Your friend’s home and your dream home can both be fictitious. They do not have to be real.
  • For your friend’s home, review Types of Chinese Homes you have learned about in this course.
  • You can research and describe a different type of Chinese home besides the ones you just reviewed.
  • You can say your teacher’s name in English if he or she doesn’t have a Chinese name.
  • Use the phrase “my friend” or a third-person pronoun when describing your friend’s home.
  • For your home, use the house plan you have created for the lesson “ Workshop” in this module.
  • Use the verbs 有 yǒu (have) and/or 没有 méiyǒu (do not have) when describing the items in the two homes.
  • Use the adverb 都 dōu (both/all) when describing the item(s) that the two homes have in common.
  • Review Parts of a House and Furniture vocabulary you have learned in this course.

  2. Know the requirements of your presentation. (Note: The sample English sentences in parentheses are just a guide. You need to come up with your own original sentences in Chinese.)

    • Say what type of home your friend has. (e.g., My friend’s home is a castle.)
    • Say two types of rooms your friend’s home has and how many there are. (e.g., His castle has three attics and two gardens.)
    • Say what type of home you have. (e.g., My home is a cabin.)
    • Say two types of rooms your home has and how many there are. (e.g., My cabin has one watchtower and two patios.)
    • Say one furniture item both your friend’s and your home have and which room it is in. (e.g., My friend’s and my studies both have rocking chairs.)
    • Say one furniture item your home has that your friend’s home does not. State which room in your home it is in. (e.g., My basement has a pool table. His basement does not have one.)
  3. Finalize your oral presentation
    • Your recording should be about 15-30 seconds long.
    • Give your presentation in Chinese, using appropriate vocabulary and grammar.
    • Start your presentation by stating your first and last name in English. (e.g., My name is Angie Liu.)
    • Speak clearly and loudly enough for the microphone to record all your words.
    • Speak at an appropriate rate. (Don’t speak too quickly or too slowly, and do not take extended pauses.)
    • Use correct tones and pronunciations throughout the speech.
    • Practice a couple of times, first in a mirror, or in front of a family member or friend.

Congratulations! You are on your way to becoming a fluent Chinese speaker!
