准备工作 Zhǔnbèi gōngzuò Preparation

It is important to do some warm-ups before you do any exercise. It is the same for doing Chinese writing. Always be well prepared before you plunge into this fun activity. Now let’s review the writing rules of Chinese characters.

Writing Rules

Review the following writing rules, with demonstrations of these rules, in some parts of the characters you’ve learned in or before this module.

Radicals in a Chinese character are written in order of “top to bottom” and “left to right.”

架 jià—top to bottom


阳 yáng—left to right

Write horizontal strokes, then vertical ones.

床 chuáng

Write left-falling strokes, then right-falling ones.

椅 yǐ

For “inside and outside” characters, the outside frame (completely or half-closed) is completed last.

窗 chuāng

For a symmetrical character, draw the middle first, then left, then right.

桌 zhuō
