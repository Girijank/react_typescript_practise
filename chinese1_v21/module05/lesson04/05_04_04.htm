Now let’s put these characters into action. You will create a simple house plan, based on one of the scenarios provided below. You will then write one or two sentences in English to describe one or two feng shui rules you have applied to the house design.

Before creating your own, look at in Chinese. This is a common house plan in Chinese for a three-bedroom, two-bathroom apartment. This is for your reference, and you will not be assessed on the new words in this sample house plan. Your plan will be similar to this but simpler.

When creating the house plan, you can use the characters taught in this module and/or the characters you have learned previously.

Use the Workshop Template for your work. In the template, there are components listed under the work area. You can move or duplicate these components to fill up the work area. The lines indicate the space where you should fill out the names of the components, such as names of the rooms, furniture items, doors, and windows.


At the bottom of your house plan, you will also need to write one or two feng shui rules you have applied to your house plan. Use the feng shui rules review to find the feng shui rule(s) you wish to apply.

You can also create your own house plan design or look online for an existing template that you like. Again, remember that you can “customize” the template of your choice by adding or removing the components to suit your own situation. Enjoy the activity!

Scenario A

Create a house plan for your dream single-family house. Your house plan should include at least four rooms and at least two furniture items in each room. Also include at least one outdoor component, such as a yard. Then, write one or two sentences in English to describe one or two feng shui rules you have applied to the house design.

Scenario B

Create a house plan for your dream apartment. Your house plan should include at least four rooms and at least two furniture items in each room. Also include at least one outdoor component, such as a balcony. Then, write one or two sentences in English to describe one or two feng shui rules you have applied to the house design.

Complete the contents of the house plan, using the appropriate characters from the demonstrations from the "Writing Chinese Characters" teaching and/or any other characters you've learned previously. You can choose to handwrite the characters or type the characters directly onto a word document and then save the document as your work.

Note:

  • Be sure to type or handwrite the characters in the worksheet instead of copying and pasting them from the lesson. You are encouraged to handwrite the characters when possible. It is the best way to understand the characters.
  • For this activity, you can also consider using the characters you’ve learned and used in the previous workshops, wherever you see it applicable.

Be Creative

You may choose an image from clipart, a picture, or a drawing of your own as the decorative icon or component in your house plan.

What to include in your house plan Hint

Rooms


Place and label at least four rooms on your house plan.

Think about:


  • Use the “Horizontal wall” and “Vertical wall” components in the template to divide the rooms.
  • Use the “Room” component in the template to label the rooms.
  • Use common characters in the titles of rooms, such as 室 shì, 厅 tīng, and 房 fáng.
