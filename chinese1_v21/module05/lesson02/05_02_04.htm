Notes

In both words 枕头 zhěntóu (pillow) and 床头柜 chuángtóuguì (nightstand), the character 头 tóu means “head” or “head of something.”

It’s Also Said

Another verb for “tidy up/set up” is 铺 pū, which is specifically used for beds — 铺床 pūchuáng (to tidy up bed). However, 整理 zhěnglǐ (to tidy up/to sort out) can be used for beds and other things that need to be tidied up or sorted out such as “tidy up the living room” or “sort out the backpack.”


打个卡 Dǎgèkǎ Checkpoint

There is a Chinese saying 一分耕耘，一分收获 yīfēn gēngyún, yīfēn shōuhuò. It means, “every bit of hard ploughing earns you a bit of harvest.” Being diligent is the path toward success. It works for your everyday life and for learning Chinese as well. Let’s continue the “hard ploughing” by practicing the new vocabulary words you’ve just learned.
