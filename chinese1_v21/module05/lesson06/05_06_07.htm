你好！我叫彭佩瑜。我住在中国广西省灵山县。我的爸爸妈妈在广州工作。我和我的爷爷奶奶和妹妹一起生活。我们家有一个院子，三个卧室，一个厨房和一个客厅。我的卧室有床和衣柜。我妹妹的卧室有床和书桌。我们的客厅有桌子和椅子，我们每天在这里吃早餐和晚餐。我们的院子很大。夏天的时候，我们在院子里开着电扇看电视。我喜欢我的家。

Hello! My name is Peiyu Peng. I live in Lingshan County in Guangxi Province, China. My parents are working in Guangzhou City. I live with my paternal grandparents and younger sister. Our house has a courtyard, three bedrooms, one kitchen, and one living room. My bedroom has a bed and a dresser. My younger sister’s room has a bed and a desk. Our living room has a table and chairs, and we have breakfast and dinner here every day. Our courtyard is very large. In the summer, we watch TV in the courtyard with the fan on. I like my house.

我爷爷和奶奶年老了。我和我妹妹做很多家务。每天上午我们扫院子和整理客厅，然后去学校。每天下午放学后，我和妹妹去地里摘蔬菜，然后在厨房做饭。吃了晚餐，我要洗碗和扫地，妹妹要擦桌子和倒垃圾。晚上我们写作业或是看电视。我们每天都很开心。

My paternal grandparents are old. My younger sister and I do many chores. Every morning we sweep the courtyard, tidy up the living room, and then go to school. Every afternoon after school, my younger sister and I will go pick vegetables in the field, and then we cook in the kitchen. After dinner, I need to wash the dishes and sweep the floor, and my younger sister needs to wipe the table and take the trash out. In the evening, we write homework or watch TV. We are very happy every day.

Let’s learn some more about Peiyu’s life and her hometown in Southern China.

Minorities in Southern China

There are 55 minority ethnic groups in China—many of whom are scattered among provinces in Southern China. For example, in Guangxi Province where Peiyu and her family are from, there are 12 long-term, living minority groups including Zhuang, Yao, Miao, Dong, Gelao, Maonan, etc. Zhuang is the most populated minority group in Guangxi (about 15 million), and it has its own language, culture, and traditions. Zhuang group is best known for its wooden houses with a distinct style.

“Left-Behind Children” in China

In recent years, it has become common in rural China for adults to leave their family behind and seek opportunities in big cities to make more money. The children they leave behind to grandparents or relatives are usually called 留守儿童 Liúshǒuértóng (“left-behind children” or “stay-home children”). It is estimated that there are over 60 million left-behind children in China—about 40 million of whom are 14 or older. These older children usually take responsibilities of their own everyday lives as well as their younger siblings’.

Scenery in Southern China

Southern China is always known for its beautiful scenery. For example, Guangxi Province has many of the world-famous scenic areas such as the Li River and the Elephant Trunk Hill in Guilin. There are many other scenic sites in Guangxi such as the Detian Waterfall, the Huangtao Ancient Town, the Chengyang Wind and Rain Bridge, and the Yangshuo Scenic Town.
