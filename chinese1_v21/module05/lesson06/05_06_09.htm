Structure Review

When introducing your chores, remember to use the structure we learned before. You will need this structure for the writing portion of your quiz.

Subject + 要 yào (be going to) + verb + object

For example:

我姐姐要打扫浴室。 Wǒ jiějie yào dǎsǎo yùshì. (My older sister is going to clean the bathroom.)

Ready to learn more about chores and responsibilities in households in China? Let’s explore some more about doing laundry in China. You will find out that things are very different over there!

Do you remember Lili Chen who is working on a project for her social studies class? Now she’s researching some interesting facts she’s learned about laundry in China. Below is what she’s found so far.

©iStock

Laundry Rooms in Homes

Laundry rooms are not common in apartments in China. This is mostly due to the lack of space. Washing machines are usually placed in the bathroom or on the balcony.

©iStock

Dryers

Dryers are not common. Clothes are hung out on clotheslines on the balcony or courtyard or just outside of the window to air dry. It is believed that the sunlight kills the bacteria and germs on the clothing items.


©iStock

Public Laundry Services

Public laundry services are not yet very common in China, due to people’s sometimes overly-private discretion on personal hygiene. They are now popular in colleges or boarding schools. Young people are starting to welcome the concept because of its convenience and efficiency. This is also because many young people live in smaller apartments that don’t have enough room for laundry appliances.

©iStock

Dry-Cleaning

People will go to public dry cleaning services for cleaning of large household items such as curtains and comforters or for clothes made of material that requires special treatment.
