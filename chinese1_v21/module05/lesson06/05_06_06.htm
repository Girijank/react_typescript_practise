垃圾日 Lājī rì Trash Day

Let’s continue to expand your vocabulary of bathroom items and some more verbs you can use to describe everyday chores around the house.

Note: You may practice writing the characters of the words or phrases in your note guide, but you will not be assessed on writing them at this time.

Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio, and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need, to sound like a native speaker!

Note:

  • In both words 马桶 mǎtǒng (toilet) and 垃圾桶 lājītǒng (garbage can), the last character, 桶 tǒng, means “bucket.” In the word 垃圾桶 lājītǒng (garbage can), the first two characters, 垃圾 lājī, mean “garbage/trash.”
  • The verb 倒 dào (to empty out) should be followed by the content to be emptied out, instead of the container for it. For example, they use 倒垃圾 dào lāijī (empty out trash) and 倒水 dàoshuǐ (to pour out water).
  • In the word 卫生纸 wèishēngzhǐ (toilet paper), the last character 纸 zhǐ means “paper.” Now you can use it together with some verbs you’ve learned before such as 剪纸 jiǎnzhǐ (cutting papers), which also refers to the paper cutting art.
