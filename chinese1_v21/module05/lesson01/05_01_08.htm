描述我的家 Miáoshù wǒde jiā Describing My House

Nice job learning the words on types of houses, different parts of a house, and some preposition words. Now it’s time to put them together to inquire about and describe your home sweet home. Let’s get going.


Inquire about the Composition of a House

Remember 多少 duōshǎo (how many) and 几 jǐ (how many (answered with small numbers)) that you learned previously to inquire about phone numbers or ages? They can also be used to ask about the composition of a house.

For example:

  • 你的公寓有几个卧室？Nǐ de gōngyù yǒu jǐgè wòshì?
    How many bedrooms does your family’s house have?
  • 他家的大房子有多少个阳台？Tājiā de dàfángzi yǒu duōshǎo gè yángtá?
    How many balconies does his family’s big house have?
Describing the Existence of Houses or Rooms

Remember 有 yǒu (to have) that you learned previously to describe your family members? It can also be used to describe the existence of your house and rooms.

For example:

  • 我家的房子有一个车库。Wǒjiā de fángzi yǒu yīgè chēkù.
    My family’s house has a garage.
  • 她的公寓有两个阳台。Tā de gōngyù yǒu liǎnggè yángtái.
    Her apartment has two balconies.

Note:

The measure word 个 gè is universally used when describing the quantity of an object. There are many more measure words you can use for specific objects. You will learn all about them in the future.

Describing the Locations of Houses or Rooms

Remember 在 zài (in/on/at) that you learned previously to describe where you live? It can also be used to describe the location of your house or rooms.

For example:

  • 我姐姐的公寓在三楼。Wǒ jiějie de gōngyù zài sānlóu.
    My older sister’s apartment is on the third floor.
  • 我(的)卧室的门在左边，窗户在右边。Wǒ (de) wòshì de mén zài zuǒbiān, chuānghù zài yòubiān.
    In my bedroom, the door is on the left, and the window is on the right.

Note:

Later in this module, you will learn how to use preposition words to describe relative locations of furniture items in your house.

More Examples

Let’s look at a few pairs of questions and answers, using the previously discussed words.

For example:

  • 你的公寓在几楼？Nǐde gōngyù zài jǐlóu? (Which floor is your apartment on?)
  • 我的公寓在五楼。Wǒde gōngyù zài wǔlóu. (My apartment is on the fifth floor.)
  • 你家的房子有几个浴室？Nǐjiā de fángzi yǒu jǐgè yùshì? (How many bathrooms does your house have?)
  • 我家的房子有三个浴室。Wǒjiā de fángzi yǒu sāngè yùshì. (My house has three bathrooms.)

Think and Apply

祝贺你！Zhùhè nǐ! Congratulations! You’ve tackled this lesson with excellence! It’s time to showcase what you’ve learned to a family member or friend. It’s going to be a great topic to discuss with them! You can showcase and teach your family member or friend the following:

  • Introduce two attachments (such as patio) or rooms (such as sitting room) of your house, using complete sentences in Chinese with 有 yǒu (to have).
  • Describe the location of two rooms in your house, using complete sentences in Chinese with preposition words 在 zài (in/on/at) and 左边 zuǒbiān (left side) or 右边 yòubiān (right side).
  • Tell your friend or family member two types of houses in China. Explain their characteristics.
  • Pick one feng shui rule that you find the most interesting, and explain it to your friend or family member.
  • Explain how to pronounce initial “f” and final “ian” in Chinese, and compare the letter sounds to similar pronunciation(s) in your own language.
