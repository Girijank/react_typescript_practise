Introduction of Beijing

Beijing is the capital of the People’s Republic of China. It is located in Northern China. Beijing has a history of over 3000 years. It is one of the largest cities in the world, with a population of over 21 million and an administrative area of 6,400 square miles. Beijing is the political, cultural, economic, and educational center of the nation. It is both modern and ancient with a lot to offer.

Climate in Beijing

Beijing has a continental climate and has four distinct seasons. Its spring is chilly and windy. Its summer is generally hot, humid, and rainy. Autumn is cool, pleasant, and beautiful in Beijing, when the surrounding mountains are covered with colorful leaves. Winter in Beijing is generally cold, and the temperature can go as low as 11 degrees Fahrenheit.

Places to Go in Beijing

With its long history, Beijing has abundant historical and cultural sites to visit, as well as natural scenery. Below are some places to go when in Beijing:

  • The Forbidden City – The Imperial Palace complex of ancient China
  • The Great Wall of China – A series of grand fortifications built in history to protect China from the nomadic invaders
  • The Beijing National Stadium – Nicknamed the Bird’s Nest, built for the 2008 Beijing Olympics
  • The Fragrant Hills Park – An urban forest park in the Northwestern part of Beijing, known for its spectacular red tree leaves covering the mountains in the fall

Beijing Dialect

The Beijing dialect (北京话 Běijīng Huà), also known as Pekingese, is the dialect of Mandarin Chinese generally spoken in urban China. The Beijing dialect is the phonetic basis of the standard Mandarin Chinese; therefore, they are very similar. A notable difference is that the Beijing dialect uses the “er” sound at the end of certain words. Later in this module, you will learn more about the Beijing dialect. But if you speak Mandarin Chinese, you will have no trouble getting around in Beijing.
