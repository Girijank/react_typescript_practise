精致生活有风水 Jīngzhì shēnghuó yǒu fēngshuǐ Fine Living with Feng Shui

Do you remember Lirong Zhang, the girl from Shanghai, China? She’s been researching about how feng shui affects housing in China, and she’s interviewed a feng shui master, Mr. Liu, in her community. Let’s see what Mr. Liu had to say about feng shui for living rooms and kitchens.

Kitchen feng shui

  • The kitchen should not be an irregular shape because it is believed to affect the family’s health.
  • The stove should not face the kitchen door, and the kitchen should not face the front door of the house.
  • The stove should not be facing or right next to the sink because the elements fire and water are not compatible.
  • The stove should not be in the center of the kitchen. Too much heat in the center will cause disharmony in the family.

Kitchen feng shui (continued)

  • Avoid placing mirrors in the kitchen. If there is one, it should not reflect the stove flames, which is believed to bring fire or misfortune to the house.
  • In the kitchen, knives should not be hung on the wall or be in plain sight. They should be concealed in places such as drawers.
  • Garlic, onion, and peppers should also be hidden in the kitchen. These items absorb yin energy, which will affect the family’s health.
  • The fridge and rice/food jars should be kept in the kitchen, and they should be filled with food all the time to symbolize abundance. It also helps bring fortune to put three Chinese coins in a red packet and place it in the rice/food jar.

Kitchen feng shui (continued)

  • The kitchen should not be next to the bathroom to avoid the unclean air coming from the bathroom to contaminate the meals.
  • The kitchen floor should be lower than the living room or bedroom.
  • The kitchen should not be placed between two bedrooms. It brings misfortune to the people living in them.
  • The washing machine should not be placed in the kitchen, so the unclean water flow will not mix with meals.
  • Indoors stairs in multi-level houses should not directly face the kitchen. This will cause unnecessary troubles for the family.

Living Room feng shui

  • It is good luck to have the living room at the center of the house.
  • The living room should not have a sharp corner or edge facing the front door. If so, a plant can be placed there to conceal it.
  • The shape of the living room should be square or rectangular. Irregularly shaped living rooms can be improved with plants, furniture, or dividers.
  • The facing of living rooms is decided by which direction its windows open to. Living rooms facing south should be painted in white; facing north, red; facing west, green; and facing east, yellow.

Living Room feng shui (continued)

  • In the living room, the corner diagonal to the front door of the house is usually called the “fortune spot.” A sofa can be placed at the fortune spot. Often, sitting there can bring you good fortune. You can also place a plant at the fortune spot. Avoid placing anything there that vibrates, such as a stereo system. It will affect your fortune.
  • In the living room, an end table should be round-shaped. It should be placed next to a sofa, but it should not be larger than the sofa.
  • • Having a fish tank in the living room is good. But avoid placing it behind a sofa or on the “fortune spot.” This will cause good fortune to flow away.

Living Room feng shui (continued)

  • Avoid white pillars in the living room. It resembles a white candle, which symbolizes funerals.
  • Paintings of plants or amiable animals can be hung in the living room. If hanging a landscape painting, be sure the water/landscape flows in the painting point toward the inside of the house, not outside. If hanging a vicious animal painting, such as tiger or a lion, be sure the head of the animal in the painting points toward the outside of the house, not inside.
