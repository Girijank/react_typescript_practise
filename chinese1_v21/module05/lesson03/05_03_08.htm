Apply It!

Now you’ve learned the feng shui in the living room and kitchen. Isn’t it cool? Now let’s put these learnings in action. Survey the kitchen and living room in your house very closely. Do they follow any of the feng shui rules you just learned in this lesson? If so, share with your parents what feng shui rules the rooms follow, and if possible, what benefits they bring. If not, you may discuss with your parents about making changes or re-arrangements in the kitchen or living rooms based on some of the feng shui rules. Bring good luck to the common areas of your happy home!

Think and Apply

祝贺你！Zhùhè nǐ! Congratulations! You’ve completed this lesson with great excellence! Now, do you think you can showcase what you’ve learned to a family member or friend? You can showcase and teach your family or friend the following:

  • Identify two items in your kitchen or living room using Chinese.
  • Identify two chores you may do in your kitchen or living room using Chinese.
  • Explain how to pronounce initial “d” and finals “ia” and “iang,” as well as other compound nasal finals in Chinese and compare them to similar pronunciation(s) in your own language.
  • Research for an online Chinese almanac and use it to find what activities you should or should not do on the day when you learned this lesson.
  • Tell two kitchen or living room feng shui rules that you find interesting.
  • Identify several Chinese cooking methods, utensils, and the Chinese cooking routine. Compare them with those in your own culture. Share your findings with your family member or friend.
