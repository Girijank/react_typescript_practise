黄道吉日 Huángdào jírì My Lucky Day

Have you ever looked at a Chinese calendar closely? It usually has the Western dates in large print, since it’s universal, and then Chinese writing on the bottom. Let’s learn more about it.

These writings usually contain two things, the lunar calendar dates, and suitable activities for each particular day. Such suitable activities are extracted from an ancient Chinese farmers almanac called 黄历 huánglì (the Yellow Calendar), also known as 通胜 tōngshèng (Tung Shing) in Cantonese-speaking regions.



The Origin of the Chinese Farmers Almanac

The Chinese Farmers Almanac, known in Chinese as 黄历 huánglì, originated in ancient China. It was said to have been created by the Emperor Huangdi thousands of years ago. It contains information, such as lunar calendar dates, festivals, solar terms, as well as climate changes, as a guide for farmers’ agricultural activities.

The Functions of the Chinese Farmers Almanac

In the ancient times, the Chinese Farmers Almanac was mostly used to guide farmers’ activities and for government officials to date signing of documents or important events. It is now a guide for people’s everyday lives, by telling dos and don’ts for each day based on an ancient algorithm called Huangdao. Because of the difference between the Western calendar and lunar calendar, the same Western date on different years will fall on different lunar dates. Here are some examples:

Western Calendar Lunar Calendar Dos Don’ts
March 18th, 2021 2nd month, 6th day having a haircut, making trades, visiting relatives moving, planting
March 18th, 2024 2nd month, 9th day tailoring, making trades, starting school going out, burying, getting married
March 18th, 2028 2nd month, 15th day getting engaged, digging the ground, renovating opening business, getting married, building a kitchen

The Chinese Farmers Almanac and houses

Many dos and don’ts given in the Chinese Farmers Almanac involve the arrangements of houses, as well as moving, building, and demolishing of houses. Below are examples of house-related dos and don’ts.

  • 10/11/2020 (lunar calendar 8th month, 25th day)

    • suitable for: cleaning a house, moving into a new home.

  • 7/11/2025 (lunar calendar 6th month, 17th day)

    • not suitable for: building a kitchen, moving into a new house

  • 4/13/2027 (lunar calendar 3th month, 7th day)

    • suitable for: demolishing a house

  • 4/18/2027 (lunar calendar 3th month, 12th day)

    • not suitable for: moving a home, installing a door
