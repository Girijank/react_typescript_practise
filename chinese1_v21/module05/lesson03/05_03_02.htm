声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson, we will learn initial “d” and finals “ia” and “iang.”

Initial “d”

This initial sounds like “d” in English as in “dog” or “do.” It’s used in words such as “叠 dié (to fold)” and “短裤 duǎnkù (shorts).”

Final “ia”

This final sounds like the combination of “ee” in English as in “eel” and “a” in English as in “father.” It’s used in words such as “夹克 jiākè (jacket)” and “下午 xiàwǔ (p.m./afternoon).”

Final “iang”

This nasal compound final is simply the combination of finals “i” and “ang,” and you pronounce them in the order as they are. It’s used in words such as “凉鞋 liángxié (sandals)” and “香港 xiānggǎng (Hong Kong).”

Note: In Chinese pinyin, there are also other combinations of finals such as “uang” (“u” and “ang”) and “iong” (“I” and “ong”). By now you may not have seen some of these combinations, but you will in the future. To pronounce them, you simply pronounce the individual finals in the order as they are.
