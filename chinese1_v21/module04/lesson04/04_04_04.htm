我的课程表 Wǒde kèchéngbiǎo My Schedule

Now let’s put these characters into action. You will create a school or activity schedule, based on one of the scenarios provided below. You will then write one sentence in Chinese characters to describe a skill you’ve obtained or used in one of the classes or activities on your schedule.

Before creating your own, look at in Chinese. This is a common school schedule in Chinese high schools. This is for your reference, and you will not be assessed on the new words in this sample schedule. Your schedule will be similar to this but simpler.


When creating the school schedule and writing the sentences, you can use the characters taught in this module and/or the characters you’ve learned previously. You are expected to use at least 40 characters to complete your school schedule.

Use the basic template for class or activity schedules. In the template, the English in each grid is the prompt and can be removed once you fill out the grid with Chinese characters.

You can also create your own schedule design or look online for an existing template that you like. Remember that you can “customize” the template of your choice by adding or removing columns or rows to suit your own situation. Enjoy the activity!

Scenario A

Create a simple schedule for your current school year. Your schedule should cover from Monday to Friday. For each day, include at least two classes. For each class, include its start and end times and at least one supply for that class. On the bottom of your schedule, write one short sentence to introduce a skill you’ve learned or used in one of the classes listed on your schedule.

Scenario B

Create a simple schedule for your summer break. Your schedule should cover from Monday to Friday. For each day, include at least two activities. For each activity, include its start and end times and at least one supply for that activity. On the bottom of your schedule, write one short sentence to introduce a skill you’ve learned or used in one of the activities listed on your schedule.

Complete the contents of the school schedule, using the appropriate characters from the demonstrations on the “Writing Chinese Characters” page and/or any other characters you’ve learned previously. You can choose to handwrite the characters or type the characters directly onto a word document and then save the document as your work.

Note:

  • Be sure to type or handwrite the characters in the worksheet instead of copying and pasting them from the lesson. You are encouraged to handwrite the characters when possible. It is the best way to understand the characters.
  • For this activity, you can also consider using the characters you’ve learned and used in the previous workshops, wherever you see it applicable.

Be Creative

You may choose an image from clipart, a picture, or a drawing of your own as the background or decoration for your schedule.

You can use the checklist below to help with your work:

What to include in your schedule Hint

Day of the Week:

Write the days of the week on the first row.

Think about:

  • What structure do you use for days of the week?
  • Review how to say and write one-digit numbers that you will use in saying a day of the week

Time Duration:

Write the start and end times of your class or activity before and after the dash.

Think about:

  • Remember to use a.m./p.m. in your time structure.
  • Remember how to say time with only the hour part.

Here is an example to help you get started. (The example may have some new words and is just for your reference. Your work should be in your own words, using vocabulary and grammar taught in this module.):

十一点三刻 --- 十二点一刻

Class/Activity

Write the name of the class or activity for that day and time.

Think about:

  • What character do you use for “class”?
  • Review different class subjects you’ve learned so far.

Here is an example to help you get started. (The example may have some new words and is just for your reference. Your work should be in your own words, using what’s taught in this module.):

地理课

Supply:

Write at least one supply you need for a particular class.

Think about:

  • How do you express needing something in Chinese?

Here is an example to help you get started. (The example may have some new words and is just for your reference. Your work should be in your own words, using what’s taught in this module.):

我需要地图。

Learned or Used Skill:

  • Write one short sentence to introduce a skill you’ve learned or used in a class/activity listed on your schedule.

Think about:

  • What verb should you use to indicate being in a class (e.g., “attending”)?
  • What verb should you use to introduce a learned skill (e.g., “know how to”)?

Here is an example to help you get started. (The example may have some new words and is just for your reference. Your work should be in your own words, using what’s taught in this module.):

上日文课我会折纸。
