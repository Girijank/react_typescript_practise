日复一日 Rìfùyīrì Day in Day out

First, let’s go over some vocabulary about describing daily and weekly schedules. Every day we are doing something the same and something different. It’s good to know how to talk about them.

Note: Pay attention to the characters that are in bold type and highlighted in dark red. You will learn to write them later in the module. The characters highlighted in slate grey are from the previous lesson(s). You should continue practicing them. For characters not in bold type or highlighted, feel free to practice writing them in your note guide, but remember that for now, you will only need to know how to write the bold type or highlighted characters.

Notes

The word 日 rì (day) is formal. It is commonly used to say a date such as 一月四日 yīyuèsìrì (January 4th). The word 天 tiān (day(casual)) is used casually, especially in speaking, such as “三天 sāntiān (three days)” or “今天 jīntiān (today).” It cannot be used to say a date.
