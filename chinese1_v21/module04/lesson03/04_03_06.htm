能力和技巧 Nénglìhéjìqiǎo Capabilities and Skills

You’ve done a great job learning the vocabulary on different skills that you learn at school. You are improving in many aspects each day you spend at school. Keep up the good work! Now it’s time to showcase your abilities or skills in Chinese.


Describing capabilities

能 néng (to be able to) is used to describe a capability or possibility.

For example:

  • 我每天能吃三碗米饭。Wǒ mměitiān néng chī sān wǎn mǐfàn. I can (am able to) eat three bowls of rice each day.
  • 他一天能读四本书。Tā yītiān néng dú sì běn shū. He can (is able to) read four books in one day.

Note: 本 běn is the measure word used for the word “books.” You will learn more about Chinese measure words in the future.

Describing learned skills

会 huì (to know how to) is used to describe a skill obtained through learning and practicing.

For example:

  • 我哥哥会说中文。Wǒ gēgehuì shuō zhōngwén. My older brother knows how to speak Chinese.
  • 她会画小猫。Tāhuì huà xiǎomāo. She knows how to draw kitty cats.

Complete the practice to reinforce your learning on this grammar point.
