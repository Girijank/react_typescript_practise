学校法则 Xuéxiào fǎzé School Dos and Don’ts

What are some regulations you need to follow when going to school, and how do you discuss them with your friends or classmates? Let’s learn all about it.


Positive commands with 要 yào (need to)

You’ve learned 需要 xūyào (to need), the 2nd character of which, 要 yào (need to), can be used to form positive imperative sentences.

For example:

上音乐课要唱歌。Shàngy īnyuèkèyàochànggē. (You) need to sing in music class.

Note: Imperative sentences in Chinese don’t always require a subject (you).

Negative commands with 不要 bùyào (do not)

By adding 不 bù (not) before 要 yào (to need to), you have the phrase 不要 bùyào (do not), with which you can form negative imperative sentences.

For example:

上数学课不要说话。Shàngs hùxuékè bùyào shuōhuà. Do not talk in math class.

Note: Sometimes a character’s tone is changed when it is spoken in a word. You will learn more about this in the future.

Lunch

  • We usually eat at the school cafeteria where we pay with a school debit card or cash.
  • Lunches offered at school cafeterias are often less expensive than outside foods, and they are nutritious and healthy.
  • Some students bring their own lunches. Some schools also allow students to purchase lunch outside of school.
©iStock

Nap time

  • We have over an hour for lunch breaks. After lunch, many students choose to take a catnap in the classroom or somewhere quiet on campus to re-energize for the vigorous learning in the afternoon.
  • Some hard-working students will sacrifice their naptime for more study time. They usually use the time to review the classes they’ve taken before lunch.
  • In some schools, especially preschools and elementary schools, students are required to take a nap. They also need to bring their own blankets and pillows for naptime.
©iStock

School store

  • We usually have school-run stores on campus, where we can purchase school supplies, textbooks, and practice books, as well as fruits, drinks, and snacks.
  • There are also many privately-owned stores off campus within walking distance, which usually cluster around the school gate. These stores provide similar supplies as school stores. Many students choose to purchase from these stores on the way to and from school.
  • There are also diners or food stands near school gates, where students get breakfast or afternoon snacks. These places usually maintain a very good business.

Where do students in Chinese schools usually get lunch?

They usually get lunch at the school cafeteria, where they can purchase healthy food with a school debit card or cash. Some students bring their own lunches or purchase lunch off campus.

What do students in Chinese schools do during lunch breaks, besides having lunch?

They usually take a nap in the classroom or somewhere quiet on campus to re-energize for the vigorous learning in the afternoon. Some students choose to use the time to do more studying.

What can students in Chinese schools purchase at school stores?

They can purchase school supplies, textbooks, and practice books, as well as fruits, drinks, and snacks.

What types of businesses are usually seen right outside of gates in schools in China?

There are privately-owned stores that provide school supplies similar to school stores. There are also diners and food stalls where students can purchase their breakfasts and afternoon snacks. These places usually maintain a very good business.
