你可以的 Nǐkěyǐde You Can Do It

Let’s learn some words you can use to describe your skills and abilities at school.

Note: Pay attention to the characters that are in bold type and highlighted in dark red. You will learn to write them later in the module. The characters highlighted in slate grey are from the previous lesson(s). You should continue practicing them. For characters not in bold type or highlighted, feel free to practice writing them in your note guide, but remember that for now, you will only need to know how to write the bold type or highlighted characters.

Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio, and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need to sound like a native speaker!


Notes

  • Many verbs in Chinese, like 读 dúand 写 xiě, can’t be used alone. They need to be linked to an object to form a “verb + object” unit such as 读书 dú shū (read book) and 写作业 xiě zuòyè (write homework).You will learn more about the “verb + object” structure in the future.
  • The character 画 huà is also a noun meaning “drawing/picture,” so you can say 画画 huàhuà (draw pictures).
