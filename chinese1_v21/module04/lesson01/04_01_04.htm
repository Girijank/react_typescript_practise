去哪里? Qùnǎlǐ? Where Are You Going??

Do you know how to express that you are planning on going somewhere, using Chinese sentences? With a few phrases and words you’ve learned so far, you can now easily do that.


Planning on doing something

To discuss your plan to do something, you need the verb 要 yào (to want). You’ve learned previously to use it to order food or drinks. It also indicates “to be going to do something.” Now you can use it to make plans.

For Example:

  • 我要庆祝我的生日。 Wǒ yào qìngzhù wǒde shēngrì. I am going to celebrate my birthday.
  • 我爷爷要吃早餐。 Wǒ yéye yào chī zǎocān. My paternal grandpa is going to have breakfast.
Going somewhere

The verb 去 qù (to go to) is used to describe where one is going. It is followed by a destination.

For example:

  • 去学校 qù xuéxiào (going to the school)
  • 去餐厅 qù cāntīng (going to the restaurant)
  • 去我外婆家 qù wǒ wàipó jiā (going to my maternal grandmother’s house)
Planning on going somewhere

The verbs 要 yào (to want) and 去qù (to go to) can be used in combination to describe going somewhere.

For example:

  • 我姐姐要去学校。 Wǒ jiějie yào qù xuéxiào. My older sister is going to school.
  • 我要准时去你家。 Wǒ yào zhǔnshí qù nǐjiā. I am going to your house on time.

(Note where the word 准时 zhǔnshí (on time) is placed.)
