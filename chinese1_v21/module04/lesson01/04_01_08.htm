用起来 Yòng qǐlái Apply It

What an awesome journey! You can now discuss your class schedules with someone. You can talk about what time you have different classes, and you are now aware of the differences and similarities between class setup in China and in the U.S. How wonderful!

Let’s keep the fun going by implementing your learning to actual life. Here are two missions for you:

  • Do you have a favorite school subject that is not taught in this lesson? If so, ask your instructor how to say that particular subject in Chinese. You will then tell your friends your favorite school subject(s) in Chinese.
  • Compare your own class and classroom setup with that of Kailan or Karen, focusing on three aspects: classroom setup, daily time schedule, and selection of learning focus. Then tell a family member or friend how similar or different they are. Enjoy the activities!

Think and Apply

祝贺你！Zhùhè nǐ! Congratulations! You’ve tackled this lesson with excellence! It’s time to showcase to a family member or friend what you’ve learned. It’s going to be a great topic to discuss with them! You can showcase and teach your family member or friend the following:

  • Describe a typical school day of yours, including what time you go to school and what classes you have.
  • Say two sentences about what you plan to do or where you plan on going, using 要 yào (to want to) and/or 去 qù (to go to) in the sentences.
  • Tell your family member or friend the time at the moment in Chinese.
  • Explain how to pronounce “üe” in Chinese, and compare the letter sounds to similar pronunciation(s) in your own language.
  • Tell three facts you’ve learned about classroom setup, class time schedule, and learning focus selection in China, and then compare them with your own.
