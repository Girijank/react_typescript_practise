穿什么? Chuān shénme? What to Wear?

It’s your first day of school. Should you wear a coat or just a sweater? How do you say these items in Chinese and what verb should you use for them? Let’s go over some vocabulary to keep you covered.

Note: You may practice writing the characters of the words or phrases in your note guide, but you will not be assessed on writing them.

It’s Also Said

  • 穿 chuān (to wear (essential items)) and 戴 dài (to wear (accessories)) both mean “to wear,” but they are used for different items. Later in this lesson, you will learn when to use which one
  • 制服 zhìfú (uniform) refers to uniforms in general. To specify “school uniform,” you can say 校服 xiàofú (school uniform).
  • 衬衫 chènshān (shirt) refers to formal shirts. For a casual T-shirt, you will say T-恤 T-xù (T-shirt). It’s a borrowed word.

打个卡 Dǎgèkǎ Checkpoint

There is a Chinese phrase 磨杵成针 móchǔchéngzhēn. (Grind an iron bar into a needle.) If you work on it hard enough, it can be achieved. Continue expanding your vocabulary with perseverance, and one day you will see what a “fine needle” you can make.
