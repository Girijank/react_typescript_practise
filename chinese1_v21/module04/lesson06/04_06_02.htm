声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson, we will learn the initial “m” and the final “uan.”

Initial “m”

This initial sounds like “m” as in “move” and “man” in English. It’s used in words such as “每 měi (every)” and “毛衣 máoyī (sweater).” (You will learn this word in this lesson.)

Final “uan”

This final sounds like the combination of “oo” as in “loose” and “an” as in “can” in English. It’s used in words such as “元 yuán (dollar)” and “穿 chuān (to wear (essential items)).” (You will learn this word in this lesson.)
