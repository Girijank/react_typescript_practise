Verbs for “to Wear”

In Chinese there are two ways of saying “to wear”: 穿 chuān (to wear (essential items)) and 戴 dài (to wear (accessories)). You use 穿 chuān (to wear (essential items)) for essential items such as pants, skirts, etc. You use 戴 dài (to wear (accessories)) for accessories such as glasses, rings, etc.

Below is a list of common items the two verbs are used for.

Verbs for “to wear” Items used for
穿 chuān (to wear (essential items))

Essential items including:

pants, shirts, skirts, coats, jackets, shoes, socks, uniforms, sweaters, shorts, sandals, jeans, sneakers
戴 dài (to wear (accessories))

Accessories including:

gloves, glasses, masks, caps, hats, necklaces, earrings, rings, scarves, bracelets, watches


Example:

  • 我姐姐喜欢穿裙子。 Wǒ jiějie xǐhuān chuān qúnzi. (My older sister likes to wear skirts.)
  • 他爷爷经常戴眼镜。 Tā yéye jīngcháng dài yǎnjìng. (His paternal grandpa often wears glasses.)
