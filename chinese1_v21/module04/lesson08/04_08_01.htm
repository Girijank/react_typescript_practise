单元考试 Dānyuán kǎoshì Module Exam

You completed another module. Hooray! In this module, you learned about discussing various aspects of school life, including describing school schedules and different classes as well as the school supplies needed for them, stating skills learned at school, saying days of the week, talking about school dress, discussing school permissions and requirements, expressing needs, and much more. Now, it’s time to show off your reading, listening, and cultural knowledge. Before you dive into the exam, take a minute to study and review the topics below. Remember to use your note guides from each lesson to help you study.

  • Saying “a.m.” and “p.m.”
  • Saying different subjects and classes
  • Saying different school supplies
  • Saying different clothing items
  • Planning on doing something with "要 yào (to want)"
  • Describing going somewhere with “去 qù (to go to)”
  • Telling time, including the hour and the minute
  • Using 需要 xūyào(to need) to describe needs of school supplies for classes
  • Using 或是 huòshì (or) in a statement and understanding its difference from 还是 háishì (or)
  • Using different verbs for “to wear”穿 chuān and 戴 dài for different clothing items
  • Refreshing knowledge on borrowed words in Chinese
  • Saying days of the week
  • Describing school schedule including times, classes, and activities
  • Describing learned skills, using 会 huì (know how to)
  • Describing capabilities, using 能 néng (can/be able to)
  • Expressing permissions or requests, using 可以 kěyǐ (may/can)
  • Understanding when to use 会 huì (know how to), 能 néng (can/be able to), or 可以 kěyǐ (may/can)
  • Describing class activities, using “verb + object” structure
  • Understanding school dos and don’ts, using 不要 bùyào (do not)
  • Describing attending class, using 上 shàng (to attend (class))
  • Describing weather from being cold, cool, warm, to hot
  • Understanding class setup and learning focuses in Chinese schools
  • Describing campus life and classroom routines and setups
  • Knowing bell schedules and student lives in Chinese schools
  • Knowing various aspects of school life in Taiwan and its comparison with that in the U.S.
  • Knowing pinyin components you’ve learned so far (Also remember to practice the tones.):
    • initials: k, w, t
    • finals: a, üe, ang, u
