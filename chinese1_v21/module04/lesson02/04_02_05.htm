各取所需 Gèqǔsuǒxū Get What You Need

Now you have the vocabulary on different classes and school supplies. It’s time to learn how to put them together into sentences to describe what you need for which class.


General needs

The word 需要 xūyào (to need) is a verb that can be placed between a subject and object to describe needs in general.


For example:

我爷爷需要勺子。Wǒ yéye xūyào sháozi. My paternal grandpa needs a spoon.

Stating reason/ purpose

You can also add the reason / purpose of the need right before the word 需要 xūyào (to need).

For example:

我爷爷喝汤需要勺子。Wǒ yéye hētāng xūyào sháozi. To drink soup, my paternal grandpa needs a spoon.

School supply needs

With the structures introduced above, you can now describe the supply or supplies you need for each class, all in one sentence.

For example:

我去学校需要背包。Wǒ qù xuéxiào xūyào bèibāo. To go to school, I need a backpack.

我上英文课需要字典。Wǒ shàng yīngwénkè xūyào zìdiǎn. To attend English class, I need a dictionary.

Note: In Chinese sentences, articles such as “a”, “an,” or “the” are not needed. A noun can directly follow a verb.
