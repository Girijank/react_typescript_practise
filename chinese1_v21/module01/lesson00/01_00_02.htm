Let’s Talk About Integrity

Learning a new language is one of the best ways to boost your brain power, creativity, and even future job options. The key to success is using various resources to learn but completing tasks and assignments on your own. Take a look at these helpful hints for more!

Using a language dictionary incorrectly

Many words have multiple meanings. Take the word “fill” for example. It can be used as a verb, such as: “They fill the bottles” or “He is filling the position.” It can be also used as a noun, for example: “I ate my fill” or “The dentist gave him a filling.”

To avoid making mistakes when using a language dictionary, pay attention to context and parts of speech such as noun, verb, adjective, etc.

Asking a native speaker to help with your assignments

Talking to native speakers is a great way to increase your language skills, but having them complete or help on your assignments is a great way to earn a poor grade (and your teacher will be able to tell right away).

Language courses are designed to help you become successful and more fluent in your chosen foreign language. But that won’t happen if you take shortcuts.


Writing in English first

Writing is a great way to get comfortable in using and thinking in a new language. So, when you need to write an assignment in a foreign language, it’s best to start with a short outline in that language. Bonus: this will also save you time since you’re only writing one version.

Save even more time and effort with these foreign-language writing tips:

  • Keep your sentences simple.
  • Use vocabulary found in your course.
  • When responding to a question, use the terms in the question to form your response.
  • If you need help, ask your instructor.

Using a translator

Have you ever used a translator on a web page in another language? If so, you know that words and phrases don’t translate exactly from language to language.

For example:

A translator may say:
“Hang your friends outside,”

when you really mean:
“Hang out with your friends.”

If you need help translating a word, use a language dictionary or the glossary.

Studying foreign language only once a week

You know all the words to your favorite song because you’ve listened to it a thousand times or more…(We won’t judge.) Repetition such as this is essential when it comes to learning a new language. And that means studying once a week won’t cut it.

Quickly practicing and reviewing words, phrases, and sentence structures every day will help your new language stick in your long-term memory. And don’t think you need to spend hours reviewing—a quick five to ten minutes is all you need to start seeing language gains.

Not speaking and listening in the foreign language

We get it. Learning a new language can be really uncomfortable at first. You might feel silly or awkward saying new words, but the only way to get better is through practice.

Whenever you can, pronounce the words and phrases you are learning out loud to build your confidence. Listening also goes a long way to help boost your foreign language confidence, whether it’s course material, a podcast, or a foreign language film.

Just be sure to listen intentionally and try to identify words and phrases you’re learning throughout your course.
