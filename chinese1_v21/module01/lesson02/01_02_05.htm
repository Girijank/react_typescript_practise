我的州 Wǒde zhōu My State

Information overload? Let’s take a short break, and stroll through states of the U.S.

Saying names of U.S. states in Chinese is straightforward. They are translations by sound, followed by the word 州 zhōu, meaning “state.” For example, Florida would be 佛罗里达州 Fóluólǐdá zhōu (州 zhōu -- state). Sounds similar, right? Please don’t worry about memorizing these Chinese translations; let’s just concentrate on their pronunciations for now. This is also the same case for the names of most cities in English. The name of the city in Chinese pronunciation is followed by the word 市 shì meaning “city;” for example, 坦帕市 Tǎnpà shì means “Tampa city.”
