Notes

Not all numbers are created equal. In the Chinese culture, several numbers are considered lucky, such as 八 bā (eight)—prosper, 六 liù (six)—smooth, 九 jiǔ (9)—long lasting, and 十 shí (ten)—complete. However, number 四 sì (four) is considered “unlucky” because it shares the same pronunciation (different tones, though) with the word 死 sǐ (death).

For number “zero,” there are two ways of writing it in characters—零 and 〇. Usually 零 is used when involving measurements (e.g., zero gallons, 205 meters), and 〇 is used for counting (e.g., sample #002, year 2000).
