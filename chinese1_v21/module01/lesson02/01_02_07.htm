Constructing Two-digit Numbers

By now you might be wondering how to say two-digit numbers in Chinese. It’s simple. Just grab two digits, put a 十 shí between then, voilà! You are done.

For example, 31 would be 三十一 sānshíyī; 64 would be 六十四 liùshísì; and 99 would be 九十九 jiǔshíjiǔ.

There are two exceptions:

  1. If the first digit is “one,” you don’t have to say it. For example, use 十二 shièr for “12;” 十六 shíliù for “16.”
  2. If the second digit is “zero,” you don’t have to say it. For example, use 二十 èrshí for “20;” and 五十 wǔshí for “50.”
