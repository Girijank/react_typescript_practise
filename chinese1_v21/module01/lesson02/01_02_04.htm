把根留住 Bǎ gēn liúzhù Rooted In Place

Have you heard the song《把根留住 Bǎ gēn liúzhù》? It’s a classic by Chinese pop singer 童安格 Tóng Āngé (Angus Tung). He was a popular singer in Taiwan from the mid-1980s to mid-1990s. The meaning of the song’s title is to “hold onto our roots.” That’s what this lesson will focus on—to discuss where you are from and where you live.

Note: In this lesson, we will focus on the states or cities where we live or are from. In the future, we’ll learn how to say specific addresses with street names and zip codes, as well as names of countries or regions.


Let’s go back to your roots and learn some vocabulary to talk about where you are from.

Notes

  • Verbs in Chinese don’t need to be changed by context. In English, “to be” can be in different forms such as “is,” “am,” “are,” “was,” and “were,” while in Chinese, 是 shì stays the same form wherever or whenever it’s used.
  • 他/她/它 tā – these three characters share the same pronunciation, tā, with different characters (他- he/她-she/它 -it).
