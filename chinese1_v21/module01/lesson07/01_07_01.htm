演讲时间 Yǎnjiǎng shíjiān Presentational Speaking

It’s time to showcase your verbal skills on what you’ve learned. For this presentation, you will record an audio or a video, speaking in complete sentences in Chinese. Your audio or video should include the grammar and vocabulary from the lessons.

Using complete sentences in Chinese, talk about one of your friends, a teacher, or a family doctor. Include the image of the person you are describing. If you use video, you can show a picture of the person in the video or even the actual person! Take the following steps to complete your description.

Note: You can pick a fictitious person for the description. This person doesn’t have to be from real life.

    2. Student Sample

    我叫 Christina 。我的眼睛是绿色的。我的头发是红色的。我比较开朗。我一九九九年出生，我今年二十一岁。

    Wǒ jiào Christina. Wǒ de yǎnjīng shì lǜsè de. Wǒ de tóufà shì hóngsè de. Wǒ bǐjiào kāilǎng. Wǒ yī jiǔ jiǔ jiǔnián chūshēng, wǒ jīnnián èrshíyī suì.

    I am Christina. My eyes are green. My hair is red. I am somewhat outgoing. I was born in 1999, and I’m 21 years old this year.

  2. Know the requirements of your presentation.

    Note: The sample English sentences in parentheses are just a guide. You need to come up with your own original sentences in Chinese.

    • Introduce yourself. (e.g., My name is Angie Liu.)
    • Introduce your friend/teacher/doctor. (e.g., My friend is Sandy.)
    • Describe your friend/teacher/doctor’s eye color. (e.g., Her eyes are brown.)
    • Describe your friend/teacher/doctor’s hair color. (e.g. Her hair is red.)
    • Describe your friend/teacher/doctor’s personality, using an adverb of degree followed by an adjective. (e.g., She is a little shy.)
    • State what year your friend/teacher/doctor was born, and how old he or she is. (e.g., She was born in 2005, and she is 15 years old this year.)
  3. Finalize your oral presentation
    • Give your presentation in Chinese, using appropriate vocabulary and grammar.
    • Start your presentation by stating your first and last name in English. (e.g., My name is Angie Liu.)
    • Speak clearly and loudly enough for the microphone to record all your words.
    • Speak at an appropriate rate. (Don’t speak too quickly or too slowly, and do not take extended pauses.)
    • Use correct tones and pronunciations throughout the speech.
    • Practice a couple of times first in a mirror or in front of a family member or friend.

Congratulations! You are on your way to becoming a fluent Chinese speaker!
