吃了吗？Chīle ma? Have You Eaten?

When meeting up with a close friend, you will probably greet each other casually. Instead of a formal “hello,” you might just want to say, “what’s up?” In Chinese, there’s a phrase just like that – 吃了吗? Chīle ma? (Have you eaten?) From this phrase, you can see how Chinese people value eating meals in their everyday lives.

Besides 吃了吗? Chīle ma?, below are some informal greetings you may hear in Chinese. Remember, such greetings are just a quick “hi” or “bye” and don’t require a detailed response. In a cultural sense, they are similar to some phrases in English, regardless of the phrases’ actual meanings.


Chinese Pinyin Similar English Phrase
吃了吗? Chīle ma? What’s up?
去哪呢? Qù nǎ ne? How’s it going?
最近忙吗? Zuìjìn máng ma? How’s it going?
最近怎么样? Zuìjìn zěnmeyàng? How’s it going?
上班呢? Shàngbān ne? What’s up?

Notes

It’s also important to know that older people are highly respected in the Chinese culture. When addressing the elderly or someone you hold in high respect, instead of using a regular 你 nǐ (you), a courteous 您 nín (you) should be used. And in the case of greeting, as you may have guessed, instead of saying 你好 Nǐhǎo (Hello), you can say 您好 Nǐnhǎo (Hello with respect).
