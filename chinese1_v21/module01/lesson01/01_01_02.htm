发音威力 Fāyīn wēilì Pronunciation Power

Before we get down to business, it’s important to know what you are about to learn. Here’s a quick introduction of the Chinese language and its elements that are essential to your learning.

©iStock

Chinese Characters

Chinese characters are square “blocks” that make up Chinese words, sentences, and articles.

When you flip open a page of a Chinese book or a Chinese newspaper, you will see square “blocks” all over the place. Those are called 汉字 hànzì (Chinese characters). Chinese people read Chinese characters as a part of their lives. It comes naturally to them just like drinking a cup of coffee or brushing your teeth every day.

©iStock

Two Systems of Chinese Characters

There are two systems of Chinese characters in this world—Traditional Chinese characters and Simplified Chinese characters. In this course, we’ll learn Simplified Chinese characters. (Always start with a “simplified” choice, right?)

As for spoken Chinese, we will use Mandarin Chinese in this course, which is widely spoken in regions such as China, Singapore, and Taiwan. Many Chinese American people speak Cantonese, which is a Chinese dialect.


©iStock

Chinese Pinyin

Chinese pinyin is a phonetic tool to help you pronounce Chinese characters.

Now you might be wondering, how do we read these “blocks” on paper? Wondering how to pronounce them? Then you need to know something called 拼音 pīnyīn (Chinese pinyin), which is a phonetic tool we will use in this course to help you learn how to pronounce the Chinese characters.

©Shutterstock

Initials and Finals

A typical pinyin consists of an initial and a final. There are 23 initials and 31 finals. We’ll just need to mix and match them to make up the sounds that we need.


©Shutterstock

Tones

Once an initial and final are combined, we still need to give it a tone to complete its pronunciation. There are four tones in Chinese: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th, plus a neutral tone. The same sound with different tones will mean different things. For example, the sound “ma” with 1st tone means “mom,” while “ma” with 3rd tone means “horse.”

Don’t let the numbers scare you (23, 31, 4, and their combinations). We’ll get to them one by one throughout this course with regular tone and pronunciation practices. You won’t be overwhelmed, and you won’t be bored either.
