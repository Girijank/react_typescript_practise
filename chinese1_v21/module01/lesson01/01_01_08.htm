拆开看 Chāikāi kàn Break It Down

Now that you have learned some sentences or phrases in Chinese, let’s break them down to see how a Chinese sentence/phrase is constructed. You can also look at the sentence’s pinyin for hints.

你叫什么名字? Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?

你 nǐ—you

叫 jiào—to be called

什么 shénme—what

名字 míngzi—name

我姓… Wǒ xìng…

我 Wǒ—I/me

姓 xìng—last name

很高兴认识你。Hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ.

很 hěn—very

高兴 gāoxìng—glad/happy

认识 rènshi—to get to know

你 Nǐ—you

Character Magic

In Chinese, the same pronunciation can correspond to different characters. The same Chinese characters can have different pronunciations. Just like in English, you will need to practice to remember the pronunciation that corresponds to different characters. Below are some examples:

Same sound, different characters and meanings Similar examples in English
jiào 叫 (to be called), 觉 (to sleep) pear; pair
míng 名 (name), 明 (bright) eight; ate
zǎo 早 (morning), 枣 (date) hear; here
Same character, different sounds and meanings Similar examples in English
好 hǎo (good); 好 hào (to indulge in something) lead; lead
和 hé (and) ; 和 huò (to mix) read; read
少 shǎo(few), 少 shào (young) live; live
