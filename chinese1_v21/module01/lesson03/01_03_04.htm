把我安顿好 Bǎ wǒ āndùn hǎo Place Me Please

So far, we’ve learned some question (interrogative) words in Chinese such as 什么 shénme (what), 多少 duōshǎo (how many), and 哪里 nǎlǐ (where). Did you notice where the question words are placed in a Chinese sentence?

In English, words are arranged differently for different sentence patterns such as statements and questions. For example, “She is Mary,” and “Who is she?”

In Chinese, sentences stay in the same order whether they are a question or not. So, to answer a Chinese question with a question word, we simply follow these steps. Let’s use the example question: 你住在哪里？Nǐ zhùzài nǎlǐ? (Where do you live?)


Find the interrogative word in the original question.

Example:

Find interrogative word: 哪里 nǎlǐ (where?)

你住在哪里？Nǐ zhùzài nǎlǐ? (Where do you live?)

Replace the interrogative word with the information requested.

Example:

Replace 哪里 nǎlǐ (where) with 佛罗里达州 Fóluólǐdá zhōu (Florida).

你住在 佛罗里达州？Nǐ zhùzài Fóluólǐdá zhōu (Do you live in Florida)?

Change the pronoun if needed (e.g., from “you” to “I”).

Example:

Change the pronoun 你 Nǐ (you) to “我 Wǒ (I)”.

我住在佛罗里达州？Wǒ zhùzài Fóluólǐdá zhōu (Do I live in Florida)?

Change the punctuation from “?” to “.”

Example:

Change the punctuation “?” to “.”

我住在佛罗里达州。Wǒ zhùzài Fóluólǐdá zhōu. (I live in Florida.)
