什么程度 Shénme chéngdù What’s The Degree

We’ve learned several adverbs of degree so far: 有点 yǒudiǎn (a little); 比较 bǐjiào (somewhat); 很 hěn (very). There are many more adverbs of degree in Chinese, but we will just learn these three for now.

In a descriptive sentence in Chinese, an adverb of degree is usually placed before the adjective, and the verb such as 是 shì (to be) is usually omitted. (Sometimes it can still be added before the adverb of degree for emphasis.) The descriptive sentence structure “noun + adverb of degree + adjective” is common in Chinese .

Let’s look at some examples of such structure:

她有点害羞.

Tā yǒudiǎn hàixiū.

She is a little shy.

她比较害羞.

Tā bǐjiào hàixiū.

She is somewhat shy.

她很害羞.

Tā hěn hàixiū.

She is very shy.

你有点高.

Nǐ yǒudiǎn gāo.

You are a little tall.

你比较高

Nǐ bǐjiào gāo.

You are somewhat tall.

你很高.

Nǐ hěn gāo.

You are very tall.
