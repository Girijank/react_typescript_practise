声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson we will learn initial “sh” and finals “i” and “e.”

Initial “sh”

This initial sounds similar to “sh” as in “sheep” in English, but you need to curl up the tip of your tongue toward your palate when pronouncing it. It’s used in words such as “是 shì (to be)” and “什么 shénme (what).”

Final “i”

This final sounds like “ee” as in “eel” in English. It’s used on words such as “你 nǐ (you) “ and “哪里 nǎlǐ (where).”

Final “e”

This final sounds like “uh” in “duh” in English. It’s used in words such as “你呢 Nǐ ne (what about you)” and “的 de (of).”
