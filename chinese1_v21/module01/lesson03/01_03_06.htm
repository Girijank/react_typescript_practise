所有格魅力 Suǒyǒugé mèilì Possessive Charms

By now we’ve encountered the possessive particle 的 de several times. It is not only used as a possessive particle but also used for other indications. Now let’s take a close look at how it works.

The particle 的 de has two functions:

  1. When it’s placed after a noun or pronoun, it indicates possession. For
    • 我的 wǒ de (my/mine)
    • 他的 tāde (his)
    • 朋友的 péngyǒu de (friend's)
  2. In Chinese, an adjective can’t be placed directly before a noun. A 的 de is needed between them: adjective + 的 de + noun. For example, if you want to say “shy boy,” it’s incorrect to say “害羞 (shy) 男孩 (boy) hàixiū nánhái;” instead it should be“害羞的男孩 hàixiū de nánhái.” Below are some more
    • 开朗的女孩 kāilǎng de nǚhái (outgoing girl)
    • 绿色的眼睛 lǜsè de yǎnjīng (green eyes)

Note: In a descriptive sentence, 的 de is also needed after the adjective, even if there’s no noun after it. For example: 我的眼睛是褐色的. Wǒde yǎnjīng shì hèsè de. (My eyes are brown.)
