Before we start writing, let’s first get familiar with Chinese characters, including their structures and writing rules.

There are over 90,000 Chinese characters, about 7,000 of which are for everyday use. Chinese characters are square-shaped. Each character consists of components called radicals, then each radical consists of even smaller components called strokes.

Structures

A Chinese character, based on the radical(s), has one of the following common :

Structure Example
Left and right 你
Independent 我
Top and bottom 高
Inside and outside 国(*)

*: The character 国 guó means “nation.” You will learn this character later in this course. In this lesson, you will not be assessed on it.

Writing Rules

When writing a character, we follow the rules below. Each writing rule is demonstrated with one or two characters shown beside it. In this course, stroke sequences for characters you learn will also be provided to demonstrate these writing rules.

Note: You will not be assessed by the new characters 国 guó (nation) and 水 shuǐ (water) used in the demonstrations.

Radicals in a Chinese character are written in red. The Chinese characters are written in order of “top to bottom” and “left to right.”
