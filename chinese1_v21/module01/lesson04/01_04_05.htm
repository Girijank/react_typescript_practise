漫画时间 Mànhuà shíjiān Comic Time

Now let’s see some action. Select one of the scenarios below, and use the template provided to create your comic strip.

Scenario A

Tracy Liu and Jack Smith are eighth-graders at the local middle school. They are attending their first Chinese club meeting of the school year on a Monday afternoon. This is the first time they meet. The meeting site is the school cafeteria.

Use Template A to create a Chinese comic strip based on this scenario.

You can use the checklist below to help with your work for

What to include in your comic strip Hint

Image one:

Place an image of a boy and a girl waving at each other to say hello.

Next to image one:

Dialogue row #1: Fill in the two blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the students’ greetings to each other.

Think about:

What character is placed at the end of most greetings in Chinese with the meaning “good”?

Image two:

Place an image of the boy and girl sitting down and facing each other to have a conversation.

Next to image two:

Dialogue row #2: Fill in the two blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing the girl’s name.

Think about:

What structure is used when introducing one’s full name or first name?

Next to image two:

Dialogue row #3: Fill in the four blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing the girl’s last name.

Think about:

What structure is used to ask for someone’s last name, and what verb is used in that structure?

Image three:

Place an image of the boy and girl holding their phones in front of them to type something.

Next to image three:

Dialogue row #4: Fill in the four blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing the girl’s phone number.

Think about:

  • What possessive adjective particle is used to indicate possession?
  • How do you say “phone number” in Chinese (in a shortened way)?
  • What verb is used in the structure to introduce one’s phone number?

Image four:

Place an image of the boy and girl standing back up and waving at each other as they leave.

Next to image three:

Dialogue row #5: You don’t need to fill in any blanks for this row. Just make sure to understand the phrase used here.

Think about:

For this phrase used here upon the students’ departure, what do each of the two characters mean?

Scenario B

Kylie Garcia and Jason Li are members of an international dance group. Kylie lives in Florida, and Jason lives in Shanghai, China. They are meeting online for the first time to prepare for an upcoming performance in Hong Kong.

Use Template B to create a Chinese comic strip based on this scenario.

You can use the checklist below to help with your work for

What to include in your comic strip Hint

Image one:

Place an image of a boy and a girl waving to each other to say hello. The boy is taller than the girl, and the girl has black hair.

Next to image one:

Dialogue row #1: Fill in the two blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the students’ greetings to each other.

Think about:

What character is placed at the end of most greetings in Chinese with the meaning “good”?

Image two:

Place an image of the two having a conversation. The boy is using a hand gesture to indicate his height, and the girl is pointing to her hair.

Next to image two:

Dialogue row #2: Fill in the four blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing the boy’s height.

Think about:

  • What interrogative word is used when asking about someone’s appearance?
  • What adjective is used to describe someone who’s tall?
  • In a “what about” question, what character should be placed before the modal particle “呢 ne”?

Next to image two:

Dialogue row #3: Fill in the four blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing the girl’s appearance.

Think about:

  • What one-character adverb of degree can be placed before an adjective?
  • What verb and what possessive adjective particle is used when describing one’s hair color?

Image three:

Place an image of the boy and girl pointing to the map of Florida and having a conversation.

Next to image three:

Dialogue row #4: Fill in the two blanks (one character in each blank) to complete the dialogue of the two discussing where the boy lives.

Think about:

What structure is used to introduce where one lives?

Image four:

Place an image of the boy and girl standing back up and waving to each other to leave.

Next to image three:

Dialogue row #5: You don’t need to fill in any blanks for this row. Just make sure to understand the phrase used here.

Think about:

For this phrase used here upon the students’ departure, what do each of the two characters mean?

After you have chosen your scenario choose an image from clipart, a drawing, or a picture for each box based on the content of the conversation. Fill out each blank with an appropriate character to complete the conversation based on the demonstrations you learned throughout the lesson. You can choose to print out the template and handwrite the characters, or type the characters directly in the document, then save the document as your work.

Note: Be sure to type or handwrite the characters in the worksheet, instead of copying-and-pasting them from the lesson. You are encouraged to handwrite the characters when possible. It is the best way to understand the characters.
