Welcome to New York! Let me introduce to you my life in New York.

我叫卢凯伦，我今年十五岁。我是女孩。我比较高，不过我有点害羞。我的头发是黑色的，我的眼睛是褐色的。我从台湾来。我住在纽约市皇后区

My name is Karen Lu. I am 15 years old this year. I am a girl. I am somewhat tall. I am a little shy. My hair is black, and my eyes are brown. I am from Taiwan. I live in Queens, New York.

We live in an apartment building. Our neighbors are the Wangs. They are from Hong Kong.
王教授很开朗。王太太的头发是黑色的，她很害羞。她的电话号码是三零八五一五六。
Professor Wang is very outgoing. Mrs. Wang’s hair is black, and she’s also very outgoing. Her phone number is 308-5156.

Chinatown
There are three main areas with a high Chinese population in New York. In Manhattan Chinatown, there are many Chinese residents who immigrated to the U.S. a long time ago. In Flushing, Elmhurst, and Corona in Queens, there are many Chinese who immigrated from Hong Kong and Taiwan. Also, in the Eighth Avenue area in Brooklyn, there are many new immigrants from the Guangdong and Fujian provinces in China.

I Need Some Dim Sum
Flushing downtown is called by many “The Chinese Manhattan.” There are many Chinese stores and restaurants there. My family and I often go there to shop and eat. There are many Chinese restaurants in New York that serve Hong Kong dim sum, Sichuan cuisine, Cantonese cuisine, and Taiwanese cuisine. My favorite food is the Taipei beef noodles.

Happy New Year!
Each year Chinese people in New York celebrate traditional festivals such as Chinese New Year, the Moon Festival, the Dragonboat festival, and many more. During the Chinese New Year, there are many traditions in Chinatown such as setting off firecrackers, the lion dance, the dragon dance, and colorful parades. There are red lanterns hanging everywhere, and my family and I never miss it. It’s so much fun!
