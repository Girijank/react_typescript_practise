岁月如歌 Suìyuè rúgē The Years Are Like Songs

Have you heard of a song called 岁月 Suìyuè (The Years), sung in 2019 by two famous female Chinese singers 王菲 Wáng Féi (Faye Wong) and 那英 Nà Yīng (Na Ying)? The song is about cherishing the passing years and fond memories. Time is always precious.

Now you are about to conclude this lesson by learning a few more things used to ask and tell one’s age.

The Use of 几岁 jǐsuì (how old)

You’ve just learned to use 多大 duōdà (how old) to inquire about someone’s age. When you are asking a child or a teenager his or her age, you usually use 几岁 jǐsuì (how old) instead. “几 jǐ” means “how many” when the expected answer is a small number (0 to teens).

Let’s look at some examples:

  • — 你多大？Nǐ duōdà? (How old are you?) — 我二十九岁。Wǒ èrshíjiǔ suì. (I am 29 years old.)
  • — 她今年几岁？Tā jīnnián jǐsuì? (How old is she this year?) — 她今年五岁。Tā jīnnián wǔsuì. (She’s five this year.)
