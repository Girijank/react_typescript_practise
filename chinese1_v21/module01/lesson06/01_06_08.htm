你有多年轻？Nǐyǒu duō niánqīng? How Young Are You?

Age is definitely more than a number; it is very important for many aspects of life. Let’s say you just met a new friend, and you may be wondering if he or she is younger or older than you. Maybe you want to start a soccer league for people in a certain age group. Or what about seeing if you qualify for the summer internship program in China for 15 to 18-year old high school students? There are many reasons why you may need to talk about age, and so in this next section, we will go over everything you need to know.

Notes

  • When saying a year in Chinese, you simply say the four digits, one by one, followed by the word 年 nián (year), and that’s it. For example:
    • 一九九二年 yī jiǔ jiǔ èr nián(Year 1992)
    • 二零二零年 èr líng èr líng nián (Year 2020)
  • In the sentence 你今年多大？Nǐ jīnnián duōdà? (How old are you this year?),今年jīnnián (this year) can sometimes be omitted.
  • In the sentence 我今年...岁。Wǒ jīnnián ... suì. (I am … years old this year), both 今年jīnnián(this year) and 岁suì (years old) can sometimes be omitted.
