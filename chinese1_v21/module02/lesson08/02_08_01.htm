单元考试 Dānyuán kǎoshì Module Exam

You made it to the end of the module. Hooray! In this module, you learned about different food and drink items, how to express your preferences to them to various degrees, how to describe them, how to order them in a restaurant, how to pay for your meal, and much more. Now, it’s time to show off your reading, listening, and cultural knowledge. Before you dive into the exam, take a minute to study and review the topics below. Remember to use your note guides from each lesson to help you study.

  • Know pinyin initials “c,” “ch,” “x,” and “q.”
  • Know pinyin finals “ai,” “ao,” “eng,” “ie,” “uo,” and “an.”
  • Express preferences of food and drink items.
  • Ask others about their preferences of food or drink items.
  • Use basic Chinese sentence structure: subject + verb + object.
  • Say and describe different types of meat, vegetables, and fruits.
  • Know about diets in the Chinese culture.
  • Use verbs “to eat” and “to drink” in sentences.
  • Explain the Chinese tea culture.
  • Express “the most” and “not.”
  • Express degrees of likes, using adverbs of degree and adverbs.
  • Ask others about likes and dislikes.
  • Explain Chinese traditional desserts.
  • Say and describe everyday food and drink items in the U.S.
  • Identify and say utensils.
  • Understand table manners and etiquette in China.
  • Know how to read a menu in Chinese.
  • Order food in restaurants using different phrases, with specification of quantity and unit.
  • Identify and say prices in Chinese.
  • Use 两 liǎng for the word “two” when ordering food.
  • Use courtesy words at restaurants.
  • Know a culture blip—dim sum and food culture in Hong Kong.
  • Know borrowed food words in Chinese and Chinese food items with Western concepts.
  • Know how to ask for the price and pay for meals in a restaurant in Chinese.
  • Pay for meals in the Chinese culture.
