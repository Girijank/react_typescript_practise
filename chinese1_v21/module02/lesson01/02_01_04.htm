食得我心 Shídéwǒxīn Food To My Heart

Now that you are learning different types of foods, it’s time to know how to express your own preferences and ask someone what they like.


Sentence Elements

A typical sentence in any language usually consists of different elements. Below are some basic elements, and we will use the sentence <<Joe eats the noodles>> as an example.

  • Subject – the person or thing that performs the action. Joe eats the noodles. “Joe” is the subject.

    Verb – the word that describes the subject’s action. Joe eats the noodles. “Eats” is the verb because it is what Joe is doing.

    Object – the person or thing that is affected by the subject’s action. Joe eats the noodles. “The noodles” is the object because it is what Joe is eating.

Sentence Structure

A common basic sentence structure in Chinese is:

Subject + verb + object

For example:

我叫丽丽。 Wǒ jiào Lìli. I to be called Lili.

Express Preferences

To express your preferences, simply follow the basic Chinese sentence structure: subject + verb + object. In this case, the verb is 喜欢 xǐhuān (to like). You can use this structure to express preferences on any objects besides foods.

For example:

  • 我喜欢米饭。 Wǒ xǐhuān mǐfàn. I like rice.
  • 丽丽喜欢面条。 Lìli xǐhuān miàntiáo. Lili likes noodles.

Note:

You can add another verb after 喜欢 xǐhuān (to like) to specify your preference. For example: 我喜欢吃米饭。 Wǒ xǐhuān chī mǐfàn. I like to eat rice.

Ask About Preferences

To ask someone of their preferences between two items, we use the sentence structure:

subject + 喜欢 xǐhuān (to like) + object 1 + 还是 háishì (or) + object 2?

For example:

  • 你喜欢米饭还是面条？ Nǐ xǐhuān mǐfàn háishì miàntiáo? Do you like rice or noodles?
  • 他喜欢奶酪还是米饭？ Tā xǐhuān nǎilào háishì mǐfàn? Does he like cheese or rice?
