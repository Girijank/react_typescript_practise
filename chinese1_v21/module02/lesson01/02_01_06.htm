饮食文化 Yǐnshí wénhuà Culture In Diet

Have you ever wondered how diets and meals are different in each culture? Let’s compare the diets and typical meals of two teens from different cultural backgrounds. Let’s meet Lili and Jack. Lili lives in Beijing, China, and Jack lives in Orlando, Florida. They met a junior chef camp last summer and are sharing a little bit about their cultures. Let’s see what they have to say about their own experiences with the diets.

Typical Meal

  • My family always eats together and shares dishes on a round table. But we each have our own bowl of rice or noodles.
  • For a regular family dinner, my mom always cooks four dishes and one soup (四菜一汤 sì cài yī tang).
  • During dinners we often chat, play number guessing games, or even sing karaoke. It’s more fun when we have guests over!
  • In my house, for dinner we often have food on our own plates. When my family eats out, we also each have our own order of food.

Utensils and Drinks

  • My family regularly uses chopsticks as the main utensil during meals. My grandma would bring spoons when there’s soup served for that meal.
  • My mom always cooks the dishes in bite-sized portions, so it’s easy for us to eat.
  • My family loves hot tea as a beverage before or after meals.
  • Sometimes we have soft drinks, but my mom likes to serve them at room temperature without ice.
  • My family uses forks and knives for most meals.
  • During meals, we usually have dishes that we cut with a knife and fork to eat.
  • When we eat at restaurants, I like to order cold drinks with ice. And I often see hot tea or coffee on the menu.

Cooked or Raw

  • My mom serves cooked dishes most of the time, especially meats and vegetables. Occasionally, especially during summer, she makes a raw cucumber dish with seasoning.
  • My grandma likes to cook and serve dishes with meat. She always leaves the bones in the meat because she says it gives more flavor to the dish.
  • My mom usually serves meat from rare to well-done, depending on my and my brother’s preferences.
  • My mom usually removes the bones from the meat before they are cooked and served, but we do have some dishes where the bone is left in, like chicken wings… my favorite!

Main Ingredients

  • For my family meals, the main course is usually rice, noodles, or buns. My mom makes the most delicious buns!
  • My grandma likes to use spices such as star anise, soy sauce, and peppercorn when cooking. I don’t see her or my mom use cinnamon that often.
  • After meals, my mom often serves fruits or hot tea. Sometimes we have desserts such as red bean cakes, but they are not very sweet.
  • We normally don’t have appetizers.
  • Meat is often served as the main course in most of the meals, especially dinners, in my family.
  • My mom normally uses salt, vinegar, or pepper for seasoning when cooking. My grandma loves to make dishes or desserts with cinnamon. The cinnamon rolls she makes are world-class!
