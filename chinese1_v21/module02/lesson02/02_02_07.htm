具体描述 Jùtǐ miáoshù All the Specifics

Now with more vocabulary, you will be able to express yourself more specifically about the food or drink you have. You can say whether it’s too hot or too cold, and if you like it or don’t like it. You can now say it all!


Adverbs of Degree

Remember the adverbs of degree we’ve learned previously that can be added before an adjective: 有点 yǒudiǎn (a little); 比较 bǐjiào (somewhat); 很 hěn (very). Now we have one more: 太…了 tài…le (too).

For example:

我很高。Wǒ hěn gāo. (I am very tall.)

水太热了。Suǐ tài rè le. (The water is too hot.)

茶有点冷。Chá yǒudiǎn lěng. (The tea is a little cold.)

Express degree of likes

The adverbs of degree can also be placed before the verb 喜欢 xǐhuān (to like) to express preferences.

For example:

  • 我有点喜欢牛奶。Wǒ yǒudiǎn xǐhuān niúnǎi. I like milk a little.
  • 丽丽太喜欢茶了。Lìli tài xǐhuān chá le. Lili likes noodles too much.
Express “most” and “not”

You can also place the superlative 最 zuì (the most) and negation 不 bù (not) before the verb 喜欢 xǐhuān (to like).

For example:

  • 我最喜欢牛奶。Wǒ zuì xǐhuān niúnǎi. I like milk the most.
  • 丽丽不喜欢茶。Lìli bù xǐhuān chá. Lili does not like tea.
Ask about likes and dislikes

Using the word 什么 shénme (what) we learned before, you can also ask about other’s likes and dislikes.

For example:

  • 你最喜欢什么？Nǐ zuì xǐhuān shénme? What do you like the most?
  • 我最喜欢牛奶。Wǒ zuì xǐhuān niúnǎi. I like milk the most.
