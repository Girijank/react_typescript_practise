声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson, we will learn the initial “ch” and finals “ao” and “eng.”

Initial “ch”

This initial sounds like “ch” in English as in “chill.” When pronouncing, you will need to pull your tongue a little into the back of your mouth. It’s used in words such as “吃 chī (to eat)” and “橙子 chéngzi (orange).”

Final “ao”

This final sounds like “ow” in English as in “how” or “cow.” It’s used in words such as “你好 nǐhǎo (hello)” and “奶酪 nǎilào (cheese).”

Final “eng”

This final is a back-nasal final, which means it is a sound with the air coming through your nose. So, final “eng” sounds like “en” in English as in “taken” with the nasal ending. It’s used in words such as “朋友 péngyǒu (friend)” and “医生 yīshēng (doctor).”
