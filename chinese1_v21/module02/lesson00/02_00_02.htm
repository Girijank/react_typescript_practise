International Chinese — A Quick Guide to Hong Kong and Macau

As introduced before, the Chinese language is widely spoken in many regions in the world. You’ve previously learned about the People’s Republic of China. Let’s continue to get to know two more regions—Hong Kong and Macau.

©iStock

Hong Kong

Hong Kong (香港 Xiānggǎng) is a special administrative region of the People’s Republic of China. It’s a metropolitan area and pivotal economic center in Asia. The culture in Hong Kong is a mixture of traditional Chinese culture and Western culture, and it is especially under great influence of British culture due to its history of being a British colony from 1841 to 1997.

Hong Kong has a huge and prosperous entertainment business, with many world-renowned stars and celebrities.

©iStock

Macau

Macau (澳门 Àomén) is another special administrative region of the People’s Republic of China. It was a Portuguese colony from 1557 to 1999. It is one-thirty-sixth the size of Hong Kong and has one-eleventh the population of Hong Kong. It is also a melting pot of Eastern and Western cultures, and it’s known as one of the world’s largest gambling centers.


©iStock

Use of the Chinese Language

In both regions, Cantonese, a dialect of the Chinese language, is spoken, and traditional Chinese characters are used in writing. In recent years, especially after the Chinese government’s takeover of both regions in the 1990’s, Mandarin, with simplified Chinese characters and pinyin as the sound annotation tool, is uniformly taught in schools in both regions.
