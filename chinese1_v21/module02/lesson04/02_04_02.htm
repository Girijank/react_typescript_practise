Shown above is a real-life restaurant menu, written in Chinese characters. As you can see, food or drink items are listed in different categories. The names of food or drinks are laid out neatly and clearly, with units and prices shown after each item. In this lesson, you will create a coffee shop or diner menu that is similar to this. Use the knowledge you’ve learned in this module to enrich the content and allow the creative and artistic side of you to shine through its creation.

Before we start this exciting expedition, let’s quickly review the writing rules of Chinese characters.

Writing Rules

Review the following writing rules, with demonstrations of these rules, using characters you’ve learned in this module.

Note: You will not be assessed by the new character 国 guó(nation) used in the demonstrations.

Radicals in a Chinese character are written in order of “top to bottom” and “left to right.”

菜 cài —top to bottom


奶 nǎi —left to right

Write horizontal strokes, then vertical ones.

十 shí (ten)

Write left-falling strokes, then right-falling ones.

个 gè

For “inside and outside” characters, the outside frame is closed last.

国 guó

For a symmetrical character, draw the middle first, then left, then right.

水 shuǐ
