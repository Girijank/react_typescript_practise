菜单时间 Càidān shíjiān Menu Time

Now let’s see some action. Based on the scenario below, use the template provided to create a coffee shop or diner menu in Chinese.

Scenario A

Tracy Liu and Jack Smith are both 8th graders at the local middle school. They are both members of the school’s Chinese club, and they are close friends. Tracy’s mom is opening a coffee shop in her neighborhood, and she asked Tracy to design a menu in Chinese for her. Tracy has started the menu but needs Jack’s help to add some more items and improve the menu design. This Monday after their Chinese Club meeting, Tracy and Jack get together to finalize the content and design of this menu.

Use Template A to create a coffee shop menu in Chinese based on this scenario.

You can use the checklist below to help with your work for

What to include in your Coffee Shop menu Hint

Top of the Menu

Add a title for this menu.

Think about:

  • Review the demonstration list to see how you can form the word “menu” in Chinese – 菜单.

“Drinks” 1st Column

Add three more drinks to the list by filling in the blanks.

Think about:

  • What characters on the demonstration list can you use to form words for three types of drinks?
  • Remember, some drinks can be single-character words.

“Drinks” 2nd Column

Fill in the measure word(s) used for ordering drinks.

Think about:

  • There are four characters on the demonstration list that you can use for measure words. See if you can identify them. You may find the list of measure words below helpful:
    • plate of
    • bowl of
    • cup of
    • count of
  • Find the characters for them, and remember which one(s) can be used for drinks.

“Drinks” 3rd Column

Add prices for all the items as needed.

Think about:

  • Add prices in the numerical format, based on your own experience. Food/drink prices may vary. You will not be assessed on what prices you fill in.

“Snacks” 1st Column

Add two more snacks to the list by filling in the blanks.

Think about:

  • What characters on the demonstration list can you use to form words for some more snacks? You may find the list of snacks below helpful:
    • sandwich
    • pie
    • cookie
    • bread
    • cheese
    • soup
  • Remember, some snacks can be single-character words.

“Snacks” 2nd Column

Fill in the measure word(s) used for ordering snacks.

Think about:

  • There are four characters on the demonstration list that are measure words. Which one(s) can be used for snacks? Think about how snacks are served at coffee shops.
  • You may find the list of measure words below helpful:
    • plate of
    • bowl of
    • cup of
    • count of

“Snacks” 3rd Column

Add prices for all the items as needed.

Think about:

  • Add prices in a numerical format, based on your own experience. Food/drink prices may vary. You will not be assessed by what prices you fill in.

“Fruits”

Fill in another fruit item, and then add the measure words for ordering fruits.

Think about:

  • Different measure words for different ways fruits are served. The answer may vary. You may revise the prices to best suit the measure word(s) selected.
  • You may find the list of measure words below helpful:
    • plate of
    • bowl of
    • cup of
    • count of

Bottom of the Menu

Express courtesy to your customers.

Think about:

  • What courtesy words can you form, using the character(s) from the demonstration list?
  • Consider the common courtesy phrases people use in coffee shops.
  • You may find the list of courtesy words below helpful:
    • please give me
    • thanks
    • you are welcome
    • excuse me

Complete the contents of the menu, using the appropriate characters from the demonstrations on the “WRITING CHINESE CHARACTERS” page. You can choose to print out the template and handwrite the characters, or type the characters directly in the document, and then save the document as your work.

Note:

  • Be sure to type or handwrite the characters in the worksheet, instead of copying and pasting them from the lesson. You are encouraged to handwrite the characters when possible. It is the best way to understand the characters.
  • You can also consider using the characters you’ve learned and used in the previous workshop for this activity, wherever you see applicable.

You may choose an image from clipart, a picture, or a drawing of your own as the background for the menu. You can also change the design of the menu table to beautify it.
