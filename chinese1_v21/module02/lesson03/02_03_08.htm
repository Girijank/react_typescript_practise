用起来 Yòng qǐlái Apply It!

It’s now time to put your learning into practical use.

Cherry is at a Chinese restaurant. She’s hungry. She looks at the menu and places her order with the waiter.

View the menu, and listen to how Cherry orders. It’s okay if you miss some of the dishes’ names. Chinese cuisine has many variations, and we’ll explore them more in the future. For now, just pay attention to the sentence structure Cherry uses to order, how she specifies quantities, and how she expresses courtesy and appreciation.

Text: 你好！我想要一盘扬州炒饭和一碗鱼头豆腐汤。也请给我一杯热茶。谢谢！

Nǐnǎo! Wǒ xiǎng yào yī pán yángzhōu chǎofàn hé yī wǎn yútóu dòufǔ tāng. Yě qǐnggěiwǒ yī bēi rèchá. Xièxie!

Hello! I want a plate of Yangzhou fried rice and a bowl of fish head tofu soup. Also, please give me a cup of hot tea.

You have now mastered the structures and vocabulary to order food in Chinese in a restaurant, and you now know the courtesy words to show your good manners. How awesome! It’s now time to put your learning into good use.

Take your family to a Chinese restaurant that’s close to you. Take a good look at the menu at the restaurant, and order for you and your family in Chinese. Ideally you should order four dishes and one soup for the family. If you feel up to it, you can also order drinks for everyone. It’ll be fun!

Think and Apply

祝贺你！ Zhùhè nǐ! Congratulations! You’ve tackled this lesson with excellence! Now do you think you can showcase to a family member or friend what you’ve learned—maybe when you guys eat out? You can showcase and teach your family or friend the following:

  • Use 我要 Wǒyào (I want…) to order a few dishes in Chinese. Remember to specify the quantity with proper measure words you learned in this lesson.
  • Use 请给我 Qǐnggěiwǒ (Please give me) to order a soup in Chinese.
  • Explain how to pronounce “x” in Chinese, and compare it to similar pronunciation(s) in your own language.
  • Explain the table etiquette in China, and ask a family member about the table manners in your own culture.
