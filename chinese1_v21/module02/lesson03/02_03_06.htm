得偿所愿 Dé cháng suǒ yuàn Get What I Want

You are now equipped with quite a lot of vocabulary words. It’s time to put them together to form sentences in Chinese that you can use when ordering.

Ordering With 我要 Wǒ yào (I want)

我要 Wǒ yào (I want) can be followed by a verb or noun to indicate what you want or want to do. To order food, you simply use 我要 Wǒ yào (I want), followed by the item(s) of your desire.

For example:

我要米饭。 Wǒ yào mǐfàn. I want rice.

我要面条和西兰花。 Wǒ yào miàntiáo hé xīlánhuā. I want noodles and broccoli.

Specify Your Order

You can use a number, followed by 碗 wǎn (bowl), 杯 bēi (cup), or 盘 pán (plate) as a measure word to further specify your order.

For example:

我要一碗米饭。 Wǒ yào yī wǎn mǐfàn. I want a bowl of rice.

For individual items that can be counted, such as fruits, you will need a measure word 个 gè (count of) between the number and item. For example, instead of saying “three apples,” you say “three counts of apples” in Chinese:

我要三个苹果。 Wǒ yào sān gè píngguǒ. I want three counts of apples.

Note:

  • When ordering two of something, instead of using 二 èr , you use 两 liǎng, another word for “two.” It is used with a measure word. For example: 两盘牛肉 liǎng pán niúròu (two plates of beef).
  • When there are two items in a stir-fry dish, you place the two items before and after 炒 chǎo (stir-fry). For example: 白菜炒牛肉 báicài chǎo niúròu (cabbage stir-fry beef).
What about… ?

You can use the structure noun/pro-noun + 呢 ne (What about… ?) to inquire what others want.

Note: Recall 你呢？ Nině? (What about you?)

For example:

我要一碗米饭。他呢？ Wǒ yào yī wǎn mǐfàn. Tā ne? I want a bowl of rice. What about him?
