演讲时间 Yǎnjiǎng shíjiān Presentational Speaking

It’s time to showcase your verbal skills on what you’ve learned. For this presentation, you will record an audio or a video, speaking in complete sentences in Chinese. Your audio or video should include the grammar and vocabulary from the lessons.

View the menu from the restaurant Teddy’s below. Using complete sentences in Chinese, express your preferences on two different items on the menu; then order for yourself one food item and one drink item in Chinese.

Note:

  • Use adverbs such as “很 hěn (very),” “有点 yǒudiǎn (a little),” “最 zuì (the most),” and “不 bù (not)” when expressing your preferences.
  • When ordering food and drink items, be sure to specify the quantities with measure words.
  • Remember to use courtesy words when ordering.
  • Your preferences on the food or drink items can be fictitious. It doesn’t have to be real.

  2. Know the requirements of your presentation.

    Note: The sample English sentences in parentheses are just a guide. You need to come up with your own original sentences in Chinese.

    • Say a greeting to the waiter. (e.g., Good morning!)
    • Say a food item that you like and another that you don’t like. (e.g., I like bacon the most. I don’t like oatmeal.)
    • Say a drink that you like and another that you don’t like. (e.g., I like pineapple juice very much. I don’t like cranberry juice.)
    • Order a food item with quantity and measure words. (e.g. I want two plates of bacon.)
    • Order a drink item with quantity and measure words. (e.g., Please give me one glass of pineapple juice.)
    • Complete your order with a courtesy word. (e.g., Thank you!)
  3. Finalize your oral presentation
    • Your recording should be about 15 seconds long.
    • Start your presentation by stating your first and last name in English.
    • Give your presentation in Chinese, using appropriate vocabulary and grammar.
    • Speak clearly and loudly enough for the microphone to record all your words.
    • Speak at an appropriate rate. (Don’t speak too quickly or too slowly, and do not take extended pauses.)
    • Use correct tones and pronunciations throughout the speech.
    • Practice a couple of times first in a mirror or in front of a family member or friend.

Congratulations! You are on your way to becoming a fluent Chinese speaker!
