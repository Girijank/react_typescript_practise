声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson, we will learn the initial “q” and the finals “uo” and “an.”

Initial “q”

This initial sounds like “ch” as in “cheese” in English, with the tip of your tongue touching the back of your bottom teeth. It’s used in words such as “曲奇 qǔqí (cookie)” and “巧克力 qiǎokèlì (chocolate) .”

Final “uo”

This final sounds like the combination of “oo-aw,” with “oo” (as in “boo”) and “aw” (as in “law”). It’s used in words such as “苹果 píngguǒ (apple)” and “多少钱 duōshǎoqián (how much). ”

Final “an”

This final sounds like the combination of “a” as in “father” followed with a nasal “n” sound. It’s used in words such as “菜单 càidān (menu)” and “碗 wǎn (bowl). ”
