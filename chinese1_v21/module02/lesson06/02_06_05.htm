Welcome to Hong Kong, China! Let me introduce to you my life in Hong Kong.

你好！我叫郭大卫，我住在中国香港。我今年十七岁。香港有很多好吃的和好喝的。我最喜欢吃蛋挞和牛肉面，我也喜欢喝奶茶。牛肉面十五元一碗。

Hello! My name is David Kwok. I live in Hong Kong, China. I am seventeen years old this year. There are a lot of delicious food and drinks in Hong Kong. I like to eat egg tarts and beef noodles the most. I also like to drink milk tea. Beef noodles are 15 yuan per bowl.

An important part of Hong Kong culture is to “drink” morning, afternoon, or evening tea with friends. But we don’t just drink tea; we also eat all kinds of dim sum such as buns and noodles.

我妈妈每天和朋友喝早茶和下午茶。有时他们也喝夜茶。他们吃很多点心。他们用现金买单。

My mom has morning tea and afternoon tea with friends every day. Sometimes they also have evening tea. They eat a lot of dim sum. They pay with cash.

Areas in Hong Kong

Hong Kong is one of the most modern cities in Asia. It’s always fast-paced and is known as the “heaven for shopping,” with many stores and shopping centers. Hong Kong has 18 districts divided into four parts: Hong Kong island, the Kowloon peninsula, the New Territories, and other outlying islands.

Places to Go

My favorite area is Tsim Sha Tui in Kowloon. It has the best fine-dining restaurants here. I remember attending a birthday celebration of my family’s friend there. It was extravagant. There are also many “dai pai dongs (street food stalls)” in this area. I sometimes eat dim sum here with my friends.

Dim Sum Galore

Hong Kong is known for its endless list of dim sum items people can enjoy. Dim sum items are placed in small bamboo steamers. Waiters/waitresses will place these steamers in carts and push them around among tables to offer to customers. Sometimes the carts are even heated to keep the dim sum warm. I have many favorites dim sum items. On the top of the list are shrimp dumplings, pork Siu Mai, steamed chicken feet, pan-fried turnip cake, beef noodle rolls, and so many more.

This makes me hungry, and I’ll go have my afternoon tea with my friends now.
