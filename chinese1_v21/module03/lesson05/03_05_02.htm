总结 Zǒngjiē Recap

Let’s quickly review what you’ve learned in this module so far. Here’s a checklist that you can go through very quickly for a little recap.

  • Saying titles for immediate family members and pets
  • Saying titles for stepfamily members, using 继 jì
  • Saying titles for paternal and maternal grandparents
  • Saying names of different countries, cities, or regions
  • Asking and answering how many people are in one’s family
  • Asking and answering who is in one’s family
  • Expressing “do not have,” using 没有 méiyǒu
  • Expressing plural pronouns, using 们 men
  • Expressing close possession or relationship without 的 de
  • Describing family members’ personalities and/or appearances, using various adjectives and adverbs of degree
  • Describing something in common among two or more people, using 都 dōu
  • Describing family’s origin, using 从...来… …cóng...lái...
  • China’s paternal society and its relationship to family structure
  • Taking care of the elderly in China
  • Typical modern and traditional family structures in China
  • Table etiquette and rituals in China
  • Family celebrations and family manners in China
  • Describing national identity amongst Chinese minorities and majority and compare with own culture
  • Identifying minority ethnic groups in China and their locations, using a map
  • Pinyin components you’ve learned so far (Also remember to practice the tones.):
    • Initials: g; n; zh
    • Finals: ei, en, ong
