以爱为继 Yǐ ài wéi jì Steps with Love

Both words 继母 jìmǔ(stepmother) and 继父 jìfù(stepfather) have 继 jì in common, which means “to continue, to follow” by itself. In the case of family, it means a relationship formed by marriage or “step.” Simply place 继 jì before the family member. Review this interactive to see examples of how 继 jì is used.

Apply It

You are now able to converse with someone about family members and pets in Chinese. You can ask about others’ families, and you can share information about your own. How wonderful!

Let’s not stop right here, and let’s continue exploring the topics. Here are some missions for you:

  • Do you have a pet that is neither a cat nor a dog? If so, ask your instructor how to say that particular pet in Chinese. You will then introduce to your friends a pet that you have (or don’t have but want to have) in Chinese.
  • Compare your own family experience with that of Lili Chen’s research on Page 6, especially that of the older generations in your family, such as your great-great-grandparents living in the 1900s. Talk to your grandparents or a family friend of similar age to see what they can share with you about their grandparents’ lives in the old times. It’ll be fun!

Think and Apply

祝贺你！Zhùhè nǐ! Congratulations! You’ve tackled this lesson with excellence! It’s time to showcase to a friend what you’ve learned. It’s going to be a great topic to discuss with friends! You can showcase and teach your family or friend the following:

  • Introduce two family members that you have, using 有 yǒu (to have).
  • Introduce two family members that you don’t have, using 没有 méiyǒu (do not have). Or if you have them all (how lucky!), you can introduce a pet that you don’t have.
  • Ask your friend how many people are in his/her family.
  • Ask your friend who are in his/her family.
  • Explain how to pronounce “g” and “ei” in Chinese, and compare them to similar pronunciation(s) in your own language.
  • Tell two facts about men’s and women’s social roles in ancient and modern China, and compare them with your own culture.
