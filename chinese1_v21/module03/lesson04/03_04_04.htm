爱之树 Ài zhī shù The Tree Of Love

Now let’s put these characters into action. You will create a family collage, based on one of the scenarios provided below, and then write two descriptive sentences to describe two of the family members on the collage.

When creating the family collage and writing the descriptive sentences, you can use the characters taught in this module and/or the characters you’ve learned previously. You are expected to use at least 20 characters to fill up your family collage

Use the Descriptions Review worksheet to review the adjectives and adverbs of degree you can use to form descriptive sentences.

Families come in different shapes and sizes. Create your own family collage design or look online for an existing template that matches your family. Remember that you can “customize” any of the templates you find by adding or removing boxes, or by moving them around to suit your own situation. Have fun!

Scenario A

Create a family collage or a family tree for your own family. Include at least three generations of your family with at least six members (you and at least five others). Don’t forget about any pet(s) the family has. Then write three descriptive sentences to describe two of the family members on the collage.

Scenario B

Create a family collage or a family tree for a famous person and his/her family. Include at least three generations of the family and at least six members (you and at least five others). Make yourself part of the family tree as well. (Imagine being martial arts actor and stuntman Jackie Chan’s grandchild or even Nikki SooHoo’s younger sibling!) Don’t forget about any pet(s) the family has. Then write three descriptive sentences to describe two of the family members on the collage.

Complete the contents of the family collage, using the appropriate characters from the demonstrations on the "Writing Chinese Characters" teaching and/or any other characters you’ve learned previously. You can choose to handwrite the characters or type the characters directly onto a word document, and then save the document as your work.

Be Creative

You may choose an image from clipart, a picture, or a drawing of your own as the background for the family collage. You can also change the design to beautify it.

You can use the checklist below to help with your work:

What to include in your Family Collage Hint

Self:


Include an image of yourself (real or imaginary) with your name and the title “I.”

Think about:


What pronoun is used to address yourself?

Family Members #1-5:


  • Include at least five other family members in your “collage,”
  • Include at least three generations of the family members.
  • For each member, include an image with their name and a title (mom, dad, grandparents, siblings, etc.).
  • For siblings, also include their ages with numerical numbers (15 years old).
  • Optional – Include your pet(s) if there are any (real or imaginary).

Think about:

  • Remember to use different titles for older or younger siblings.
  • Remember to use 继 jì (step-) if there are stepfamily members.

Descriptive Sentences:


  • Write two sentences, using adjectives and adverbs of degree, to describe respectively the physical or personal characteristics of two family members on the collage.
  • Write one more sentence to describe a common characteristic for these two family members, using the word for both or all and plural pronouns.

Think about:

  • When writing the first two sentences, remember to use adverbs of degree before the adjectives such as “very” and “a little.” Below are some sample sentences in English:

    Examples in English: (Yours will be in Chinese and using vocabulary that you have learned.)

    My uncle is very curious. My aunt is a little strict.

  • When writing the third sentence, remember to use 都 dōu (both/all) and 们 men (plural for pronoun). Below is a sample sentence in English:

    Examples in English: (Yours will be in Chinese and using vocabulary that you have learned.)

    They are both very loving.
