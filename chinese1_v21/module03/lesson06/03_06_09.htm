文化一瞥 Wénhuà yīpiē Culture Blip

Meet Wanting Zhong, a young girl from Harbin, a city in Northern China. She has a lot to share about her life and cultural surroundings in Harbin as well as her childhood memories about family celebrations, especially birthday parties. Enjoy her narration.

Note: You don’t need to understand the whole narration in Chinese, but you should try to catch familiar words while listening, and get familiar with the ups and downs of Chinese speech.

Welcome to Harbin, China! Let me introduce to you my life in Harbin.

你好！我叫钟婉婷，我住在中国北方的城市哈尔滨。我家有五个人，爷爷，奶奶，爸爸，妈妈和我。我的爷爷和奶奶都很善良。我的爸爸有点帅，我的妈妈比较年轻。我们没有宠物。

Hello! My name is Wanting Zhong. I live in Northern China in the city of Harbin. There are five people in my family: my paternal grandpa, my paternal grandma, my dad, my mom and I. My paternal grandpa and grandma are both very kind. My dad is a little handsome. My mom is somewhat young. We don’t have pets.

Harbin is in the northern part of China, close to the border with Russia. It has windy springs and warm but rainy summers. During autumns here, there are drastic temperature drops at night.

哈尔滨的冬天很冷，从十一月到第二年的三月。我的生日是十二月十八日，是在冬天。我小的时候，我爸爸妈妈总是庆祝我的生日。他们给我吃蛋糕。以前我爷爷在夏天经常给我买冰激凌。他现在老了，不经常出门了。

It’s very cold during winters in Harbin from November to March of the next year. My birthday is on December 18th. It’s in the winter. When I was little, my mom and dad always celebrated my birthday. They gave me cake to eat. In the past, my paternal grandpa often bought me ice cream in the summer. He is old now, so he doesn’t go out often.

Activities and Festivals in Harbin

Harbin is known for its ice and snow sculptures. Its nickname is “Ice City.” Each year, it holds the Harbin International Ice and Snow Sculpture Festival. My mom always took my younger sister and me there when we were little.

Activities and Festivals in Harbin

Another fun thing to do in Harbin is to go skiing. It has the largest and best ski resort in China—Yabuli Ski Resort. In 2018, they held a costumed skiing competition, and it was a success.

Activities and Festivals in Harbin

Another wonder to watch in Harbin is winter swimming. Each winter, local people gather along the side of the Songhua River and swim when the average temperature is about -13 degrees Fahrenheit. Brrrr!

Street Snacks in Harbin

There are many street snacks I always had when I was little. I enjoyed snacks such as Harbin sausage, Harbin Madier ice cream, Guobaorou (crispy sweet and sour pork), Dalieba (Russian-style big bread), just to name a few. My favorite is the Madier ice cream.

Architecture in Harbin

The architecture in Harbin has heavy European (especially Russian) influences as it’s close to the border. Here we often see characteristic domes of Russian-style buildings on the street.
