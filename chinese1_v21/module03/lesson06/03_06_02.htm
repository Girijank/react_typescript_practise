声母韵母 Shēngmǔ yùnmǔ Initials and Finals

In this lesson, we will learn the initial “z” and the finals “un” and “ing.”

Initial “z”

This initial sounds like “ds” as in “kids” and “loads” in English. It’s used in words such as “孩子 háizi (child)” and “最 zuì (the most).”

Final “un”

This final sounds like the “uen” as in “fluent” in English. It’s used in words such as “春天 chūntiān (spring).” (You will learn this word in this lesson.)

Final “ing”

This final is a back-nasal final, which means it is a sound with the air coming through your nose. It sounds like “in” in English as in “bin” with the nasal ending. It’s used in words such as “眼镜 yǎnjìng (glasses)” and “年轻 niánqīng (young).”
