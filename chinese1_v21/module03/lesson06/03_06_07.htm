节日和庆祝 Jiérì hé qìngzhù Festivals and Celebrations

Remember Kailan Zhang who comes from a traditional family in Beijing, China? She shared some of her family traditions, and now she’s going to share information about various traditional festivals in China throughout the seasons. Let’s experience the fun celebrations of these seasonal festivals.

Spring Festival

  • The Spring Festival is also known as Chinese New Year. It celebrates the end of a cold winter and the start of a new spring.
  • The Spring Festival is in the spring. Its celebration lasts for 15 days, from the 1st day of the lunar new year to the 15th day. The 15th day is also known as the Lantern Festival.
  • During Spring Festivals, people do many activities, such as those listed below.
    • Have New Year family dinner.
    • Eat dumplings also known as “jiaozi.”
    • Set off firecrackers.
    • Put up couplets.
    • Visit relatives.
    • Children receive red packets from adults.

Dragon Boat Festival

  • The Dragon Boat Festival commemorates the famous scholar in Chinese history, Qu Yuan, for his patriotism. He drowned himself in the Miluo River in honor of his nation. Nowadays, people celebrate it to dispel disease and welcome good health.
  • The Dragon Boat Festival is in the summer. It is on the 5th day of the 5th month in the Chinese lunar calendar.
  • During the Dragon Boat Festival, people do the following to celebrate:
    • Eat sticky rice cakes known as “zongzi”
    • Dragon boat racing
    • Drink realgar
    • Hang herbs on the door such as Chinese Mugwort and Calamus
    • Wear perfume pouches

Moon Festival

  • The Moon Festival is also known as the Mid-Autumn Festival. It celebrates people’s hard work and harvest for the year. It is also a time for people to appreciate the full moon and celebrate the union of the family.
  • The Moon Festival is in the autumn. It is on the 15th day of the 8th month in the Chinese lunar calendar.
  • During the Moon Festival, people do the following to celebrate:
    • Have family reunion dinner
    • Eat mooncakes known as “yuebing”
    • Appreciate the full moon
    • Make colorful lanterns
    • Play riddles or recite poems

Winter Solstice

  • Winter Solstice is the winter day when the day is the shortest and the night the longest. In China, it celebrates the balance and harmony in life and togetherness in family.
  • Winter Solstice is in the winter. It is on December 21st, 22nd, or 23rd in the solar calendar.
  • Winter Solstice celebrations mostly involve eating various types of foods that symbolize togetherness of family and bring warmth to the body:
    • Eat dumplings known as “jiaozi”
    • Eat sticky rice balls known as “tangyuan”
    • Drink rice wines
    • Have hotpot with fatty meats
    • Eat pumpkin cakes
