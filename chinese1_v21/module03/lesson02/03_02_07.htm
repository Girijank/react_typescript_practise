单数复数 Dānshù fùshù Single Or Plural

You are now equipped with quite some vocabulary on family member titles. How awesome! Two other important structures when talking about family are the use of pronouns and possession. Let’s see what those look like in Chinese.


``
The use of 们 men to indicate plural in pronouns.

The character 们 men is placed after a pronoun to indicate plural. Check this chart for some examples:

Single form Plural form
我 wǒ (I, me) 我们 wǒmen (we, us)
你 nǐ (you) 你们 nǐmen (you (plural))
他 tā (he) 他们 tāmen (they (in general))
她tā (she) 她们 tāmen (they (all female))
它 tā (it) 它们 tāmen (they (all non-human))
Express close possession or relationship without 的 de

Formally, 的 de is used after a noun or pronoun to indicate possession or relationship. Sometimes 的 de is omitted for close personal relationships in informal conversations.

For example:

我的姐姐 Wǒde jiějie (my older sister) can be changed to: 我姐姐 Wǒ jiějie (my older sister).

Below are a few more examples in full sentences:

我爸爸是一个医生。 Wǒ bàba shì yīgè yīshēng. My dad is a doctor.

你女儿很善良。 Nǐ nüér hěn shànliáng. Your daughter is very kind.
