长辈大爱 Zhǎng bèi dà ài Grandparents Grand Love

It’s time to learn how to address your grandparents in Chinese. You are now going to add more to your repertoire of family member titles.

Note: Pay attention to the characters that are in bold type and highlighted in dark red. You will learn to write them later in the module. The characters highlighted in grey are from the previous lesson(s). You should continue practicing them. For characters not in bold type or highlighted, feel free to practice writing them in your note guide, but remember that for now, you will only need to know how to write the bold or highlighted characters.

Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need to sound like a native speaker!



Notes

In different regions in China, some different phrases are used to address grandparents. For example, in some regions people say 姥姥 lǎolao for maternal grandmother and 姥爷 lǎoye for maternal grandfather. You will learn about these titles in the future.
