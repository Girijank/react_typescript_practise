追随传统 zhuīsuí chuántǒng Following Traditions

This summer Kailan Zhang, a teenage girl from a traditional family in Beijing, China, attended a cultural immersion summer camp in Shanghai where she met Karen Lu, an American Chinese girl from Flushing, New York. During the camp, they attended a “show-and-tell” presentation, during which they shared their different family traditions. Below are the personal stories they shared. This is from Kailan. Remember to complete the comprehension questions.

Holidays

  • As a family, we celebrate all traditional Chinese holidays such as Chinese New Year, Moon Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, and Double Ninth Festival.
  • Chinese New Year is a major holiday in the Chinese tradition. During Chinese New Year, as a family, we have the New Year’s Eve family dinner, visit relatives, set off firecrackers or fireworks, and hang couplets and lanterns. The children in the family receive red packets from the adults.
  • As a family, we don’t celebrate Western holidays, such as Christmas.

Family Celebrations

  • We celebrate every family member’s birthday. There are usually big birthday parties for older family members.
  • We often visit extended family throughout the year, and the whole family always gets together on major holidays.
  • It is not common here for people to celebrate some Western events, such as baby showers or gender reveal parties.

Family Responsibilities

  • We don’t openly express affection by hugging or kissing. Instead, we express love by actions such as giving gifts.
  • My dad is the oldest son of the Zhang family, and he takes care of my paternal grandpa and grandma. His siblings share financial responsibilities when needed.

Weddings and Funerals

  • We attend weddings and funerals of immediate and extended family members. Such events usually include a banquet. We wear formal traditional clothes to the events and bring a “red packet” with cash in it as a gift. In some regions such as Taiwan, people bring a "白包 báibāo (white packet)" with money inside to funerals.
  • For weddings, the bride’s family provides the dowry, and the groom’s family provides the “彩礼 cǎilǐ (bride price).” The amount of both is usually agreed on by both families before the wedding.
  • After the wedding, the newlyweds return to the husband’s house. They will visit the wife’s parents three days after the wedding.
  • Traditionally, the theme color for weddings is red, and for funerals, it’s white.

Dating and Marriage (a love story)

My great-grandma, who is in her 90s, told me her love story with my great-grandpa back in the 1940s. My great-grandma was 25 and not engaged, which was considered “old” in her time. Then my great-grandpa, who saw her at the market once, came to visit her father with a decent amount of “bride price,” hoping for his approval. While they were talking in the living room, my great-grandma took a peek at my great-grandpa behind the door. She liked him and asked her dad to approve. They then got married. My great-grandma said they never even touched each other’s hands before their marriage.

And this is from Karen. Remember to complete the comprehension questions.

Holidays

  • We also celebrate major traditional Chinese holidays including Chinese New Year, Moon Festival, and Dragon Boat Festival.
  • For Chinese New Year celebrations, we follow most of the traditional Chinese traditions except no setting off fireworks!
  • We are lucky because we also get to celebrate Christmas and other Western holidays, during which we do preserve some Chinese traditions, such as adults giving red packets or presents to children, having a family dinner, and visiting relatives.

Family Celebrations

  • We celebrate everyone’s birthday in the family. It’s usually a small gathering with a nice dinner and cake.
  • Once a year, we have a family reunion where everyone in the family comes.
  • We adopted some Western holidays and traditions. For example, we will have baby showers for family members who are pregnant.

Family Responsibilities

  • My grandparents seldom express affection, but my parents are direct in showing their love for me by hugging or kissing.
  • My maternal grandparents live in a senior home with their pension. My mom and her siblings take turns visiting them and taking care of financial matters when needed.

Weddings and Funerals

  • We attend immediate and extended family members’ weddings and funerals. We wear formal clothes to the events, whether it’s traditional or Western. We bring flowers or gifts to the events.
  • Here, weddings are usually paid for by the bride’s parents. There is no dowry or bride price involved.
  • After the wedding, the newlyweds go on a honeymoon.
  • Here, the common color theme for weddings is white, and for funerals, it’s black.

Dating and Marriage (a love story)

My maternal great-grandparents have already passed away, but my maternal grandma told me their love story. My great-grandpa was a veteran from World War II. He was already an American citizen at that time. He and my great-grandma, who worked at her dad’s grocery store in San Francisco, already had been dating for a year when he joined the war. They exchanged love letters and pictures of each other weekly and affectionately until the war was over. Once they reunited, they got married right away and were never separated the rest of their lives.
