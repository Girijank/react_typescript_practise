我来自... Wǒ láizì… I Am From…

When talking with a new friend, you will want to know where he or she is from. You will also want to introduce where you are from. Let’s explore some vocabulary you might use or hear during such conversations in Chinese.

Note: Pay attention to the characters that are in bold type and highlighted in dark red. You will learn to write them later in the module. The characters highlighted in grey are from the previous lesson(s). You should continue practicing them. For characters not in bold type or highlighted, feel free to practice writing them in your note guide, but remember that for now, you will only need to know how to write the bold type or highlighted characters.


Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need to sound like a native speaker!


Notes

  • When using the 2nd character of the word 城市 chéngshì (city), 市 shì is often placed after the name of a city. For example: 坦帕市 Tǎnpà Shì (Tampa City).
  • Recall the phrase 我从…来. Wǒ cóng…lái. ( I am from ...). You can now use this structure to introduce the country, region, or city that you are from. For example: 我从美国来。 Wǒ cóng Měiguó lái. (I am from the United States.)
