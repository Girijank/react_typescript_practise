以前没有过 Yǐqián méiyǒu guò Never in the Past


Negation of Past Action

Do you remember the word 没有 méiyǒu (do not) you previously learned to indicate not being in possession of something? Now you will use it with 过 guò (pass/ (past tense indicator)) to express negation of past action.

Here’s the structure you should use:

Subject + 没有 méiyǒu + verb + 过 guò + (object)

For example:

我没有去过香港。Wǒ méiyǒu qù guò Xiānggǎng. (I haven’t been to Hong Kong.)

她没有吃过冰激凌。Tā méiyǒu chī guò bīngjīlíng. (She hasn’t eaten ice cream.)

You can also add 以前 yǐqián (in the past/before) after the subject in the sentence.

For example:

我奶奶以前没有看过电影。Wǒ nǎinai yǐqián méiyǒu kàn guò diànyǐng. (My paternal grandma hasn’t seen a movie before.)

他妹妹以前没有穿过牛仔裤。Tā mèimei yǐqián méiyǒu chuān guò niúzǎikù. (His younger sister hasn’t worn jeans before.)

Note:

There are other phrases you can add to the structure to further enrich the sentence. You will learn them in the future.
