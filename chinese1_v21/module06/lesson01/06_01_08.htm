快乐社区 Kuàilè shèqū Happy Community

Nice job learning the vocabulary on shared facilities for community recreation or activities. Now let’s explore the sites or places that people go for recreation, entertainment, or fitness in apartment communities in Chinese-speaking regions.


©iStock

Gyms and Fitness Sites

Keeping fit and staying healthy is highly valued in Chinese communities, especially in clustered apartment buildings. Gyms and fitness centers can be easily found in apartment communities in China. Many such sites are located within walking distance of the residents. Some communities also provide indoor or outdoor facilities, such as swimming pools and basketball courts.

©iStock

Mahjong and Chess Rooms

Mahjong and chess games are popular among community residents in China. Many apartment communities provide rooms in their activity centers where residents can play mahjong, chess, or other board games, such as poker or checker games. Sometimes such rooms also provide comfortable areas where residents can get together to enjoy tea and chat. In the summertime, such activities can also be set up outdoors.


©iStock

Music and Karaoke Sites

A fun way to get people together in communities in China is playing music. Many apartment communities provide indoor or outdoor sites where residents can play music together. Senior people like to play Chinese traditional music, while the younger generation enjoys playing pop music. Singing karaoke is another musical activity enjoyed by residents of all ages. Such sites are usually equipped with stereo systems and televisions, and many community events, such as parties or meetings, can be held there. Weather permitting, residents also enjoy playing music or singing in outdoor pavilions or verandas.

©iStock

Children’s Play Sites

Children in apartment communities in China are usually provided with sites where they can play. For indoor sites, facilities are provided such as games, educational toys, or mini movie theaters. Many communities also hold educational events at these sites, such as book-reading nights or parent-children relationship seminars. Outdoor children’s play, such as playgrounds and educational bulletins are also common in apartment communities.


©iStock

Library and Reading Rooms

Reading is encouraged in communities in China. Many apartment communities provide libraries or reading rooms for residents of all ages. To promote reading, many communities also hold events, such as reading competitions, poetry reciting games, or themed reading groups during Chinese traditional festivals. Sometimes young student residents rely on the community libraries for their school studies and research.

©iStock

Dancing Sites

Residents in Chinese communities enjoy dancing as a pastime and a way of keeping fit. Senior residents mostly like Chinese traditional dances, while the younger generation mostly likes modern dances such as breakdancing. Many apartment communities have their own dancing teams and often participate in competitions or celebrations with other teams. Apartment communities often provide indoor studios or outdoor plazas for town square dancing, which is popular in many Chinese communities. Many people also practice tai chi at these sites.
