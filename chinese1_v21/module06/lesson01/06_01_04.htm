市场和购物 Shìchǎng hé gòuwù Markets and Shopping

Do you wonder what types of markets there are in communities and how to go shopping in Chinese-speaking regions? Let’s explore several types of markets on the other side of the globe.

©iStock

Farmers Market

农贸市场 Nóngmào shìchǎng

Farmers markets are commonly seen in communities in China. People go there to get their daily supplies of fresh meat, vegetables, and fruit. A typical farmers market has many stalls where various types of food are sold. Such markets are usually within walking distance from people’s apartments. Bargaining is a common practice in farmers markets in China.

©iStock

Retail Market

零售市场 Língshòu shìchǎng

Retail stores, including grocery stores, can be seen in communities in China. They are usually within walking distance from people’s homes. In many places, retail stores are located on the ground floor of apartment buildings for people’s convenience. Such stores provide supplies and services that people need in their everyday lives, from cleaning supplies, snacks, and clothes to haircuts, massages, and dry cleaning.


©iStock

Wholesale Market

批发市场 Pīfā shìchǎng

Wholesale markets are usually in commercial areas in cities or towns where retailers in communities can get their supplies in bulk. Sometimes, residents in communities visit wholesale markets, despite the distance, and shop in bulk for the better prices. Wholesale markets are usually set up by their specialties, such as fabric wholesale, meat wholesale, furniture wholesale, etc.

©iStock

Flea Market

跳蚤市场 Tiàozǎo shìchǎng

Flea markets are a unique type of market in communities in China where people buy or sell various items, new or pre-owned, from hand-crafted arts and crafts by local artists to antiques passed down for generations in someone’s family. People in communities, especially seniors, enjoy visiting flea markets as a pastime, and they sometimes find hidden treasures of great value.


©iStock

Supermarket

超级市场 Chāojí shìchǎng

Supermarkets, or hypermarkets, are becoming popular in metropolitan cities in China such as Beijing and Shanghai due to the dense population. They are sprawling across the nation rapidly. There are chain markets provided by large companies, domestic and international, in the supermarket and hypermarket industry in China, and the competition is high. People in big cities can now find almost anything they need in supermarkets or hypermarkets that are usually conveniently located in or near their communities.

Naming Markets and Stores

Have you noticed two characters, 店 diàn and 市 shì, in the vocabulary you just learned? 店 diàn means “store,” and 市 shì means “market.” (You learned this previously by its other meaning—“city.”) You can use these two characters to form the names of various markets or stores by adding the specialty of the market or store before them. Let’s see some examples.

Notes

  • This naming does not apply to all markets and stores, and you will learn some exceptions in the future.
