学期考试 Xuéqī kǎoshì Segment Exam

You made it to the end of the course. Hooray! In this segment, you worked through three modules covering the themes of “My School,” “My House,” and “My Community.” It’s now time to show off your reading, listening, and cultural knowledge on these modules and themes. Before you dive into the exam, take a minute to study and review the topics below. Remember to use your note guides from each lesson and module to help you study.

Pronunciations

  • Pinyin initials “k,” “w,” “t,” “m,” “f,” “y,” “d,” “s,” “l,” “p,” “b,” and “r”
  • Pinyin finals “a,” “ue,” “ang,” “u,” “uan,” “ian,” “ui,” “iang,” “ia,” “in,”” uai,”” iu,”” u,” and “er”

“My School”

  • The use of "a.m." and "p.m."
  • Subjects and classes
  • School supplies
  • Clothing items
  • Planning on doing something with "要 yào (to want)"
  • Describing going somewhere with “去 qù (to go to)”
  • Telling time, including the hour and the minute
  • Using 需要 xūyào(to need) to describe needs of school supplies for classes
  • Using 或是 huòshì(or) in a statement and understanding its difference from 还是 háishì(or)
  • Using different verbs for “to wear” 穿 chuān and 戴 dài for different clothing items
  • Refreshing knowledge on borrowed words in Chinese
  • Days of the week
  • Describing school schedule including times, classes, and activities
  • Describing learned skills, using 会 huì (know how to)
  • Describing capabilities, using 能 néng (can/be able to)
  • Expressing permissions or requests, using 可以 kěyǐ (may/can)
  • Understanding when to use 会 huì (know how to), 能 néng (can/be able to), or 可以 kěyǐ (may/can)
  • Describing class activities, using “verb + object” structure
  • Understanding school dos and don’ts, using 不要 bùyào (do not)
  • Describing attending class, using 上 shàng (to attend (class))
  • Describing weather from being cold, cool, warm, to hot
  • Understanding class setup and learning focuses in Chinese schools
  • Describing campus life and classroom routines and setups
  • Knowing bell schedules and student lives in Chinese schools
  • Knowing various aspects of school life in Taiwan and its comparison with that in the U.S.

“My House”

  • Parts of a house or apartment
  • Furniture items in a bedroom and related chores
  • Furniture items in a kitchen or a living room and related chores
  • Furniture items in a bathroom and related chores
  • Items and chores related to laundry
  • Asking and telling the composition of a house, using 多少 duōshǎo (how many) or 几 jǐ(how many (answered with small numbers))
  • Using preposition words to describe the locations of houses or rooms
  • Using 有 yǒu (to have) to describe existence of furniture in a room
  • Using 要 yào (to want, to be going to) to introduce chores to do
  • Describing relative positions of furniture items, using the formula “item #1 + 在 zài (at) + Item #2 + 的 de (of) + preposition word”
  • Describe the existence of a furniture item with its position, using the formula “Item #1 + 的 de (of)+ preposition word + 有 yǒu (to have) + item #2”
  • Understanding different types of homes in China
  • Understanding the basic concepts of feng shui
  • Knowing the feng shui rules for the inside and outside of a house and its location
  • Knowing the feng shui rules for the bedroom
  • Knowing the feng shui rules for the kitchen and the living room
  • Knowing about the Chinese Farmers Almanac, including its origin, function in the past and in the present, and how to use it
  • Understanding cooking methods, utensils, and cooking routines in China
  • Knowing about common compositions and furniture items and responsibilities around the house in urban and rural China
  • Knowing various aspects of family lives, especially house compositions and responsibilities, and cultural aspects in Southern China.
  • Understanding the cultural aspects related to public and home-based laundry in China

“My Community”

  • Places in a community
  • Identifying and applying how to discuss specialized stores with “店 diàn” in Chinese
  • Identifying and applying how to express negation of past actions with "没有 méiyǒu"
  • Exploring shared activity/entertainment/recreation areas in apartment communities in China
  • Reading signs representing places in the community
  • Activities done around town
  • Identifying and applying how to use tag questions with "吗 ma"
  • Comparing and contrasting popular pastimes in China with own culture
  • Identifying Chinese music genres and the music culture in China
  • The downtown area of a community
  • Comparing and contrasting downtown city layouts in China and in the U.S
  • Giving suggestions with "吧 ba"
  • Tag questions with "不 bù"
  • Affirmative-negative questions in Chinese
  • Using a map to identify and provide the locations of different buildings in a community, using prepositions
  • Travel and places in and around the community
  • The difference between 坐 zuò and 骑 qí for “to ride” in different modes of transportation
  • How to do something with "怎么 zěnme"
  • Order of addresses in Chinese
  • Identifying key information and details in a narrative about rural Versus urban life for a Chinese student from Beijing
