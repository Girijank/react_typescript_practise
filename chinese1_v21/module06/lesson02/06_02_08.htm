Notes

  • The word “to play” in Chinese takes various forms, depending on what you are playing. In sports, you often use the verb 打 dǎ (to hit), such as 打乒乓球 dǎ pīngpāngqiú (to play ping-pong). It is also used for sports that mostly involve balls such as volleyball, basketball, badminton, tennis, and American football. However, the soccer ball is an exception, for which you need to use 踢 tī (to kick).
  • The word 运动 yùndòng (to exercise/fitness/sports) can be used as a verb itself. It can also be used as a noun, when the verb to be used with it is 做 zuò (to do). So you can say both 运动 yùndòng and 做运动 zuòyùndòng for “to do exercise.”
  • The word 吗 ma (question particle) is a particle placed at the end of a statement to turn it into a question. The character itself doesn’t have any meaning.
