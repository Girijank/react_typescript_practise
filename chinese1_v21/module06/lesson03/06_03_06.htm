问题和建议 Wèntí hé jiànyì Questions and Suggestions

Let’s see how to utilize the vocabulary you just learned in a real-life situation.

It’s Sunday morning. Lili and her family are planning for a day trip to the downtown area in their city. Lili is telling them her plan for where to go and what do to. Let's listen to part of what she told them.

我们上午十点去博物馆看画，上午十一点去广场散步和拍照，下午一点去剧院看音乐剧，下午两点去图书馆读书。

Wǒmén shàngwǔ shídiǎn qù bówùguǎn kàn huà, shàngwǔ shíyīdiǎn qù guǎngchǎng sànbù hé pāizhào, xiàwǔ yīdiǎn qù jùyuàn kàn yīnyuèjù, xiàwǔ liǎngdiǎn qù túshūguǎn dúshū.

We will go to the museum to look at the paintings at 10 a.m.; we go to the plaza to walk and take pictures at 11 a.m.; we'll go watch a musical at the theater at 1 p.m.; and we will go to the library to read at 2 p.m.

Nice job learning the vocabulary on different places you can go to in the city downtown. Now it’s time to put them into action, using complete sentences in Chinese. Let’s learn some sentence structures on how to ask questions or give suggestions on what places to go. You can find this handy when showing your friend around the city.


Give suggestions with 吧 ba

The particle 吧 ba has several functions. Here you are learning how to use it to give suggestions. Check out the structure:

subject + verb + object + 吧 ba

In this structure, the subject can sometimes be omitted when it’s obvious whom you are referring to within a context.

Example #1:

你去吃晚餐吧。Nǐ qù chī wǎncān ba.
You go eat dinner.

Omitting subject:

去吃晚餐吧。Qù chī wǎncān ba.
Go eat dinner.

Example #2:

我们去医院吧。Wǒmen qù yīyuàn ba.
Let’s go to the hospital.

Omitting subject:

去医院吧。Qù yīyuàn ba.
Go to the hospital.

Note:

In Chinese, to start a sentence with “let’s,” you simply say 我们 wǒmen (we).

Affirmative-negative question with 不 bù + verb

The particle 不 bù (not) can be used to form affirmative-negative questions. Below is the structure:

subject + verb + 不 bù + repeat verb + object?

Example #1:

你去不去图书馆？Nǐ qù bù qù túshūguǎn?
Are you going to the library or not?

Example #2:

他看不看电影？Tā kàn bù kàn diànyǐng?
Does he watch movies or not?

When the verb has two characters, you can either repeat the whole verb, or only say the first character of the verb before 不 bù.

For example:

喜欢 xǐhuān (to like) → 喜欢不喜欢 xǐhuān bù xǐhuān (like or not)

喜欢 xǐhuān (to like) → 喜不喜欢 xǐ bǔ xǐhuān. (like or not)

Example #3:

他需不需要字典？Tā xū bù xūyào zìdiǎn?
Does he need a dictionary or not?

Note: Sometimes when you verbally say the word 不 bù in combination with other characters, its tone changes. But in writing or typing, the tone stays unchanged. In the future, you will learn more about tone changes in spoken Chinese.

Affirmative-negative question with 不 bù + adjective

The same structure also works with adjectives:

subject + adjective + 不 bù + repeat adjective + object?

Example #1:

你的茶热不热？Nǐde chá rè bù rè?
Is your tea hot or not?

Example #2:

她姐姐漂不漂亮？Tā jiějie piào bù piàoliàng?
Is her older sister pretty or not?

Tag question with 不 bù

The particle 不 bù can also be used to form tag questions similar to those formed with 吗 ma that you previously learned.

Below is the structure:

statement + comma ”,” + verb + 不 bù + repeat verb?

The verbs often used for tag questions are: 好 hǎo (good)/是 shì(yes)/可以 kěyǐ(can/may).

Example #1:

  • 他是学生，是不是？Tā shì xuéshēng, shì bù shì?
    He is a student, isn’t he?
  • 我们去剧院，好不好？Wǒmen qù jùyuàn, hǎo bù hǎo?
    Let’s go to the theater, okay?
  • 今天你做饭，可不可以？Jīntiān nǐ zuòfàn, kě bù kěyǐ?
    You are cooking today, aren’t you?
