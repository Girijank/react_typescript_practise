How many districts does Kailan’s city have?

Kailan’s city, Beijing, has sixteen administrative districts, among which are six city districts, such as 东 城区 Dōngchéng qū (Dongcheng District), 西城区 Xīchéng qū (Xicheng District), and 海淀区 Hǎidiàn qū (Haidian District).

What is 西单 Xīdān (Xidan) presently known as?

Presently, 西单 Xīdān (Xidan) is known as the “Shopping Heaven for the Youth.”

What types of buildings or stores does Kailan’s district downtown have?

It has a culture square, shopping mall, book building, hotel, concert hall, culture museum, and office buildings.

Where does one expect to find at the place called 西单大悦城 Xīdān dàyuè chéng (Xidan Joy City)?

One is expected to find various types of merchandise from all over the world since it is an international shopping mall.


Big Cities, Small Cities


Recently Kailan is also working on a school project to compare the downtown areas in megacities in China, such as Beijing and Shanghai, and in small cities or towns. Below are some of her findings.

  • Downtown areas in megacities in China usually have chain shopping malls owned by big companies, while in smaller cities or towns, more privately-owned businesses are easily seen.
  • Downtown areas in megacities in China usually have tall buildings to accommodate more businesses, while in smaller cities or towns, the buildings are in smaller scale.
  • Downtown areas in megacities in China usually have large public buildings such as concert halls or theaters, while in smaller cities or towns, there are entertainment sites such as family restaurants or karaoke bars.
  • Downtown areas in megacities in China usually have wider streets and overhead bridges to accommodate heavy pedestrian traffic, while in smaller cities or towns, the streets are narrower and not many overhead bridges are built.
  • There are usually multiple downtown areas in megacities in China for multiple city districts, while in smaller cities or towns, there is usually one downtown area for each city or town.
  • Presently the smaller cities or towns in China are developing fast, and they are “copying” the downtown layouts in megacities and expanding the scales of businesses and buildings.
