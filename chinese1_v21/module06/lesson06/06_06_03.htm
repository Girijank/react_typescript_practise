录影中! Lùyǐng zhōng! In Action!

It’s now time to see what you’ve learned in action. Watch the dialogue video to see how the vocabulary and structures you’ve learned so far can be used in real-life settings; then you can apply them in your own daily life.

Helpful Hint

When viewing the dialogue videos in this course, remember:

  • Watch each video twice. First, listen through it to see how much you can initially understand. Pay attention to the context and non-verbal cues, such as facial expressions, hand gestures, and body movements.
  • Don’t worry if you don’t understand everything during the first viewing. Make use of the closed captioning to help you understand. Pause the video when necessary so you can have a full view of the sentences in characters or pinyin.
  • Feel free to speak along with the dialogue in the video. This will help you build a strong sense of intonations, pronunciations, and cadence in Chinese speech.
  • Read the comprehension questions beforehand. Keep the questions in mind while watching the video.
  • Answer the questions in your note guide, and then check your answers.
  • Note: You will have similar questions on the assessments, so be sure to take the time to practice now.

What places did Chen Lili and Zhang Ming think about going, and where did they decide to go in the end?

Chen Lili and Zhang Ming were talking about going to the library, downtown, or the countryside. They decided to go to the countryside in the end.

What do you think Chen Lili and Zhang Ming will be doing in the downtown area if they go there?

They may be watching a movie at the movie theater, watching a show at the theater, checking out an exhibition at the museum, or just strolling in the plaza.

How many ways did Chen Lili or Zhang Ming suggest getting downtown? Which one is likely the most expensive and which one the least?

Zhang Ming suggested going by walking first, and then taking bus two routes. Then Chen Lili suggested taking a taxi. Next, Zhang Ming suggested getting there by riding bikes. Of the three, the most expensive is taking a taxi, for which Zhang Ming expressed that he may not have enough money. Riding bikes is likely the least expensive since it’s what Zhang Ming suggested right after the taxi idea.
