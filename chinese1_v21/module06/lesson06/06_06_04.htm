到处走走 Dàochù zǒuzǒu Going to Places

Your friend from China is here to visit your town. How exciting! Now you will need to show your friend around and take him or her places. How will you arrange transportation, and how will you tell your friend about it? Let’s learn some vocabulary just for that.

Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio, and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need to sound like a native speaker!

Note: You may practice writing the characters of the words or phrases in your note guide, but you will not be assessed on writing them.


Notes

  • In 坐火车 zuòhuǒchē (to ride a train), 坐 zuò means “to ride” (its original meaning is “to sit down”), and 火车 huǒché means “train.” In 骑单车 qídānchē (to ride a bike), 骑 qí means “to ride,” and 单车 dānchē means “bike.” Both 坐 zuò and 骑 qí mean “to ride” but are used for different modes of transportation. Later in this lesson you will learn when to use which.
  • The word 散步 sànbù means “to walk” as a leisure pastime, and the word 走路 zǒulù means “to walk” to a destination. For example, 我走路去学校。Wǒ zǒulù qù xuéxiào. (I walk to school.) Sometimes 走 zǒu can be used by itself with the same meaning.
  • The first two characters, 巴士 bāshì, in the word 巴士站 bāshìzhàn (bus station) mean “bus,” and the third character, 站 zhàn, means “station.” In addition, 巴士 bāshì (bus) is a borrowed word from English. Select here to review a list of the borrowed words.
