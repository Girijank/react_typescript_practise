问路 Wènlù Asking for Directions

Notes

  • In the word 开车 kāichē (to drive a car), 开 kāi means “to operate,” and 车 chē means a vehicle. 开 kāi has multiple meanings and can be used in many situations. For example, you can say 开灯 kāidēng (turn on the light); 开门 kāimén (open the door); and 开课 kāikè (start a class). You will learn about its usage in more details in the future.
  • The word 转 zhuǎn (to turn) is often used with directions 左 zuǒ (left) or 右 yòu (right). 转 zhuǎn (to turn) can be placed after the direction word. For example, you can say 左转 zuǒzhuǎn, which means “turn left.”
  • The question word 怎么 zěnme (how), when used in a sentence, is placed directly before the verb. For example, you can ask 去医院怎么走？Qù yīyuàn zěnme zǒu? (How do I get to the hospital?)
  • The word 摩托车 mótuōchē (motorcycle) is a borrowed word from English. Search the Toolbox for the resource labeled “Borrowed Words.”
  • 路 lù (road) and 号 hào (number) are both used to say an address in Chinese.
