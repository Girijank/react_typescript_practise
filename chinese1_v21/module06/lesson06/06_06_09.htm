文化一瞥 Wénhuà yīpiē Culture Blip

Gang Chen is a high school student from Beijing, China. He’s active and enjoys outdoor activities. He also enjoys hiking and traveling. He is going to share some experiences he has in and around his city. Let’s hear what he has to say.

你好！我叫陈刚。我住在中国北京市西城区玉泉路十八号。 北京市很大，也有很多可以去的地方。 我们社区有图书馆和活动中心。 我总是去活动中心的健身房跑步， 去游泳池游泳。 我们社区也有医院和警察局。 我们的生活很方便。 我也喜欢去市中心， 因为那里有电影院，博物馆和购物中心。 购物中心旁边还有体育场，我经常去那里看演唱会。 我喜欢我的社区。

Hello! My name is Gang Chen. I live at #18, Yuquan Road, Xicheng District, Beijing, China. Beijing is very big, and it has many places to go to. Our community has a library and an activity center. I always go to the gym at the activity center to jog and go to the swimming pool there to swim. Our community also has a hospital and a police station. Life is convenient here. I also like to go downtown, because there are movie theaters, museums, and shopping centers there. Next to the shopping center there is also a stadium. I often go there to watch concerts. I like my community.

我最喜欢去乡村玩。 那里有湖和山， 人们也很开朗热情。 我经常骑单车去巴士站， 再坐巴士去火车站， 然后坐火车去郊区。 在那里我会租一辆单车， 骑单车去湖边，在那里拍照。 我也会去乡村旁边的农场买新鲜的猪肉， 水果和蔬菜， 放在我的背包里带回家。 我每次去乡下都很高兴。

I like to go to the countryside the most. There are lakes and mountains there, and people there are very outgoing and friendly. I usually ride my bike to the bus station, then ride the bus to the train station, and then ride the train to the suburbs. There I rent a bike and ride the bike to the lakeside and take pictures there. I also go to the farm next to the village to buy fresh pork, fruit, and vegetables, and take them home in my backpack. I am very happy every time I go to the countryside.


Gang shares some more information about his city, Beijing, and its suburban rural areas.

Places to Go in Beijing

  • The Forbidden City and the Palace Museum
    The Forbidden City is China’s most significant attraction. It has over 800 years of history. The complex covers an area of 720,000 square meters, and it has 9,999 rooms.
  • The Tiananmen Square
    The Tiananmen Square is the world's largest inner-city square. It’s surrounded by several grand gates including the Tiananmen Gate, the Zhengyangmen Gate, and several historical museums and monuments.
  • The Beijing National Stadium
    The Beijing National Stadium was built for the Summer Olympic sheld in 2008 in Beijing. It’s nicknamed the Bird’s Nest for its unique design and spectacular presence. It can hold up to 80,000 people. Many large events such as sporting games and concerts have been held here.

Places to Go in Beijing Suburban Areas

  • The Great Wall of China
    Located 72 kilometers northwest of Beijing, the Great Wall of China is one of the world’s most famous historic wonders. Its construction dates back 2,300 years and has been reconstructed and renovated through the long history of China.
  • Yesanpo National Park
    Yasanpo National Park is about a two-hour drive to the west of Beijing. It has six main scenic sites, including the Bai Li Xia Gorge (Hundred Li Gorge). It is a popular sightseeing or hiking destination for people from the Beijing.

Transportation in Beijing

Public transportation within the Beijing is very powerful. It has a large web of subway routes underground, and on the ground, there are various transportation methods to move large amounts of people fast and efficiently, such as taxis, personal cars, buses, trolleys, motorcycles, and bikes.

Transportation in Beijing Rural Areas

People living in rural areas in Beijing rely on smaller and more personal modes of transportation such as motorcycles and cars. Bus routes also reach most of the rural villages in suburban Beijing. The subway systems are also gradually expanding their coverage to rural areas.

Language Check -- The Beijing Accent

When you travel around in Beijing, whether in urban or rural areas, you will hear the Beijing dialect. The Beijing dialect has a slight variation from Mandarin Chinese. It has the following characteristics:

  • The Beijing dialect is spoken at a faster speed than Mandarin Chinese.
  • The Beijing dialect is spoken at a higher pitch, and the pronunciations are often exaggerated.
  • A special “er” sound is frequently used in the Beijing dialect. It’s usually at the end of a noun, but there is no fixed rule on its usage. Here are a few examples:
Mandarin Chinese Beijing Dialect
门 mén (door) 门儿 mén’er (door)
公园 gōngyuán (park) 公园儿 gōngyuán’er (park)
