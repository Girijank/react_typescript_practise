Stop, Collaborate, and Learn

There is a famous Chinese saying, 三人行，必有我师 Sānrén xíng, bì yǒu wǒ shī. (When three are walking together, I am sure to find teachers among them.) There is always someone in a group that you can learn from. Collaborating with others is a great way to learn from each other. For this collaboration, you’ll have the chance to collaborate with your classmates, using a method that is suitable for you. You will surely learn a lot from others and be the teacher of others in your group.

This collaboration has three parts:

Choose Your Collaboration

    “Introduction Interview” Collaboration Rubric

    Performance Level Excellent Good Needs Improvement

    Content

    12-10 Points

    The work contains all of the required elements:

    • time-appropriate greeting
    • three questions asked to partner using:
      • 什么 shénme (what)
      • 哪里 nǎlǐ(where)
      • 多少 duōshǎo (how many)
      • 多大 duōdà (how old)
      • 哪一年 nǎyīnián (which year)
      • 什么样 shénme yàng (like what)
    • three answers appropriate to the questions asked from partner using:
      • 叫 jiào (to be called) and/or 姓 xìng (to be last named)
      • 我 wǒ (I)

Find a Collaboration Partner

Find a partner to work with. Contact your instructor for assistance with finding a partner. Once you have found someone to work with, you and your partner will communicate back and forth to complete the collaboration task.

Be sure to meet all the requirements in the applicable collaboration rubric.

Submit Your Collaboration

When you’ve completed parts one and two, you’ll need to complete the Collaboration Reflection and turn it in, along with your completed collaboration project. The Collaboration Reflection is the evidence of your collaboration, along with a peer and self-evaluation.

Be sure to include all required information listed on the applicable collaboration rubric.

Assessment

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Carefully review the directions and grading rubric for your chosen collaboration product before starting work.
  3. Submit your Collaboration Reflection document and your collaboration product to Segment One Collaboration.
