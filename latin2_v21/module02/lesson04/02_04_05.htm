"The Moon is Down" (Part I)

We hope you are having fun reading about great wars in which Rome had fought. This story will give you a glimpse about Hannibal, the greatest foe Rome had ever known. Get ready, soon, you’ll officially meet him!

Media nocte, finitum est. Oppidum nostrum occupatum est, milites nostri superati sunt, bellum nostrum finitum est. Luna occidit. Sum femina Galla. Sum vidua (widow). Numquam legere et scribere cognovi, sed pontifices Galli, Druides, omnia verba mea lingua Celtica describunt. Liberi mei et liberi liberorum meorum et liberi eorum de proelio inter Hannibalem et Romanos cognoscent.

By the middle of the night, it was finished. Our town is occupied, our soldiers were defeated, our war was done. The moon has fallen. I am a Gallic woman. I am a widow. I never learned to read and write, but the Gallic priests, the Druids, are writing all my words in the Celtic language. My children and my children’s children and their children will learn about the battle between Hannibal and the Romans.
