The Etruscan Wars

The Romans were eager to conquer more regions and cities throughout Italy. Yet, the Etruscans, whose kings they had expelled out of Rome, strove to keep their independence and civilization free from Roman rule.

In the midst of the Latin Wars, Rome was also fighting in the Etruscan Wars. The Etruscan city of Veii fought three wars with Rome beginning in 483 BCE, including the Veientine War of 437-435 BCE in which Lars Tolumnius, King of Veii, was killed, securing Fidenae for Rome. But after the Gauls had attacked Rome in 390 BCE at the river Allia, half of the Roman army fled to Veii for protection.

The Romans also sent Vestal priestesses and sacred objects to the Etruscan city Cerveteri, creating a mutual granting of hospitium publicum by the Romans and Caeretans which lasted until 396 BCE. Rome looked at Cerveteri as the center of civilization, even sending their young aristocrats there to learn Etruscan literature. Despite this temporary alliance, Rome continued its campaigns against Etruria. Between 358 and 351 BCE, Rome and the Etruscan city of Tarquinia were at war.
