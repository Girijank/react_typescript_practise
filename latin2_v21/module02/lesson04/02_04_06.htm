Relative Pronoun Practice

It’s time to chew on relative pronoun sentences. Take your time, diagram them accordingly, and then translate them into great English. Practice each sentence a few times.

Apollo, ex quo Mercurius bovem rapuit, ad speluncam Mercurii venit.

Apollo, from whom Mercury stole cattle, came to Mercury’s cave.

Illa ovis aquam devorat, quae e flumine excedit.

That sheep gobbles up the water, which flows out of the river.

Patricii ad illos locos redierant, in quibus tempus certum steterat .

The patricians had returned to those places, in which time had stood fixed.

Hic sus, cuius corpus ligatum est, illam herbam non edit.

This pig, whose body was bound, did not eat that grass.

Illa vulnera in capite regis, quae a pila piloque creata erant, tempore curata sunt.

Those wounds on the head of the king, which had been created by a ball and a stick, were healed by time.

Poetae, quibus vinum dabatur, multa carmina scribent.

The poets, to whom wine was being given, will write many songs.

Imperium regiae, quam rex non habet, illis incolis impedimentum est.

The command of the palace, which the king does not possess, is a hindrance to those inhabitants.

Femina Galla, cuius coniunx mortuus est, legere aut scribere non poterat.

The Gallic woman, whose husband was dead, was not able to read or write.

Primae leges Romae, quam decemviri creaverant, multos annos stetit.

The first laws of Rome, which the decemviri had created, stood up for many years.

Rex, cuius pedes sunt magni, reginam, quacum intra regiam ambulat, amat.

The king, whose feet are large, loves the queen, with whom he walks inside of the palace.
