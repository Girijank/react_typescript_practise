Education in the Roman Republic (509 BCE–27 BCE)

Do you ever wonder what type of Latin class you would have been in if you were living in Rome during the Roman Republic? Just as you take English classes, Roman schoolchildren took Latin classes! Let’s learn more about how the educational system operated in Rome.

In ancient Rome, the education system looked very different from what we have today. Although the Greeks had a structured, formal education system well before the Romans, ancient Rome formed no such system until around the 3rd century BCE. Just like many other facets of Roman daily life, education in Rome, especially during the time of the Republic and before, centered around the home and an individual's duty to their family. This education, led by the paterfamilias, was just as much a moral education as an academic one.

At this time in Roman history, children learned some reading, writing, and enough mathematics to enable them to understand simple business transactions and to count, weigh, and measure. Most learning centered around domestic, military, and agricultural skills, all taught by a child's first teachers —their parents.
