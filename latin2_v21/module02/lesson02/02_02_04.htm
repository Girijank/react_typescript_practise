Translation of Hic,Haec,Hoc | Ille,Illa,Illud

“Hunc ego institutum disciplina mea vicarium
pro me rei publicae do dicoque.” (Livy, Ab Urbe Condita, Book 5, Ch. 18)

“I give and dedicate this young man who has been trained under my tutelage as my substitute for the republic.”


Translation

hic, haec, hoc: refers to something that is near to the speaker

  • Singular = this (near me)
  • Plural = these (near me)

Translation

ille, illa, illud: refers to something that is far away from the speaker

  • Singular = that (over there)
  • Plural = those (over there)

Word Usage

Adjective Form: The adjective will agree with the noun that it describes in case, number, and gender.

Pronoun Form: Hic and Ille are sometimes translated as the third person pronouns:

  • He (this/that one — masculine, singular)
  • She (this/that one — feminine, singular)
  • It (this/that one — neuter, singular)
  • They (these/those ones — masculine, feminine, neuter, plural)
  • Pronouns will agree with their antecedent in number and gender. The case of a pronoun is determined by its use in the sentence.
    • Example: Marcum in Foro vidi. Hic cum Lucio erat. (I saw Marcus in the Forum. He was with Lucius)
    • In the second sentence above, the antecedent for hic is Marcus.The translation he makes more sense in this sentence than the translation this.

Difference between is, hic, ille in a Sentence:

  • Hunc saccum habes? Do you have this sack? (hunc saccum = refers to this sack right here next to you)
  • Illum saccum habes? Do you have that sack? (illum saccum = refers to that sack over there)
  • Eum saccum habes? Do you have this (that) sack? (eum saccum = no emphasis on what sack it is)

Mercury's Memo: Alternate Translations

When you see a sentence with ille…hic in a compound sentence, translate hic as “the latter” and ille as “the former.”

Example: Vir feminam amat.Ille est rex; haec est regina. The man loves the woman. The former is a king; the latter is a queen. (Ille = Nominative sg. m.; haec = Nominative sg. f.)

The demonstrative pronoun ille can also be translated as “well-known” or “famous.”
