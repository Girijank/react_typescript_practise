Fabula Fabulosa: Mercury Steals Apollo’s Cattle

It’s me, Mercury! Let’s listen in to another one of my famous tricks!

After creating the lyre, tum ego ad urbem Thessalius cucurri. I was still a baby and up to no good. I saw a herd of cattle grazing on the mountains and thought up a devious plan. I wanted to steal the herd! However, the cattle belonged to my half-brother Apollo, and he would not be happy at all with me stealing them from him. So, here’s what I did: I decided to steal only 50 cattle and leave no trace of my steps. Quomodo ego hoc feci? I fashioned a pair of sandals that I used to fly above the ground so there would be no footsteps, and then I had the cattle walk backwards so Apollo would think that the cattle had not left the mountain.Hoc erat magnum furtim. I was so clever! Once I had driven the cattle off the mountains, I sacrificed two of the cattle to my fellow gods on Mt. Olympus. To do this, I found sticks and kindling and rubbed them together to make fire! I was such a smart baby
