Positions of the Cursus Honorum

Highest Rank

Consul: the highest position in Rome. He commanded the army, dealt with foreign affairs, and presided over the Senate and assemblies.

Senator: member of the Roman ruling class who governed the assemblies and controlled the spending of money for the state

Praetor: served as a judicial officer with civic and provincial jurisdiction

Quaestor: financial administrator

Aedile: urban maintenance such as roads, food and water supplies, festivals

Censor: responsible for the census, elected every five years for a year and a half period

Tribune: office to protect the rights of the plebian class and had to be from this class

Plebs: largest social class in ancient Rome and also the poorest. They did not have voting rights.
