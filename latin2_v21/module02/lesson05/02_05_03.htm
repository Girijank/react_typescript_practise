What to include in your Republic Society Project Hint
Step 1: Choose a place in Roman Republic society. Remember:
The lower you start out on the societal ladder, the more you must do to climb higher!
Step 2: Research: Per the class position you chose, research and write about your role and responsibilities in the Roman Republic. Remember:
For your approximately 150-word paragraph:
  • Include the name of your position in your research paragraph.
  • Your information must be in complete English sentences.
  • Use academic sources and cite them in your Works Cited.
Step 3: Latin Composition: Write three sentences in Latin about yourself and your life goals in the Roman Republic. Remember:
You will be writing three sentences in Latin. At least one must include a relative clause. Your sentences should be simple, using only the vocabulary and grammar you have learned up to this point in the course.
➤ Latin sentence 1

What position in Roman society do you currently hold?

(Ex. I am a senator who lives in Etruria.)

 Remember:

For your sentence which includes a relative clause, make sure to:

  • Have an independent clause and a relative clause sentence.
  • Have your relative pronoun match in gender and number with its antecedent.
➤ Latin sentence 2

What is your main role in Roman society?

(Ex. As senator, I give advice to the consul.)

 Remember:

If using “ut” in your description, remember this means “as” and serves as a connecting word in a sentence.

➤ Latin sentence 3

What is your #lifegoal in the Roman Republic?

(Ex. In Rome, I wish to be a Roman consul.)

 Remember:

If using “cupio” in your sentence, remember that it takes a complementary infinitive to complete its action.

Step 4: Right and Wrong Moves: What can you tell your fellow Romans to do (and not do) to advance themselves up the cursus honorum? Remember:
  • In English, fill out the chart with three right moves and three wrong moves.
  • To make your advice memorable, be sure to include an image for one right move and one wrong move.
Nota bene: Did you proofread your work and cite your sources? Remember:

Every sentence should use correct punctuation, grammar, and syntax. All research sources should be cited using MLA format.
