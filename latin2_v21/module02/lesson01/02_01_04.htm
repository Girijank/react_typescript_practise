Fabula Fabulosa: Mercury’s Tortoise

I have a message for you… it’s about me! I’m going to tell you about some of my most mischievous tales from my childhood. Why? Because I’m so proud of my clever ways. A warning to you—I was a tricky little one!

I am the inventor of the string musical instrument, the lyre. As a baby, I was so bored in my crib that I crawled out of it and began to roam around in the shadowy cave on Mount Cyllene to which I was born. I saw a tortoise with a speckled shell passing by, and ego id cepi! I then hollowed out its shell, grabbed some reeds, and extended the reeds across the shell. Then, I stretched an oxhide over the shell and made a bridge and two arms. Mihi nomen est Mercurius, et ego sum saevus! Almost complete—I made strings out of sheep gut for my new instrument to finish inventing the first-ever lyre. How clever am I!
