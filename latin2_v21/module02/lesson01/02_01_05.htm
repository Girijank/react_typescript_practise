Intensive Pronoun/Adjective: Idem, Eadem, Idem

Ha! I tricked you! You may have thought there would be no grammar introduced here, but here it is! Just keeping you on your toes…

Before we talk about the Intensive Noun/Adjective/Pronoun, idem, eadem, idem, let’s first review the definitions of a pronoun and adjective. Also, be sure to review the Noun/Adjective/Pronoun Guide to learn how we utilize our pronoun and adjective specific terms in Latin.

Remember,is, ea, id, our demonstrative pronouns used for "he, she, it."

The Intensive Pronoun/Adjective idem, eadem, idem, meaning the same, is a compound of the Demonstrative Pronoun is, ea, id, and -dem, with slight changes for pronunciation.

Idem, Eadem, Idem

If you have memorized the demonstrative is, ea, id, you already know idem. Just cover up the -dem and see the similarities. Here’s a formula to help you use this pattern successfully:

is, ea, id (declined forms) + -dem

There are slight exceptions to the formation formula.

  • Nominative singular forms will be: idem, eadem, idem.
  • Accusative singular m. and f. forms are spelled a bit differently than the “is, ea, id” Accusative singular forms. The forms will be eundem and eandem.
  • Genitive plural forms are spelled a bit differently than the “is, ea, id” Genitive plural forms. The forms are eorundem, earundem, eorundem. (You might have thought it should be eorumdem, earumdem, eorumdem.)
  • Adjective form: It will operate as an adjective when paired with a noun. idem, eadem, idem is normally written before the noun it modifies.
    • Translate idem, eadem, idem as “the same (noun).”
  • Pronoun form: It will operate as a pronoun when it stands alone in a sentence. It can also stand alone as a pronoun.
    • Translate idem, eadem, idem as “the same (man, woman, thing)” according to its gender.

Sentence Usage:

  1. Idem vir agros coluit. = The same man tilled the fields.
  2. Easdem puellas spectabamus. = We saw the same girls.
  3. Eadem templa sunt pulchra. = The same temples are beautiful.
  4. Eae feminae eis viris eosdem libros dederat. = Those women had given the same books to those men.
  5. Caper eius eaedem herbae impedimentum est. = His goat is a hindrance to that same grass.
  6. Tuus oculus eadem in herba vidit. = Your eye saw those same things in the grass.
