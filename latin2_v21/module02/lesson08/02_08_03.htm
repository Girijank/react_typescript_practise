Reflexive, Possessive Adjectives

You have already learned about possessive adjectives, which give emphasis to whom a person or object belongs. Remember, they are:

  • meus, mea, meum = my
    • Example: Avus meus pilam iactat. = My own grandfather is throwing the ball.
  • noster, nostra, nostrum = our
    • Example: Suis familiae nostrae est giganta. = The pig of our own family is gigantic.
  • tuus, tua, tuum = your
    • Example: Dolorum tuorum time! = Be fearful of your own tricks!!
  • vester, vestra, vestrum = your
    • Example: Imperia vestra frangere cupitis. = You want to break your own commands.

Oh, but I am tricking you because if you want to use a 3rd person possessive adjective in English, it always has a reflexive meaning.

3rd Person Possessive, Reflexive Adjectives form

suus, sua, suum = his/her/its/their own

  • Both the singular and plural use the same form. The possessive reflexive adjective will agree with its noun in case, number, and gender.
  • In translation, the gender of the possessive reflexive adjective will agree with the subject.

Sentence Examples

  • Femina casam suam videt. The woman sees her own house.
  • Vir casam suam videt. The man sees his own house.
  • Corpus casam suam videt. The body sees its own house.

Ad astra per sententias!

Identify the Latin reflexive possessive adjective(s) within each sentence. Then, translate the sentence into English.

Iter nostrum cupiditatibus suis mutatae erant.

nostrum; suis. Our own journey had been changed by its own desires.

Hostis hominibus suis se lavare persuasit.

suis. The enemy persuaded his own men to bathe themselves.

Senatores civitatem meam ruperunt.

meam. The senators have broken my own city-state.

Corpora sua hastis vulnerantur.

sua. Their own bodies are being wounded by the spears.
