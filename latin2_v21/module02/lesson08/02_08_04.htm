Ante incursionem crudelem Hannibalis, nos, Insubres et proximi, Boii, in Romanos iterum et iterum pugnabant. Primum, Romani legatos suos pro ducibus nostris permutare persuaserunt. Nos, Insubres, cupiditatem dementem non tenuimus. Tum, ubi Romani Gallos alios oppugnabat, se in nos verterunt (turned) et nos faciliter vicerunt. Tandem, Hannibal et Poeni trans montes, Alpes, ascendebant et descendebant. Cupiebamus iuvare Hannibalem, maximum principem, vincere Romanos, hostes communes.

Before the invasion of Hannibal, we, the Insubres, and our neighbors, the Boii, fought again and again against the Romans. At first, the Romans promised to exchange their own envoys for our leaders. We, the Insubres, did not have this crazy desire. Then, when the Romans were attacking other Gauls, they turned themselves against us also and easily conquered us. At last, Hannibal and the Carthaginians climbed up and climbed down across the mountains, the Alps. We wanted to help Hannibal, the greatest leader, to defeat the Romans, our common enemy.
