"The Moon is Down" (Part III)

You’ve met Hannibal! Watch out, Rome is in trouble! Let’s continue to learn more about Hannibal from the continuation of our story.

Nostri milites socios Romanorum in Italia oppugnabant. Ubi Hannibal in Italiam veniebat, laudabamus Poenos et nos, omnes socii Hannibalis, Romanos oppugnabamus. Dum maritus meus et milites ipsi circum Romanos in Trebia hieme plicabant, ego et uxores in casis cum liberis in Gallia Cisalpina laborabant. Dum coniunx meus et milites illi maximum numerum militum Romanorum proper lacum Trasumnnum aestate interficiebant, ego et uxores frumenta in agris Cisalpinis colebant.

Our soldiers attacked the allies of the Romans in Italy. When Hannibal came into Italy, we praised the Carthaginians and we, all the allies of Hannibal, kept attacking the Romans. While my husband and the other soldiers were surrounding the Romans in Trebia in the winter, the wives and I were working in the houses with our children in Cisalpine Gaul. While my husband and those soldiers were killing a very large number of Roman soldiers near Lake Trasimene in the summer, the wives and I were tending to the crops in the Cisalpine fields.

Dum Hannibal et coniunx meus et milites illi Fabium Cunctatorem et Romanos petebant, ego et uxores in agris Cisalpinis laborabant. Deinde Hannibal et socii eius Romanos prope Cannas maxime pepulerunt. Populi in Italia, in Sicilia, in Gallia se ad Hannibalem iungebant. Hannibal et socii eius Romanos et socios eorum multos annos terrebant.

While Hannibal and my husband and those soldiers were seeking Fabius the Delayer and the Romans, the wives and I were working in the Cisalpine fields. Then Hannibal and his allies greatly defeated the Romans near Cannae. People in Italy, in Sicily, and in Gaul joined themselves to Hannibal. Hannibal and his allies scared the Romans and their allies for many years.
