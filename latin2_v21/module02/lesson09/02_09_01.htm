Carthage: The Barca Family

The Barca family was the ruling family of noble rank who led the Carthaginians during the Punic Wars. They were savvy, wise, tactfully fierce leaders who expanded the Carthaginian empire throughout Hispania and Italia. Together, this family posed the most serious threat to Rome’s power that the Romans had ever seen.

  • Hamilcar Barca (275 BCE–228 BCE):This patriarch had a deep-seated hatred for the Romans and wanted to ensure that Carthage remained more powerful than Rome. After the First Punic War, he led Carthaginian troops into Hispania to gain control of the region.
  • Hasdrubal the Fair (270 BCE–221 BCE): Hamilcar’s son-in-law. He extended Carthaginian command in Hispania and claimed a port city of Hispania as “Carthago Nova.” There, he signed the Treaty of Embro.
  • Hannibal (247 BCE–182 BCE):The eldest and most famous son of Hamilcar. Hannibal was a military genius and mastermind who led Carthage to multiple victories against the Romans.
  • Mago (243 BCE–203 BCE):Hamilcar’s third son. He fought strategically alongside his brothers at almost every battle.

Rome: The Scipio Family

At the same time as the rise of the Barca family in Carthage, the elite Roman Scipio family was serving as consuls and commanders during the Punic Wars for the city of Rome.

  • Publius Cornelius Scipio (?–211 BCE) was a Roman consul during the Second Punic War.
  • His brother Calvus and his son, Publius Cornelius Scipio Africanus (235 BCE–183 BCE), the superb military commander, led Rome to victory in the Second Punic War.
  • Scipio Aemilianus (185 BCE–129 BCE), an adopted grandson of Scipio Africanus, would later win victory for Rome in the Third Punic War.

The Rise of Hannibal, Carthaginian Leader:

Hannibal is known as one of the greatest leaders and military commanders the world has ever known. At an early age, his father, Hamilcar, took Hannibal to an altar in Hispania and made Hannibal swear never to be a friend to Rome. For his entire life, he carried this hatred towards the Romans. In Hispania, he learned battle techniques and war tactics and quickly became known for his brilliance and military aptitude. After the assassination of his brother Hasdrubal in 221 BCE, the Carthaginians immediately elected Hannibal as their commander at the age of 25. Hannibal became Rome’s greatest enemy, and he nearly destroyed the Roman Republic in the Second Punic War.

The Rise of Scipio Africanus, Roman Leader:

Although only in his twenties when he assumed command of the Roman army in the Second Punic War, his achievements, wisdom, and tactical warfare planning measured far beyond his years. It was his persuasive oratory skills combined with his almost “prophetic” foresight, steadfast determinism, and belief in self and Roman mores which helped in his pursuit of victory. It also seemed as though Scipio structured his command from the “book of Hannibal,” wherein his battle and political moves mirrored those of Hannibal early in the Second Punic War.

Cogitavisti? Did You Know

  • Carthago Nova= New Carthage. The Barca family had secured so much territory and so many allies in Hispania that they created a city called Carthago Nova in Hispania, which is now the Spanish city of Cartagena.
  • The Spanish city of Barcelona derives its name from the Barca family name.
  • The Spanish soccer team FC Barcelona is colloquially known as "Barca".
