Let’s see how the reflexive pronouns look in Latin. The most commonly used forms are in the Accusative case. Take some time to reflect on it!

Reflexive Pronoun
Person Example English Translation Person Example English Translation
First sing. me vidi I saw myself First pl. nos vidimus We saw ourselves
Second sing. te vidisti You saw yourself Second pl. vos vidistis You saw yourselves
Third sing. se vidit He saw himself
She saw herself
It saw itself 		Third pl. se viderunt They saw themselves

Usage in Latin sentence:

  1. Me lavo: I wash myself.
  2. Te doces: You teach yourself.
  3. Ea se vulnerat: She wounds herself.
  4. Nos lavamus: We wash ourselves.
  5. Vos docetis: You teach yourselves.
  6. Eae se vulnerant: These women wound themselves.

Nota Bene:

All of the reflexive pronouns in these sentences are in the Accusative Case.
