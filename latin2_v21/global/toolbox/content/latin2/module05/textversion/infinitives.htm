Infinitives—Text Version

What is an Infinitive?

Announcer: An infinitive is a verbal noun that takes the place of a noun in a sentence. It doesn't have a subject, and it doesn't have different endings to indicate person. In translations, the word "to" is used to indicate an infinitive form.

Complementary Infinitives

The present Active infinitive form is the 2nd principal part of a verb, usually ending in "-re."

salto, saltare, saltavi, saltatus, -a, -um, the 2nd principal part is "saltare." [On screen: the term "saltare" is circled in the group of words "salto, saltare, saltavi, saltatus, -a, -um".]

Translated as a present active infinitive, this means "to dance." [On screen: saltare = to dance]

A complementary infinitive is an infinitive being used as the direct object of another verb.

Look at the sentence: "Puella saltare amat."(The girl loves to dance.)

The infinitive "saltare" means "to dance" and follows the main verb in the English translation. [On screen: "saltare" is circled in the sentence "Puella saltare amat." "To dance" is circled in the sentence "The girl loves to dance."]

Infinitives with Possum

When the main verb of a Latin sentence is a form of "possum" (Latin for "am able"), you will almost always see an infinitive acting as its direct object.

In the sentence "Puella saltare potest.", the infinitive "saltare" is being used to show that the girl is able to dance. [On screen: "saltare" is circled in the sentence "Puella saltare potest." "To dance" is circled in the sentence "The girl is able to dance."]

Infinitives with Sum

While infinitives are used as verbal nouns, they are not always the direct object. In a sentence where the main Latin verb is a form of "sum" (to be), the infinitive often behaves differently.

In the sentence "Saltare est vivere."—"To dance is to live."—the infinitive is the subject, and the 2nd infinitive "vivere" is called the predicate nominative. [On screen: "Saltare" and "vivere" are circled in the sentence "Saltare est vivere." "To dance" and "to live" is circled in the sentence "To dance is to live."]
