1st Declension Case Endings

1st Declension
M. & F.		 Case Uses
sing. pl.
Nominative -a -ae subject, predicate Nominative, predicate adjective
Genitive -ae -arum possessive, of..., 's, ...s'
Dative -ae -is indirect object (to whom, for whom)
Accusative -am -as direct object, object of certain prepositions
Ablative -a -is by means, object of certain prepositions

Declining a 1st Declension Noun

1st Declension

femina, feminae f. woman
Singular English translation Plural English translation
Nominative femina the woman feminae the women
Genitive feminae of the woman feminarum of the woman
Dative feminae to/for the woman feminis to/for the woman
Accusative feminam the woman feminas the women
Ablative femina by/with the woman feminis by/with the woman

Nota bene: Although most 1st declension nouns are feminine, there are a few masculine forms. They are declined the same way, but you will want to pay close attention to gender when it comes to adjective and participle agreement.

1st Declension

agricola, agricolae m. farmer
Singular English translation Plural English translation
Nominative agricola the farmer agricolae the farmers
Genitive agricolae of the farmer agricolarum of the farmers
Dative agricolae to/for the farmer agricolis to/for the farmers
Accusative agricolam the farmer agricolas the farmers
Ablative agricola by/with the farmer agricolis by/with the farmers

Sentence Examples:

  1. Agricola feminam amat. The farmer loves the woman.
  2. Puellae ad portam ambulant. The girls walk towards the door.
  3. Feminae gemmas tenent. The women hold the gems.
