3rd Declension Nouns: Genitive singular ends in –is.

Vocabulary Examples:

  • lex, legis, f.—law
  • dux, ducis, m.—leader
  • frater, fratres, m.—brother
  • caput, capitis, n.—head

Nota bene: Remember, when declining any noun, the stem is found by removing the ending from the Genitive form. In some declensions, the Nominative form will look different from the rest of the forms when declined. This is the case for 3rd Declension nouns that end in "r."
