Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns are used in Latin to indicate the use of the first person (I/we/us/me) or second person (you/you all/y'all) in a sentence. The demonstrative pronoun is/ea/is is also considered a personal pronoun, as it can be used to show he/she/them/her/they in a sentence.

1st Person

Singular     Plural  
Nominative ego I nos we
Genitive mei of me nostrum/ nostri of us
Dative mihi to/for me nobis to/for us
Accusative me me nos us
Ablative me by/with me nobis by/with us

2nd Person

Singular     Plural  
Nominative tu you vos you all
Genitive tui of you vestrum/ vestri of you all
Dative tibi to/for you vobis to/for you all
Accusative te you vos you all
Ablative te by/with you vobis by/with you all

Sentence Examples:

  • Mihi librum date! Give the book to me!
  • Puellae nobiscum ad campum ambulabant. The girls were walking with us to the field.
  • Romani te laudant. The Romans praise you.
  • Vos multos canes habetis. You all have many dogs.
  • Is feminam amat. He loves the woman.
  • Eae statuas amant. They love the statues.
