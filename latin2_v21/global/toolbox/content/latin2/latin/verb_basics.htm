Verb Basics

Verb Terms:
Conjugation Latin divides all the verbs into four groups or families, called conjugations. The infinitive ending determines the conjugation.
Tense

Latin, like English, has six tenses that show the time an action takes place:

Present

Imperfect (simple past)

Future

Perfect

Pluperfect

Future Perfect
Person

1st person = I or we

2nd person = you singular, you plural

3rd person = he/she/it or they
Number Singular or Plural
Voice

Like English, Latin has two voices:

Active -- the subject is the doer of the action

Passive -- the subject received the action
Mood

There are three moods in Latin:

Indicative -- statements and simple questions

Imperative -- commands

Subjunctive -- wishes, unreal situations, etc.

Verb Stems

Dictionary Entry for Verbs consists of the four principal parts and the meaning.

Examples: porto, portare, portavi, portatus,-a,-um = to carry

  1. porto (1st person singular, present tense)
  2. portare (infinitive)
  3. portavi (1st person singular, perfect tense)
  4. portatus,-a,-um (perfect passive participle)
  5. to carry (English meaning)

moneo, monere, monui, monitus,-a,-um = to warn

dico, dicere, dixi, dictus,-a,-um,-a,-um = to say

sentio, sentire, sensi, sensus,-a,-um = to feel

Stems Formula Example
Present

= 2nd principal part (infinitive) minus -RE

(exception: 3rd conjugation drop the -ERE) and

3rd conjugation present tense add -is, -imus, -itis, -unt

3rd conjugation imperfect tense add -ebam, -ebas, -ebat, etc.

3rd conjugation future add -am, -es, -et, -emus, -etis, -ent

N.B. For imperfect and future tenses of 3rd -IO and 4th conjugation verbs there is an -ie- in the stem for most forms. Examples: capiebat, audiemus

 porta-
Perfect = 3rd principal part minus -i portavi-
Participial = 4th principal part minus -us, -a, -um portat-
