Nouns: 3rd Declension i-stem Nouns

Dictionary Entry for a noun = Nominative singular form, Genitive singular, gender

  • civis, civis, m./f.–citizen

3rd Declension Nouns:

  • Genitive singular ends in –is.
  • Certain nouns are "i-stem" based upon their Nominative and Genitive forms.

Identifying Masculine/Feminine I-stems

  1. Mirror Effect: Nominative singular and Genitive singular are mirror images.
    Example: navis, navis, f.—ship
  2. Nominative ends in -x or -s, and the Genitive stem ends in a double consonant.
    Example: pars, partis, f.—part, share; arx, arcis, f.—citadel
  3. Vocabulary Examples:
    • arx, arcis, f.—citadel
    • mare, maris, n.—sea

Identifying Neuter I-stems

  • Nominative ends in -al, -e, -r.
  • Vocabulary Examples:
    • animal, animalis, n.—animal
    • mare, maris, n.—sea
    • exemplar, exemplaris, —n. example

Nota bene: Neuter I-stems use the Nominative/Accusative plural ending -ia; Ablative singular can end in -I; and Genitive plural ends in -ium.
