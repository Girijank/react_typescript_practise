Nouns: 2nd Declension Nouns

Dictionary Entry for a noun = Nominative singular form, Genitive singular, gender

  • murus, muri, m.—wall
  • templum, templi, n.—temple

2nd Declension nouns:

  • have Genitive singular form that ends in –i
  • are in the masculine or neuter genders

Vocabulary Examples:

  • amicus, amici, m.—friend
  • vir, viri, m.—man
  • bellum, belli. n.—war

Declining 2nd Declension nouns

2nd Declension
amicus, amici, m. friend
singular English translation plural English translation
Nominative amicus the friend amici the friends
Genitive amici of the friend amicorum of the friends
Dative amico to/for the friend amicis to/for the friends
Accusative amicum the friend amicos the friends
Ablative amico by/with the friend amicis by/with the friends

Nota bene: Remember, when declining any noun, the stem is found by removing the ending in from the Genitive form. In some declensions, the Nominative form will look different from the rest of the forms when declined. This is the case for 2nd Declension nouns that end in "r."

2nd Declension
ager, agri, m. field
singular English translation plural English translation
Nominative ager the field agri the fields
Genitive agri of the field agrorum of the fields
Dative agro to/for the field agris to/for the fields
Accusative agrum the field agros the fields
Ablative agro by/with the field agris by/with the fields

Nota bene: Make sure you identify the gender of your 2nd Declension nouns before declining. The Nominative and Accusative forms differ between masculine and neuter forms.
