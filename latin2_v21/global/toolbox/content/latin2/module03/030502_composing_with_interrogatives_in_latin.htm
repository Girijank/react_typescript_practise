Composing with Interrogative Pronouns/Adjectives

Interrogative Pronouns

How to compose in Latin?

  • Write the question in English.
  • Identify the Interrogative pronoun and write its case, number, and gender.
    • Write the interrogative pronoun at the beginning of the question.
      Compose the remaining parts of your question according to your normal sentence composing rules.
      Example: What is the time? = Quid est tempus?

Interrogative Adjectives

How to compose in Latin?

  1. Write the question in English.
  2. Identify the Interrogative adjective and the noun it is modifying.
    • Then, identify the case, number, and gender of the adjective-noun pair.
  3. In Latin, write the interrogative adjective-noun pair at the beginning of the question.

    4. Compose the remaining parts of your question according to your normal sentence composing rules.
    Example: Quem equum vir pueris dabit? What horse will the man give to the boys?

Nota Bene: Sentence structure in Latin is flexible, but you can follow a general layout: QUESTION WORD - Subject - indirect object (if there is one) -- direct object -- prepositional phrase(s) -- infinitive (if there is one) -- main verb.
