Present System Passive Voice, 3rd/4th Conjugation

Passive Voice – the subject is being acted upon

Present System – verbs in the Present, Imperfect, or Future tenses

Forming the Present System, Passive voice for 3rd and 4th conjugation verbs

In Latin, the passive voice of all conjugations is formed by adding the passive personal endings to the present stem.

  • Present Passive = present stem (infinitive -re) + passive personal endings
  • Imperfect Passive = present stem + (-ba-) + passive personal endings
  • Future Passive = present stem + (-e-) for 3rd conjugation OR (-ie-) for 3rd -io/4th conjugations) + passive personal endings

Sentence Examples:

  • Pueri a consulibus in proelio pugnare mittuntur. The boys are being sent by the consuls to fight in the battle.
  • In bello Troiano, equus in dolum facietur. In the Trojan War, the horse will be made into a trick.
  • A populis in oppido audiebaris. You were being heard by the people in the town.
