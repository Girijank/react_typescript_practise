Announcer: Today, you are going to learn about the irregular verbs "sum" and "possum." Let's begin.

Irregular Verbs: Sum and Possum

The verbs "sum" and "possum" are irregular verbs. When forming these verbs, they do not follow the same pattern as a regular Latin verb.

[On screen: sum, esse, fui, futurus, -a, -um = to be; possum, posse, potui- = to be able]

Instead, they conjugate to the beat of a different drum.

Take a look at the sentences. Poeta sum. (I am a poet.) and Poeta non es. (You are not a poet.)

Notice that "sum" and "es" both mean "to be," using different subjects, but the forms look completely different. [On screen: "sum" is circled in the sentence "Poeta sum." and "I am" is circled in the sentence "I am a poet." "es" is circled in the sentence "Poeta non es." and "you are" is circled in the sentence "You are not a poet."]

[On screen: A table is displayed where "sum" is circled in the 1st person singular.]

  Singular Plural
1st Person sum
I am		 sumus
we are
2nd Person es
you are		 estis
you all are
3rd Person est
he/she/it is		 sunt
they are

The forms of "sum" in the present tense do not form any particular pattern. Instead, they need to be memorized.

Once you have your present tense of "sum" memorized, you are ready to learn the verb "possum."

Take a look at these sentences: Saltare possum. (I am able to dance.) and Saltare non potes. (You are not able to dance.)

Can you spot the forms of "sum" in the verbs of these sentences?

[On screen: A table is displayed where the first part of the word "pos" is underlined and the second part of the words are circled—"sum," "sumus" and "sunt." in "possum," "possumus" and possunt." The first part of the word, the "pot" is underlined and the second part of the words are circled - "es," "est" and "estis." in "potes," "potest" and "potestis."]

  Singular Plural
1st Person possum
I am able		 possumus
we are able
2nd Person potes
you are able		 potestis
you all are able
3rd Person potest
he/she/it is able		 possunt
they are able

When forming "possum" in the present tense, all that is needed is an additional "pos" in front of "sum," "sumus," or "sunt" and "pot" in front of "es, est, or estis."

Have these forms memorized, and any time you encounter "sum" or "possum" in your translations, you will know what to do with these irregular verb friends!
