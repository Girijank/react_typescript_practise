Present System Passive Voice—Text Version

Announcer: Let's talk about the Present System Passive Voice.

In the Passive Voice, the subject receives the action of the verb, rather than completing the action. The present system Passive does so in three tenses: present, imperfect, and future.

[On screen: Aedifico, aedificare, aedificavi, aedificatus, -a, -um = to build]

The first piece of the present system, Passive Voice puzzle is your present stem—the 2nd principal part, minus the -re. [On screen: Puzzle piece appears with "aedificare" and then transitions to remove "re". Final word on puzzle piece "aedifica".]

[On screen: Table displays with "-tur" is circled.]

  Singular Plural
1st Person -r -mur
2nd Person -ris -mini
3rd Person -tur -ntur

The next piece of the puzzle is the Passive personal endings (-r, -ris, -tur, -mur, -mini, -ntur).

[On screen: Puzzle piece appears with "aedfica," a second puzzle piece appears with "tur" on it and pieces join together.] Added to your present stem, this piece forms a present tense, Passive Voice verb. [On screen: puzzle piece appears with "aedificatur."] Remember, in the Passive Voice, the subject receives the action of the verb.

Present Tense, Passive Voice

"Villa ab agricola aedificatur." (The farmhouse is being built by the farmer.)

We use the "-tur" ending to indicate present tense, 3rd person, singular because the house is being built. The house is receiving the action. [On screen: "tur" is circled in the sentence "Villa ab agricola aedificatur." "Is being built" is circled in the sentence "The farmhouse is being built by the farmer."]

The Imperfect Tense, Passive Voice

The imperfect tense, Passive Voice uses the same pieces. [On screen: "Villa ab agricola aedificabatur" (The farmhouse was being built by the farmer.)]

The present stem and Passive Voice personal endings, with a new piece in between—the imperfect "ba". [On screen text: "ba" is circled in the sentence "Villa ab agricola aedificabatur."]

Aedificatur becomes aedificabatur—the farmhouse was being built by the farmer. [On screen text: "Was being built" is circled in the sentence "The farmhouse was being built by the farmer."]

Future Tense, Passive Voice

In the 1st and 2nd conjugation, the future tense Passive Voice uses bor, beris, bitur, bimur, bimini and buntur to indicate "will be," like in the sentence "Villa ab agricola aedificabitur." (The farmhouse will be built by the farmer.) [On screen text: "bi" is circled in the sentence "Villa ab agricola aedificabitur." "Will be built" is circled in the sentence "The farmhouse will be built by the farmer."]

The 3rd and 4th conjugations use the same "ie" piece as the future Active Voice, with Passive Voice personal endings added on, like in "Villa ab agricola munietur." (The farmhouse will be fortified by the farmer.) [On screen text: "ie" is circled in the sentence "Villa ab agricola munietur." "will be fortified" is circled in the sentence "The farmhouse will be fortified by the farmer."]

In all of the examples, no matter how the tense of the verb endings changes, just remember that the subject receives the action!
