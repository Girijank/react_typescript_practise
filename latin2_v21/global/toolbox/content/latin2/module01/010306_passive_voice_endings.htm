Present System, Passive Voice Personal Endings

Present System: Present, Imperfect, and Future tense verbs

Nota Bene: These endings will be used with the Present stem of a verb.
  • Present stem = 2nd principal part (infinitive) minus "-re"
  • Example: amare (to love) – re = ama- (Present stem)

Comparing Active versus Passive Voice Personal Endings

Active Voice – the subject is acting Example: amo – I love
Passive Voice – the subject is being acted upon Example: amor – I am being loved

Voice tells you about the relationship between the subject and its verb.

Active voice is usually what we’re using. Active voice shows that the subject is acting—i.e., it’s performing the action of the verb.

Passive voice is less common but necessary and important! Active voice shows that the verb is acting upon the subject—i.e., something happens to the subject.

Nota Bene: Voice is not the same thing as tense (which tells you the when of a verb)

Examples
Active Passive
We carry the books The books are carried.
We carried the books. The books were carried.
We will carry the books. The books will be carried.
