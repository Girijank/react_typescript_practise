Here are some important and unique characteristics about the Hebrew alphabet:

  1. Notice how the Hebrew alphabet has two formats; one is used primarily for print and the other is used in handwriting, since it's a lot more fluid and easier to write quickly.

  2. Each letter in the alphabet has a numeric value according to its placement in the series of letters:
    1. The first nine letters (י-א) are assigned the numbers 1–10.
    2. The next nine letters (ק-כ) are assigned 20–100.
    3. The last three are assigned the numbers 200–400.
    4. Final forms are assigned the numbers 500–900, as follows:

      Alphabet letter Numeric value
      ך 500
      ם 600
      ן 700
      ף 800
      א ץ
    5. There is no representation for the number zero.
    6. Numbers are written right to left.
    7. Letter combination provide values within the range; for example, the combination of י ח+ is the number 18 where "י" represents the 10 and the "ח" represents the 8, which together make the number 18.
