Vowels—Nikkud

Nikkud is the marker system used to represent the vowels and emphasis in Hebrew writing. Nikkud determines the pronunciation of letters but was originally created to improve the language and to preserve and keep its pronunciation uniform.

Modern Hebrew has five vowels that are represented in the following way:

Pay attention to the symbols that are positioned under or over the letter!

Pronunciation Hebrew Vowel English Vowel
a as in yacht אַ אָ a
eh as in bed ey as in hey אֶ אֵ e
i as in machine אִי אִ i
o as in low ֺאו ֺא o
oo as in zoo אוּ אֻ u
a as in alone—silent in the middle of a syllable. אְ -

There are many rules for when and how to use each symbol, but luckily you don't have to learn that or use them to be able to properly pronounce the word.

The nikkud, while pretty and important, is not always necessary to be able to read the word properly. There are two forms of writing: with nikkud and without nikkud.

Spelling that lacks nikkud is called "full spelling," which uses five consonants that are also used to indicate vowels.

Those letters are:

Hebrew letter English vowel
א Used to mark a, e, i, o, u
ה Used to mark the a, e, and the o vowels, only at the end of a word
ו Used to mark the o and u vowels
י Used to mark the i and at times e and a
