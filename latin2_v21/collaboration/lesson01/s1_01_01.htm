Stop and Collaborate

The Roman Monarchy was a fascinating period, lasting from 753 BCE–509 BCE in Rome, where seven kings ruled the city. The seven kings were all very interesting in their own rights. Some were noble, some were distrustful, and one in particular was kicked out of Rome! Nevertheless, they all contributed to the creation of the physical, legal, and cultural infrastructure of Rome, much of which still lives on, not only in modern Rome, but in our everyday cultures worldwide. For this collaboration, you will have the opportunity to discuss one of the kings in a fun and exciting way. Let’s get started!

This collaboration has three parts:

Part One

Answer the following questions and record your answers:

Select a king from the Roman monarchy (753 BCE–509 BCE):

  • Write down at least four interesting facts about the king.
  • Find images of two architectural structures that the king created.
  • Write down at least two events, architectural structures, or activities the king created that are still relevant today.

Include the following:

  • Give three sentences in Latin introducing your selected king. Include his name, the dates of his reign, and what he was most known for (in complete sentences).
  • Prepare one paragraph of at least 4–5 sentences, in English, comparing the activities, architectural monuments, or leadership traits of your chosen king to those that are still relevant today.
  • Create an eight-question survey of questions to ask your partner. The first four questions should be in Latin, asking your partner the name of his/her king, what he/she liked or disliked about the king, and an architectural monument that the king created. The remaining four questions should be in English, asking your partner about which king they chose and other details that they included in their description.
Part Two

Find a Collaboration Partner

Find a partner to work with. Contact your instructor for assistance with finding a partner. Once you have found someone to work with, you and your partner will communicate back and forth to complete the collaboration task.

Be sure to meet all the requirements in the applicable collaboration rubric.
