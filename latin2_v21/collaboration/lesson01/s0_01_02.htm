Stop and Collaborate

The Roman Empire, beginning from 27 BCE and lasting until the fall of the western empire in 476 CE, was the most dominating empire the world had ever seen. The Roman emperors were all monumental characters who changed the face of the earth. One can never tire of studying any of the emperors because they all had such huge personalities—some good, some bad, and some incredibly questionable. For this collaboration, you will have the opportunity to explore one of your favorite emperors and discuss your likes and dislikes about the emperor in a fun and exciting way. Let’s get started!

This collaboration has three parts:

Part One

Answer the following questions and record your answers:

Select an emperor from the Roman Empire (27 BCE—476 CE):

  • Write down at least four interesting facts about the emperor.
  • Find images of two architectural structures that the emperor created.
  • Write down at least two events, architectural structures, or activities the emperor created that are still relevant today.

Include the following:

  • Give three sentences in Latin, introducing your selected emperor. Include his name, the dates of his reign, and what he was most known for (in complete sentences).
  • Prepare one paragraph of at least 4–5 sentences, in English, comparing the activities, architectural monuments, or leadership traits of your chosen emperor to those that are still relevant today.
  • Create an eight-question survey of questions to ask your partner. The first four questions should be in Latin, asking your partner the name of his/her emperor, what he/she liked or disliked about the emperor, and an architectural monument that the emperor created. The remaining four questions should be in English, asking your partner about which emperor they chose and other details that they included in their description.
Part Two

Find a Collaboration Partner

Find a partner to work with. Contact your instructor for assistance with finding a partner. Once you have found someone to work with, you and your partner will communicate back and forth to complete the collaboration task.

Be sure to meet all the requirements in the applicable collaboration rubric.

Submit Your Collaboration Reflection and the Final Collaboration Product
