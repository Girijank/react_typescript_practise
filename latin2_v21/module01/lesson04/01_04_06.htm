Ablative of Agent

We now know that there is a Passive Voice in Latin where the subject is being controlled by someone. Did you ever wonder how we are to translate that “by someone” in Latin? We have a special function of the Ablative Case for this translation. It is called the “Ablative of Agent.”

English Structure:

Active Voice Passive Voice Ablative of Agent
The man throws the ball. The ball is thrown by the man. by the man
The farmer loves the woman. The woman is loved by the farmer. by the farmer
The master teaches the student. The student is taught by the master. by the master
The inhabitants used to cultivate the fields. The fields used to be cultivated by the inhabitants. by the inhabitants
The pleasant man will see his wife. The wife will be seen by the pleasant man. by the man

Latin Structure:

  • When a subject is acted upon by a person (a human being, not an inanimate object or non-human thing), this is expressed in Latin in the Ablative Case preceded by the preposition a/ ab = “by.”
  • Thus, a sentence that contains an Ablative of Agent will have these three P's:
    1. Passive verb
    2. Preposition a/ab which is translated as "by" in this construction
    3. a Person in the Ablative Case following a/ab
      English Example: Mark is wounded by Luke.
      Latin Structure: Marcus a Lucio vulneratur.
      Ablative of Agent: a Lucio.

Ablative of Agent Sentences

Latin Sentence Ablative of Agent English Translation
Saxum a viro iacitur. A viro The rock is being thrown by the man.
Femina ab agricola amatur. Ab agricola The woman is being loved by the farmer.
Discipula a magistro docetur. A magistro The student is being taught by the master.
Agri ab incolis colebantur. Ab incolis The fields used to be cultivated by the inhabitants.
Coniunx ab iucundo viro videbitur. Ab iucundo viro The wife will be seen by the pleasant man.

Ad astra per sententias: Ablative of Means vs. Ablative of Agent

Juno's Note: Ablative of Agent vs. Ablative of Means

Agent: Ablative Case + Person + a/ab. Translation: by the (noun)

Means: Ablative Case + Object + no Latin preposition. Translation: with/by (noun)

Example:

Agricola frumentum carro portabat. The farmer carried the grain with the cart. Ablative of Means = carro = with the cart
