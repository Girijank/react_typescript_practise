3rd Conjugation Active vs. Passive Voice

  • Simply swap out the Active Voice endings for the Passive Voice endings. The only EXCEPTION to this rule is for the Present Tense Passive Voice 2nd Person Singular form.
  • For the 2nd Person Singular form, the Present Tense Passive Voice and Future Tense Passive Voice are identical.
  • You can figure out which one you are using in the sentence based upon context. In memoria tene!

3rd Conjugation Verb: mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um = to send

Active Voice
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) mitto mittebam mittam
2nd person singular (you) mittis mittebas mittes
3rd person singular (he/she/it) mittit mittebat mittet
1st person plural (we) mittimus mittebamus mittemus
2nd person plural (you all) mittitis mittebatis mittetis
3rd person plural (they) mittunt mittebant mittent
TRANSLATION send/sends, am/are/is sending was/were sending, used to send will send
Passive Voice
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) mittor mittebar mittar
2nd person singular (you) mitteris mittebaris mitteris
3rd person singular (he/she/it) mittitur mittebatur mittetur
1st person plural (we) mittimur mittebamur mittemur
2nd person plural (you all) mittimini mittebamini mittemini
3rd person plural (they) mittuntur mittebantur mittentur
TRANSLATION are being sent was/were being sent, used to be sent will be sent
