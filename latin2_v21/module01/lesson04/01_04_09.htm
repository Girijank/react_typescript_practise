Romulus Founds Rome

Use the English paragraphs to prepare yourself for the story which continues into Latin. Once you feel ready, translate the Latin numbered sentences for a grade. Then, answer the grammar and short essay questions.

Numitor, descendent of Ascanius and the king of Alba Longa, has one son and one daughter. His evil brother, Amulius, however, wanted to become king; Numitor is expelled by Amulius from the kingdom, and Amulius kills Numitor’s son. He forces Numitor’s daughter, Rhea Silvia, to become a Vestal Priestess. Vestal Priestesses were not able to produce children, but the god Mars saw and loved her! With Mars, Rhea Silvia produces two sons!  Angrily, Numitor orders his soldier to kill the twins.

Translation Help and New Vocabulary

1.04 New Grammar to Look Out For!

  • Passive Voice Verbs
    • Tenses: Present, Imperfect, Future

Vocabulary Aid

  • a or ab, followed by an ablative, should be translated as ‘by ____’.
  • miles, militis m. soldier
  • rex, regis, m. king
  • geminus, gemini, m. twin
  • Tiberis, Tiberis, m. Tiber River
  • alveus, alvei m. hollowed-out boat
  • nutrio, nutrire, nutrivi, nutritus, -a, -um to nourish
  • educo, educere, eduxī, eductus,-a, -um to raise
  • The future tense signs for third and fourth conjugation verbs are -e- and -ie- respectively.

Translation Checklist

  • Have you identified the tense, voice, person, number, and mood of the verb?
  • Have you identified the case, number, and gender for the nouns and adjectives?
  • Do your English definitions for each of the words in the Latin translation match the definitions found in the toolbox dictionary?
  • Have you double-checked your Latin vs. English word order?
    • HINT: In Latin, adjectives follow the nouns they modify, genitive nouns follow the nouns they possess, and verbs are typically found at the end of the sentence.
  • Did you transfer all punctuation from Latin to English?
