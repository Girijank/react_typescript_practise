Aeneas Lands in Latium

Let’s learn what Vergil had written about Aeneas when he landed in Italy.

In this reading, we have given you the lines in the actual Aeneid text so that you can read a copy of Aeneid and know exactly where to go in the story to learn more. This is a great way for you to begin deeper research as a Classical Studies student.

Having just spent the majority of Book six of the Aeneid in the underworld, meeting the figures of his past and of Rome's future, Aeneas's ship lands in the Tiber river at the start of Book seven (lines 1-36).  Vergil then explains that Latinus, the King of Latium (the part of Italy that will become Rome), had just received a prophecy that his daughter, Lavinia, would marry a foreigner (lines 37-106). Latinus takes this prophecy to relate to the just-arrived Trojans. The Trojans then send envoys and gifts to Latinus and are welcomed (lines 107-191). They then gain an alliance with Latinus (lines 192-285) and a proposal of marriage with Lavinia. When Juno learns of this, she sends the Allecto, a Fury and goddess of rage, to drive Lavinia's mother, Amata, to insanity and suicide (lines 341-405) and then to enrage Turnus who had previously been engaged to Lavinia (lines 406-474).

The entire rest of the Aeneid will focus on the war between the Trojans/Latins and Turnus's army of Rutulians.  The city of Alba Longa in Latium becomes the new home for Aeneas and his son, Ascanius. It is here where Ascanius will raise a family who will become the ancestors of Romulus and Remus.
