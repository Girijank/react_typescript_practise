3rd-io Conjugation: Active vs. Passive Voice

Simply swap out the Active Voice endings for the Passive Voice endings. The only EXCEPTION to this rule is for the Present Tense Passive Voice 2nd Person Singular form.

  • The 2nd Person Singular form of the Present Tense Passive Voice has NO “i” in it.

Consider the verb 3rd-io Conjugation verb: capio, capere, cepi, captus, -a, -um —to seize

Active Forms
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) capio capiebam capiam
2nd person singular (you) capis capiebas capies
3rd person singular (he/she/it) capit capiebat capiet
1st person plural (we) capimus capiebamus capiemus
2nd person plural (you all) capitis capiebatis capietis
3rd person plural (they) capiunt capiebant capient
TRANSLATION seize/seizes, am/are/is seizing was/were seizing, used to seize will seize
Passive Forms
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) capior capiebar capiar
2nd person singular (you) caperis capiebaris capieris
3rd person singular (he/she/it) capitur capiebatur capietur
1st person plural (we) capimur capiebamur capiemur
2nd person plural (you all) capimini capiebamini capiemini
3rd person plural (they) capiuntur capiebantur capientur
TRANSLATION am being seized was/were being seized will be seized

Notice how the Present Tense 2nd person Singular form does not have the -i as its stem vowel.
4th Conjugation Active vs. Passive Voice

4th Conjugation is almost identical to the 3rd-io Conjugation in the Present System Passive Voice, with the exception of the Present Tense Passive Voice 2nd person Singular form.

Consider the verb audio, audire, audivi, auditus, -a, -um = to hear

Active Voice
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) audio audiebam audiam
2nd person singular (you) audis audiebas audies
3rd person singular (he/she/it) audit audiebat audiet
1st person plural (we) audimus audiebamus audiemus
2nd person plural (you all) auditis audiebatis audietis
3rd person plural (they) audiunt audiebant audient
TRANSLATION hear/hears, am/are/is hearing was/were hearing, used to hear will hear
Passive Voice
Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) audior audiebar audiar
2nd person singular (you) audiris audiebaris audieris
3rd person singular (he/she/it) auditur audiebatur audietur
1st person plural (we) audimur audiebamur audiemur
2nd person plural (you all) audimini audiebamini audiemini
3rd person plural (they) audiuntur audiebantur audientur
TRANSLATION am being heard was/were being heard, used to be heard will be heard
Conjugating Passive Voice

Remember to follow these simple steps to conjugate the 4th Conjugation Present System Passive Voice verbs:

Let’s use the verb munio, munire, munivi, munitus,a,um = to fortify

  1. 1
    Start with an infinitive.
    Remember, that is the verb that ends in -are, -ere, or -ire.     		munire
  2. 2
    Drop the -re munire ⇨ muni
  3. 3 Add the correct ending to the stem for Present Tense. For the Imperfect Tense, add -e + endings. For the Future Tense, add the endings.

      Present Imperfect Future
    1st person singular (I) munior muniebar muniar
    2nd person singular (you) muniris muniebaris munieris
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) munitur muniebatur munietur
    1st person plural (we) munimur muniebamur muniemur
    2nd person plural (you all) munimini muniebamini muniemini
    3rd person plural (they) muniuntur muniebantur munientur
    am being fortified was/were being fortified, used to be fortified will be fortified
    If you follow these steps, you’ll always conjugate the passive verb correctly for the 4th Conjugation.
