What is a Declension?

All of the nouns in Latin are divided into five groups or families called declensions. Each noun you learn in your vocabulary will belong to one of these declensions:

1st Declension

2nd Declension

3rd Declension

4th Declension

5th Declension

When we say "decline" a noun, you will need to write the noun with all of its Latin case forms with the correct declension endings, including the English translation for each form.

Declining a noun is an important action to remember. You will be required to demonstrate your declining skills throughout the course; this is one part of how you will master Latin.

1st Declension Nouns Review

It never hurts to review your noun declensions. Let's start with 1st Declension!

How do I know a noun is 1st Declension?

The Genitive singular ending ends in -ae.

  • agricola, agricolae m. farmer
  • insula, insulae f. island

Agricola feminam amat. The farmer loves the woman.

1st Declension Noun Endings

Listen to the pronunciation of this conjugated verb.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative -a -ae
Genitive -ae -arum
Dative -ae -is
Accusative -am -as
Ablative -a -is

Agricola insulae feminam amat.

The farmer of the island loves the woman.


Notice how the noun insula, insulae f. Island is 1st Declension based upon its -ae in the Genitive singular form. Listen to the pronunciation of this declined noun to help you remember these endings. Don't forget to repeat what you hear!

  Singular English Meaning Plural English Meaning
Nominative insula the island insulae the islands
Genitive insulae of the island insularum of the islands
Dative insulae to/for the island insulis to/for the islands
Accusative insulam the island insulas the island
Ablative insula by/with the island insulis by/with the islands
