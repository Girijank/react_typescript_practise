2nd Declension, Masculine and Neuter Nouns Foundation

How do I know a noun is 2nd Declension?

The Genitive singular ending ends in -i.

2nd Declension can be either masculine or neuter gender:

  • 2nd Declension masculine:

    • The Nominative singular will end in either -us, -er, or -ir.

      Example: puer, pueri m.—boy

      Puer amicos videt. The boy sees the friends.

  • 2nd Declension neuter:

    • The Nominative and Accusative forms are identical—hence the -um ending in the dictionary entry.

      Example: templum, templi n. —temple

      Puer templum videt. The boy sees the temple.

Juno's Note: More 2nd Declension

If you follow these steps, you'll always be able to recognize any 2nd Declension noun in a sentence.

  • Begin by looking up your noun in the dictionary. Because nouns change endings, the form you see in your sentence will often not match the first dictionary entry.
  • If you see a noun form like agros in the sentence, you will want to figure out the stem and find the correct entry in the dictionary.
  • The stem is formed by dropping the Genitive singular ending from the second dictionary entry.
  • Example: When you drop the case ending -os, we are left with the stem agr-. When you look in the dictionary, you will see the entry: ager, agrī, m., field. You can see that the Genitive -i ending from that second entry makes this 2nd Declension. Dropping this ending will also give us the noun stem (matching the agr- stem from agros).
