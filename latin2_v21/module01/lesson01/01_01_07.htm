2nd Declension: Genitive singular ends in -i.

3rd Declension Noun Rules

  • 3rd Declension: Genitive singular ends in –is.
  • 3rd Declension I-stem: Genitive singular ends in –is.
    • Certain nouns are I-stem based upon their Nominative and Genitive forms.
  • 3rd Declension (regular and I-stem) has three genders:
    • Masculine/Feminine have the same set of endings.
    • Neuter has one group of endings. (The Nominative and Accusative forms are identical.)

3rd Declension
I-stem Masculine/Feminine

If a Latin noun follows the rules below, mark it as an “I-stem” noun. It will have the letter “-i” as part of most of its case endings.

Masculine/Feminine I-stems

  1. Mirror Effect: Nominative Singular and Genitive Singular are mirror images.
    Example: navis, navis f. ship
  2. Nominative ends in -x or -s, and the Genitive stem ends in a double consonant.
    Example: pars, partis f. part, share; arx, arcis f. citadel
Case Singular   Plural  
Nominative ---------- arx -es arces
Genitive -is arcis -ium arcium
Dative -i arci -ibus arcibus
Accusative -em arcem -es or -is arces or arcis
Ablative -e / -i arce / arci -ibus arcibus

Masculine and Feminine I-stems use the Genitive Plural ending -ium; Ablative singular can end in -i.

Homines arcium agricolas spectant. The men of the citadels are watching the farmers.

Notice how the noun arx, arcis f. citadel is 3rd Declension I-stem based upon its Nominative singular ending in -x and its Genitive singular base ending in the double consonant -rc. 3rd Declension
I-stem Neuter

Neuter I-stems

Nominative ends in -al, -e, -r.

Examples:

  • animal, animalis n. animal;
  • mare, maris n. sea;
  • exemplar, exemplaris n. example

Neuter I-stems use the Nominative/Accusative plural ending -ia; Ablative singular can end in -i; and Genitive plural ends in -ium.

Example: Piscis est exemplar animalis in mari. A fish is an example of an animal in the sea.
