Grammar Review: Present System, Passive Voice

You’ve done so well with learning the Passive Voice! Now let’s put it all together through video and text to help you become a passive voice guru.

Here’s a formula to help you use this pattern successfully:

present stem + stem vowel + Passive Voice tense endings

Let’s take a look using the verb amo, amare, amavi, amatus,-a, -um = to love as our example. Remember that any verb conjugation and its accompanying stem vowel can be substituted in for the examples.

Present Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amatur.
(The wife is being loved by the leader.)

Present Stem + Stem Vowel + Present Passive Endings
am- + a + -r,-ris,-tur,-mur,-mini,-ntur

Imperfect Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amabatur.
(The wife used to be loved by the leader.)

Present Stem + Stem Vowel + Imperfect Passive Endings
am- + a + -bar,-baris,-batur,-bamur,-bamini,-bantur

Future Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amabitur.
(The wife will be loved by the leader.)

Present Stem + Stem Vowel (1st/2nd conjugation only; 3rd conjugation no stem vowel; 3rd-io/ 4th conjugation has an “i” before the endings) + Future Passive Endings
am- + a + -bor,-beris,-bitur,-bimur,-bimini,-buntur
(1st/2nd conjugation)
-ar,-eris, -etur, -emur, -emini, -entur
(3rd/4th conjugation)
