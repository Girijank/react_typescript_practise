Servius Tullius, 6th King of Rome

I am Servius Tullius, 6th king of Rome.  I ruled from: 578 BCE to 534 BCE. I am known for the following:

  • marriage to Tarquinia
  • prophecy by Tanaquil
  • creation of election by Senate
  • the Census
  • creation of five classes
  • servian Wall
  • murder of spouses by Tarquinius Superbus and Tullia Minor

Servius Tullius, 6th King of Rome

Servius established a new census system—a national register for taxation and military that divided the populus into new tribes and assigned them to one of the five classes according to their wealth.  This replaced the older curiae, which had implied regional divisions and was rooted in clan loyalties. 

He is also credited with the construction of the Temple of Diana on the Aventine Hill. He was assassinated by his younger daughter Tullia, whose crime is marked by the dedication of the Roman street, Sceleratus Vicus.
