Passive Voice Review

You have now learned all the tenses in both the Active and Passive Voices. Let’s do a review of the Passive Voice.

Passive Voice Definition: When the verb shows that the subject is receiving the action, it is in the Passive Voice.

Tense Active Voice Passive Voice
Present Grandpa mows the lawn. The lawn is being mowed by Grandpa.
Imperfect Grandpa used to mow the lawn. The lawn used to be mowed by Grandpa.
Future Grandpa will mow the lawn. The lawn will be mowed by Grandpa.
Perfect Grandpa mowed the lawn. The lawn was (has been) mowed by Grandpa.
Pluperfect Grandpa had mowed the lawn. The lawn had been mowed by Grandpa.
Future Perfect Grandpa will have mowed the lawn. The lawn will have been mowed by Grandpa.

Latin Active and Passive Voice Verb Synopsis

Now that you know how the Passive Voice looks and works in every verb tense, let’s do a synopsis of the verb amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um = to love in the 3rd person singular forms Active and Passive Voice.

If you can close your eyes and do this synopsis without looking, then you know that you have mastered verbs!

Tense Latin English Tense Latin English
Active Voice Passive Voice
