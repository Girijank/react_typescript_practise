Grammar Review: Perfect System, Passive Voice

Now let’s review the Perfect System in the Passive Voice.

Let’s take a look!

Latin Formation:

  • The 4th Principal Part of the verb is also called the Perfect Passive Participle (PPP).

    amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um

    laudo, laudare, laudavi, laudatus, -a, -um

    removeo, removere, removi, remotus, -a, -um

    teneo, tenere, tenui, tentus, -a, -um

  • Consists of two words: the Fourth Principal Part and the proper tense of sum

    Perfect Passive Tense = PPP + Present Tense of SUM.

    Pluperfect Passive Tense = PPP + Imperfect Tense of SUM

    Future Perfect Passive Tense = PPP + Future Tense of SUM

Perfect Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amata est.
(The wife has been loved by the leader.)

4th PP + Subject-Verb Agreement + Present Tense “sum”
amatus, -a, -um + Matching gender and number + sum, es, est, sumus, estis, sunt

Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amata erat.
(The wife had been loved by the leader.)

4th PP + Subject-Verb Agreement + Present Tense “sum”
amatus, -a, -um + Matching gender and number + eram, eras, erat, eramus, eratis, erant

Future Perfect Tense Passive Voice

Coniunx a duce amata erit.
(The wife will have been loved by the leader.)

4th PP + Subject-Verb Agreement + Present Tense “sum”
amatus, -a, -um + Matching gender and number + ero, eris, erit, erimus, eritis, erunt

Exerceamus! Perfect System Verb Translation

Identify the tense, voice, and translation of each verb.

exercitum est

Perfect Passive, it has been trained

monita ero

Future Perfect Passive, I will have been warned

rogati eramus

Pluperfect Passive, we had been asked

docti estis

Perfect Passive, you have been taught
