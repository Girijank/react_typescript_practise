Verb Terms: Throughout your Latin studies, you will learn each of these forms.

Conjugation

Latin divides all the verbs into four groups or families called conjugations. The infinitive ending determines the conjugation.

  1. 1st Conjugation
  2. 2nd Conjugation
  3. 3rd Conjugation/ 3rd-io Conjugation
  4. 4th Conjugation

Tense

Latin, like English, has six tenses that show the time an action takes place:

  1. Present
  2. Imperfect (simple past)
  3. Future
  4. Perfect
  5. Pluperfect
  6. Future Perfect

Person

  1. 1st Person = I or we
  2. 2nd Person = you singular, you plural
  3. 3rd Person = he/she/it or they

Number

Singular or Plural

Voice

Voice shows the relationship between the subject and the verb. Like English, Latin has two voices:

  • Active—The subject is the doer of the action
  • Passive—The subject receives the action

Mood

Mood has classifications that denote the attitude of the speaker. Latin has three moods:

  1. Indicative—Statements and simple questions
  2. Imperative—Commands
  3. Subjunctive—Possibility and wish statements
