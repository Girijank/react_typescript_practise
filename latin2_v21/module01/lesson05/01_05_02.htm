Step 2
Compare and Contrast Paragraph: Now, in English, compare and contrast Rome’s foundation story with the two places you researched. Make sure to include the following:

  • similarities between Rome’s foundation story and the foundation stories of the two places
  • differences between Rome’s foundation story and the foundation stories of the two places
  • full sentence answers with a minimum of 200 words
  • citation of the sources you used for your research, using MLA format

Step 3
Latin Writing Assignment: Write three sentences in Latin about Rome’s foundation story. At least two of the three sentences must be written in the Passive Voice

Example: Carthage Foundation Story

  • Sentence 1: Carthago a Phoenicibus constituebatur. (Carthage was founded by the Phoenicians.)
  • Sentence 2: Dido est regina Carthaginis. (Dido is the queen of Carthage.)
  • Sentence 3: Dido aut a sacerdote aut ab viro de Troia amabatur. (Dido was loved either by a priest or by a man from Troy.)

Juno's Note: Composing Passive Sentences in Latin

Writing Passive sentences in English versus Latin are two very different processes! Visit Composing a Passive Sentence in Latin for a step-by-step guide. You can always review these steps in your Toolbox.

When writing in Latin, one challenge many scholars face is the declining of Roman names. Since names are nouns and can be used as such in a sentence, make sure they have the correct case endings! You can visit Latin Name Declining in your Toolbox for a refresher.
