Perfect Tense Passive Voice

So far in your Latin studies, all of your perfect tense verbs have been in the Active Voice. Now we finally get to use the 4th Principal Part of the verb for something! That something is the Perfect Tense Passive Voice.

How do I form Perfect Tense Passive Voice?

Remember to follow these simple steps to conjugate verbs in this form:


  1. 1
    Find the 4th Principle Part of the Verb (PPP) amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um
    laudo, laudare, laudavi, laudatus, -a, -um
    Nota bene: This is the verb part that ends in -us, -a, or -um. The gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural) of the PPP depend upon the gender and number of the subject of the sentence. removeo, removere, removi, remotus, -a, -um
    teneo, tenere, tenui, tentus, -a, -um
  2. 2
    Add the correct person and number of the Present Tense of the verb “sum.” sum, es, est, sumus, estis, sunt
    Nota bene: The “sum” verb must agree with the subject of the sentence in person and number.
  3. 3

    Put it all together and translate in English:

    Perfect Tense Passive Voice
    servo, servare, servavi, servatus, -a, -um = to save

    Singular Plural
    1st servatus, -a,-um sum
    I have been saved OR I was saved     		1st servati, -ae, -a sumus
    We have been saved OR we were saved
    2nd servatus, -a,-um es
    You (s.) have been saved OR you (s.) were saved     		2nd servati, -ae, -a estis
    You (pl.) have been saved OR you (pl.) were saved
    3rd servatus, -a,-um est
    He/She/It has been saved OR he, she, it was saved     		3rd servati, -ae, -a sunt
    They have been saved OR they were saved

Sentence Examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Verb Agreement
Navis tempestate dementi pressa est. The ship has been pressed hard by the crazy storm. pressa est = 3rd person singular, feminine to agree with navis.
Salus militis in difficili bello soluta est. The safety of the soldier has been loosened in the difficult war. soluta est = 3rd person singular, feminine to agree with salus.
A bono duce ducti estis. You all have been led by a good leader. ducti estis = 2nd person plural, masculine and serves as the subject of the sentence. With “you all” as masculine, the subjects would be men.

Ad astra per sententias: Sentence Application

Translate these sentences from Latin to English, then find the person, number, and gender of Perfect Tense Passive Verb.

Pax et lex a Numa Pompilio servatae sunt.

Peace and Law have been protected by Numa Pompilius.

servatae sunt: 3rd person plural, feminine to agree with the combined pax et lex which are both feminine, 3rd person

Coniunx et templa ab viro laudata sunt.

The wife and temple were praised by the husband.

laudata sunt: 3rd person plural, neuter to agree with the compound subject coniunx et templa

Salus e tempestate in villa culta est.

Safety from the storm was established in the farmhouse.

culta est: 3rd person singular, feminine to agree with salus which is 3rd person, feminine singular

Juno’s Note: Perfect Tense Passive Voice Translation

No matter the conjugation, in Latin, you will conjugate all verbs in the Perfect Tense Passive Voice in the same way. Likewise, in English, the translation will always have “was verbed” or “has been verbed.”
