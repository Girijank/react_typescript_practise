How do I form Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice?

Remember to follow these simple steps to conjugate verbs in this form:


  1. 1
    Find the 4th Principle Part of the Verb (PPP) amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um
    laudo, laudare, laudavi, laudatus, -a, -um
    Nota bene: This is the verb part that ends in -us, -a, or -um. The gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural) of the PPP depend upon the gender and number of the subject of the sentence. removeo, removere, removi, remotus, -a, -um
    teneo, tenere, tenui, tentus, -a, -um
  2. 2
    Add the correct person and number of the Imperfect Tense of the verb “sum.” eram, eras, erat, eramus, eratis, erant
    Nota bene: The “sum” verb must agree with the subject of the sentence in person and number.
  3. 3

    Put it all together and translate in English:

    Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice
    moveo, movere, movi, motus, - a, -um = to move

    Singular Plural
    1st motus, -a,-um eram
    I had been moved    		 1st moti, -ae, -a eramus
    We had been moved
    2nd motus, -a,-um eras
    You (s.) had been moved    		 2nd moti, -ae, -a eratis
    You (pl.) had been moved
    3rd motus, -a,-um erat
    He/She/It had been moved    		 3rd moti, -ae, -a erant
    They had been moved

Sentence Examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Verb Agreement
Ab incolis in insula consultae eramus. We had been consulted by the inhabitants on the island. consultae eramus = 1st person plural, feminine and serves as the subject of the sentence. With “we” as feminine, the subjects would be women.
Modi avorum amoenorum ab pueris relicti erant. The ways of the pleasant grandfather had been abandoned by the boys. relicti erant = 3rd person plural, masculine to agree with modi.
Pompa et superbia ad urbem rediturae erant. Ostentation and haughtiness had been returned to the city. rediturae erant = 3rd person plural, feminine to agree with the compound subject pompa et superbia

Ad astra per sententias: Sentence Practice

Translate these sentences from Latin to English; then find the person, number, and gender of Pluperfect Tense Passive Verb.

Milites a copiis Graecis pugnati erant.

Soldiers had been fought by Greek troops.

pugnati erant: 3rd person plural, masculine to agree with milites which is 3rdperson plural masculine

Omne spatium casae a liberis occupatum erat.

Each space of the house had been occupied by the children.

occupatum erat: 3rd person sg, neuter to agree with omne spatium which is 3rd person singular neuter

Pompa a duce ducta erat.

The parade had been led by the leader.

ducta erat: 3rd person singular, feminine to agree with pompa which is 3rd person singular feminine

Juno’s Note: Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice Translation

No matter the conjugation, in Latin, you will conjugate all verbs in the Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice in the same way. Likewise, in English, the translation will always have “had been verbed.”
