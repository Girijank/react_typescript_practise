Latin Formation of the Perfect System Passive Voice

Let’s watch this video to learn how to form the three tenses of the Perfect System Passive Voice in Latin!

Recap:

  1. 1
    4th Principal Part of the verb = Perfect Passive Participle (PPP).
  2. 2
    The Perfect Passive Participle (PPP) is a verbal adjective and must agree with the subject of the sentence.
    If the subject is puella (feminine singular), the PPP will be amata.
    If the subject is puellae (feminine plural), the PPP will be amatae.
    If the subject is vir (masculine singular), the PPP will be amatus.
    If the subject is viri (masculine plural), the PPP will be amati.
    If the subject is oppidum (neuter singular), the PPP will be amatum.
    If the subject is oppida (neuter plural), the PPP will be amata.
    If the subject is a compound subject, follow these directions:
    • feminine + feminine word, the PPP will be amatae (feminine plural)
    • masculine + masculine word, the PPP will be amati (masculine plural)
    • masculine + feminine word, the PPP will be amati (masculine plural)
    • neuter + any gender word, the PPP will be amata (neuter plural)
  3. 3

    In Latin, the Perfect System Passive Voice includes:

    Perfect Tense Passive Voice

    4th PP + Present Tense “sum”
    • Latin example: -amatus,-a, -um sum = I have been loved.
    • All English translations of the Perfect Passive include the words "has/ have been" or “was/ were verbed.”
    • English example: The laws have been loosened by the kings.

    Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice

    4th PP + Imperfect Tense “sum”
    • Latin example: amatus, -a, -um eram = I had been loved.
    • All English translations of the Pluperfect Tense Passive Voice include the words "had been."
    • English example: The leader had been pressed hard by the army.

    Future Perfect Tense, Passive Voice

    4th PP + Future Tense “sum”
    • Latin example: -amatus, -a, -um ero = I will have been loved.
    • All English translations of the Future Perfect Tense Passive Voice include the words "will have been."
    • English example: The storm will have been sent by the gods.
