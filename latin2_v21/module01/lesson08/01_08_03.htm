The Perfect System, Active versus Passive Voice

Time to dig back into your grammar and learn the Passive Voice for the Perfect, Pluperfect, and Future Perfect Tenses which all together is called the Perfect System Passive Voice. First, review your Verb Guide to refresh your memory on important verb vocabulary.

Let’s first review what voice is:

The voice of the verb tells you whether the subject (shown by person and number of the verb ending) does the action, or if the subject is receiving the action of the verb.

Active Voice: In the Active Voice, the subject does the action (e.g., amas = you love.)

Passive Voice: In the Passive Voice, the action instead happens to the subject (e.g., amaris = you are being loved).

Remember, the Perfect System is comprised of the Perfect, Pluperfect, and Future Perfecttenses. These forms are considered "past tense" in English, with varying uses. The word "been" in English is often used for these tenses in the Passive Voice.

Let’s compare these Passive Voice examples from the Perfect System:

Active Voice
The man threw the ball.
The farmer had loved the woman.
The master will have taught the student.
The inhabitants have cultivated the fields.
The pleasant man had seen his wife.
Passive Voice
The ball was being thrown by the man. /The ball has been thrown by the man.
The woman had been loved by the farmer.
The student will have been taught by the master.
The fields have been cultivated by the inhabitants.
The wife had been seen by the pleasant man.
