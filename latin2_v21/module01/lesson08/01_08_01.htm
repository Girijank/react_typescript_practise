The Etruscan Kings of the Roman Monarchy

The Etruscan Dynasty of Rome (c. 616 BCE – 510 BCE): Tarquinius Priscus, Servius Tullius, and Tarquinius Superbus.

There were three kings from the northern Italian region of Tarquinia. The Etruscan people lived there.

Who were the Etruscans?

The Etruscans were a thriving, rich, sophisticated civilization who had their own language, kings, trade empire, and customs. They were especially good in:

  • manufacturing
  • metallurgy
  • trade

They travelled to Rome and brought their customs, traditions, gods, and designing techniques to the early Romans. They became kings of Rome, naturally and strategically. The Romans adopted many of the Etruscans' practices, including their funeral games. At the funerals for wealthy Etruscans, men fought to the death to honor the deceased. The fights became popular amusement at funerals and transcended into the pomp and circumstance surrounding wealthy Roman funerals. These funeral bouts would influence the invention of the Roman gladiator games.
