Tarquinius Priscus, 5th King of Rome

You’ve learned so much about the Roman kings. Let’s learn about Tarquinius Priscus, the 5th king of Rome.

The first Etruscan monarch and fifth king of Rome is credited with the foundation of the Ludi Romani, the Circus Maximus, and construction of the Cloacae, Rome’s sewage system. He is also credited with bringing the Etruscan military triumph tradition to Rome. Much of Rome's military symbolism and civil offices is believed to have been developed during this period. He is also credited with creating the Circus Maximus.

Tarquinius Priscus, 5th King of Rome

I am Tarquinius Priscus, 5th king of Rome.  I ruled from: c. 616 BCE–579 BCE. I am known for:

  • The death of Ancus Marcius
  • Elections
  • Etruscan heritage
  • Increasing the Senate
  • Plunder funding from wars
  • Circus Maximus
  • Temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus

The Circus Maximus is a large chariot racing arena located between the Aventine Hill and the Palatine Hill. It contained an oval track, starting gates for the horses, and turning posts. Charioteers would ride chariots pulled by teams of either four, six, eight, or twelve horses. It was an exciting public venue which also served as host for other Roman games. The Romans used the venue until the sixth century CE.
