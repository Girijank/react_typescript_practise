The Aeneid Story and Vergil

Let’s meet one of the most famous poets of Rome, Publius Vergilius Maro, a.k.a., Vergil. Vergil was born in Mantua in northern Italy in 70 BCE. He lived during the time of Emperor Augustus Caesar who was looking for a way to unify the Roman people during the country’s turbulent times. He turned to his friend Vergil, who was tasked with creating a national epic, a story to glorify Rome’s origins and celebrate the divine lineage of the Roman emperor, Augustus (whom you will learn about in a later module). This story was called the Aeneid.

The Aeneid is the story of the Trojan hero Aeneas, son of Venus and the mortal Anchises. After escaping the destruction of Troy in the Trojan War, Aeneas set sail with his father, son, and a group of survivors in search of a “new Troy.”

A Roman reader would have known that several hundred years later, Aeneas’s descendants, Romulus and Remus, would build Rome. Atque plurimi centum anni postea, his descendant, Augustus, would become the first emperor of Rome. Vergil’s epic poem, almost 10,000 lines long, was unfinished at the time of his death in 19 BCE. Post mortem suam, fabulam vastari cupivit. Against his wishes, Augustus had the poem published. Students like you have been reading and studying it ever since!
