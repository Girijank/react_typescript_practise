Present Tense Passive Voice: 1st and 2nd Conjugation Verbs

Let’s learn the Passive Voice endings for the Present system. Then, let’s conjugate a verb in the Present Tense Passive Voice!

Present Tense Passive Voice

Present Tense Passive Voice Endings

Person Singular Translation Plural Translation
1st Person -r I am being verbed. -mur We are being verbed.
2nd Person -ris You are being verbed. -mini You all are being verbed.
3rd Person -tur He, she, it is being verbed. -ntur They are being verbed.

1st Conjugation Verb:
laudo, laudare, laudavi, laudatus, -a, -um = to praise

Person Singular Translation Plural Translation
1st Person laudor I am being praised. laudamur We are being praised.
2nd Person laudaris You are being praised. laudamini You all are being praised.
3rd Person laudatur He, she, it is being praised. laudantur They are being praised.
Present Passive, 1st Conjugation

Let’s learn the steps to conjugating a 1st Conjugation verb in the Present Tense Passive Voice. You’ll notice how similar it is to conjugating the Active form.

We will use the verb: amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um = to love

  1. 1
    Start with an infinitive.
    Remember it is the verb that ends in -are.     		amare
  2. 2
    Drop the -re ending. amareama-
  3. 3 Add the Present Tense Passive Voice Endings—except for the 1st Person singular, when you add the ending -or.

    Person Latin Form English Translation
    1st person singular (I) amor I am being loved.
    2nd person singular (you) amaris You are being loved.
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) amatur He, she, it is being loved.
    1st person plural (we) amamur We are being loved.
    2nd person plural (you all) amamini You all are being loved.
    3rd person plural (they) amantur They are being loved.

Now let’s see the Present Tense Active Voice and the Passive Voice side by side.

Present Passive, 2nd Conjugation

Let’s now compare a 2nd Conjugation verb in the Present Tense Active versus Passive Voice!

2nd Conjugation Verb = doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um = to teach

Active Voice
Person Latin Form English Translation
1st person singular (I) doceo I teach, am teaching, do teach
2nd person singular (you) doces you teach, are teaching, do teach
3rd person singular (he/she/it) docet he/she/it teaches, is teaching, does teach
1st person plural (we) docemus we teach, are teaching, do teach
2nd person plural (you all) docetis you all teach, are teaching, do teach
3rd person plural (they) docent they teach, are teaching, do teach
Passive Voice
Person Latin Form English Translation
1st person singular (I) doceor I am being taught.
2nd person singular (you) doceris You are being taught.
3rd person singular (he/she/it) docetur He, she, it is being taught.
1st person plural (we) docemur We are being taught.
2nd person plural (you all) docemini You all are being taught.
3rd person plural (they) docentur They are being taught.

Here is another 2nd conjugation verb in the Present Tense Passive Voice

Sentence Examples:

  1. Vir docetur. The man is being taught.
  2. Vir et femina docentur. The man and the woman are being taught.
  3. Praeda in oppido incolis datur. The loot in the town is being given to the inhabitants.
  4. Multi populi in terra pugnantur. Many people are being fought on the land.
