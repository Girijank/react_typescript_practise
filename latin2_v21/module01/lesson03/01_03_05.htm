Imperfect Tense Passive Voice: 1st and 2nd Conjugation Verbs

Let’s learn the Imperfect Tense Passive Endings! It will be easy for you to conjugate a verb in the Imperfect Tense Passive Voice because you’ll simply do a switcheroo with the Imperfect Active endings.

Imperfect Tense Active versus Passive Voice
Active Endings
Person Singular Plural
1st Person -bam -bamus
2nd Person -bas -batis
3rd Person -bat -bant
Passive Endings
Person Singular Plural
1st Person -bar -bamur
2nd Person -baris -bamini
3rd Person -batur -bantur
  • Compare the Active and Passive endings.
  • Remember, the Active is translated using "was/were verb+ing," and the Passive uses "was/were (being) verb+ed." Tap the next tab to see the translating in action!
  • Example Sentences:
    • Io prope flumen Inachum spectabatur. Io was being watched near the Inachus river.
    • Vir ab femina amabatur. The man used to be loved by the woman.
    • Fama civibus Romae dabatur. Fame was being given to the citizens of Rome.
Conjugating 1st/2nd Conjugation Imperfect Tense, Passive Voice

Let’s learn the steps to conjugating a 1st Conjugation and 2nd Conjugation verb in the Imperfect Tense Passive Voice. You’ll notice how similar it is to conjugating the Active form.

We will use the verbs:
1st Conjugation: amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um —to love
2nd Conjugation: doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um —to teach

  1. 1
    Start with an infinitive.
    Remember it is the verb that ends in —are.     		amare
    docere
  2. 2
    Drop the -re ending. amare ama-
    docere doce-
  3. 3 Add the Imperfect Tense Passive Voice Endings.

    Person Latin Form English Translation
    1st person singular (I) amabar I used to be/ was being loved
    2nd person singular (you) amabaris You used to be/ were being loved
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) amabatur He/she/it used to be/ was being loved
    1st person plural (we) amabamur We used to be/ were being loved
    2nd person plural (you all) amabamini You all used to be/ were being loved
    3rd person plural (they) amabantur They used to be/ were being loved
    Person Latin Form English Translation
    1st person singular (I) docebar I used to be taught, was being taught
    2nd person singular (you) docebaris You used to be taught, were being taught
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) docebatur He, she, used to be taught, was being taught
    1st person plural (we) docebamur We used to be taught, were being taught
    2nd person plural (you all) docebamini You all used to be taught, were being taught
    3rd person plural (they) docebantur They used to be taught, were being taught
