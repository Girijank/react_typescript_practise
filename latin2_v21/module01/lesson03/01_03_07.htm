Fabula Fabulosa (Fabulous Story): Juno and the Trojan War

'Now golden-throned Hera (Juno), standing on the mountain
of Olympus, beheld him with her eyes, and she at once
understood him who was bustling about in the battle
bringing glory to her own brother and brother-in-law.' —Homer, Iliad, Book 14, lines 154-155

Oh, I definitely had my hand in this war! I was so dominating. Listen to my story to learn more about my actions during the Trojan War.

I am the queen of the gods and wield much power. In the Trojan War, I was on the side of the Greeks and continuously helped my beloved Greeks in battle. At one point in the war, my Greeks were so wearied that they had proposed to abandon their efforts and return to Greece. Consilium eos adiuvare cogitavi. I noticed my husband, Jupiter, a mercurial fan of the Trojans, perched on Mt. Ida keeping an attentive eye on the Trojan battlefield. I knew that I needed to distract his gaze. So, I first tricked my sister Venus to give me her magical girdle; then, I persuaded the god of Sleep to follow me to Mt. Ida. There, with my loving charm and Sleep by my side, I enchanted Jupiter and lulled him to sleep. Eo tempore ipso, meus frater, Neptunus, et ego, Graecos pugnare Troianos adiuvaverunt.

At that very time, my brother, Neptune, and I helped the Greeks to fight the Trojans.

The great Greek Ajax knocked down the Trojan warrior Hector who then had to retreat, leaving the Trojans without their fearless leader. As a result, my Greeks were able to kill many Trojans that day and gain momentum in battle.
