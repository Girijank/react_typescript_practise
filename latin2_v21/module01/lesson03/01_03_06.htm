Future Tense Passive Voice: 1st and 2nd Conjugation Verbs

You are doing fantastic! Now, let’s learn the Future Tense Passive Voice endings!

Future Tense Active versus Passive endings
Active Voice Endings
Person Singular Plural
1st Person -bo -bimus
2nd Person -bis -bitis
3rd Person -bit -bunt
Passive Voice Endings
Person Singular Plural
1st Person -bor -bimur
2nd Person -beris -bimini
3rd Person -bitur -buntur

The 2nd Person singular form: Instead of the Active ending of -bis, it changes to the Passive Ending -beris.

The only ending change that you have to truly think about is the Passive Voice. 2nd Person singular ending. Memorize this ending, and you will be golden!

Conjugating 1st/2nd Conjugation Future Tense, Passive Voice

Let’s learn the steps to conjugating a 1st Conjugation and 2nd Conjugation verb in the Future Tense Passive Voice. You’ll notice how similar it is to conjugating the Active form.

We will use the verbs:
1st conjugation: amo, amare, amavi, amatus,a,um = to love
2nd Conjugation: doceo, docere, docui, doctus,a, um = to teach

  1. 1
    Start with an infinitive.
    Remember it is the verb that ends in -are.     		amare
    docere
  2. 2
    Drop the -re ending. amare ama
    docere doce
  3. 3 Add the Future Tense Passive Voice Endings.

    Person Latin Form English Translation
    1st person singular (I) amabor I will be loved.
    2nd person singular (you) amaberis You will be loved.
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) amabitur He/she/it will be loved.
    1st person plural (we) amabimur We will be loved.
    2nd person plural (you all) amabimini You all will be loved.
    3rd person plural (they) amabuntur They will be loved.
    Person Latin Form English Translation
    1st person singular (I) docebor I will be taught.
    2nd person singular (you) doceberis You will be taught.
    3rd person singular (he/she/it) docebitur He/she/it will be taught.
    1st person plural (we) docebimur We will be taught.
    2nd person plural (you all) docebimini You all will be taught.
    3rd person plural (they) docebuntur They will be taught.
