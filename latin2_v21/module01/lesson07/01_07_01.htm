The Roman Monarchy

Over the next few lessons, you will learn about the seven kings of Rome in the Roman monarchy which lasted from 753 BCE–509 BCE.

As you are reading the stories below, keep in mind that the history of Rome is a combination of both mythical and historical tales. Let's start with Romulus, Rome's legendary first king.

Romulus, 1st King of Rome

Only one person could become the king of Rome. However, both Romulus and Remus wanted the throne! So, they both turned to the gods and omens to decide. Romulus, standing on the Palatine Hill and Remus, on the Aventine Hill, both looked in the sky and used the tradition of augury to determine who should rule Rome. Romulus saw six birds fly above him first. Then, Remus saw 12 birds. They couldn’t agree on what was better—seeing birds first or seeing more birds! So, they both began to build their own cities, making themselves the leader of their own dominion. But Romulus, angered by Remus’s success, jumped over the walls of Remus’s city and killed him, leaving only one twin to rule the land. Success! In 753 BCE, Romulus was now the king and so he named his kingdom after himself—Rome!

Titus Tatius

In some stories, Romulus also shared the crown with Titus Tatius, king of the Sabines, a neighboring tribe.

  • As the king of Rome, Romulus needed to populate the city. So, he opened his doors to all types of folk—robbers, beggars, murderers (not the best of people). Romulus didn’t care—he needed people in his city.
  • Thus, Romulus devised a plan to go to the neighboring city of Sabina to invite all its men over for a party. While the Sabine men partied in Rome, Romulus and his Romans snuck into the Sabine city and stole all of the women away and made them marry the Roman men.
  • The Sabine king, Titus Tatius, was mortified, but instead of starting a costly war with Rome, Titus Tatius agreed as a peace offering to co-rule Rome with Romulus.
