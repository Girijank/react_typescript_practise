Numa Pompilius, 2nd King of Rome

I am Numa Pompilius, 2nd King of Rome. I ruled from: 715 BCE–673 BCE. I am known for: 

  • Temple of Janus
  • Egeria
  • Calendar Change
  • Religion/Pontifex Maximus
  • Peace during my rule

Post Romulum, Numa Pompilius est rex. Egeria, uxor Numae Pompilii, est nympha et in antro habitat. Menses Januarii et Februarii ab Numa Pompilio ad calendarium Romanum adduntur. Religio et officium, Pontifex Maximus, a rege instituuntur. Dum est rex, templum Iani, dei portarum, aedificatur. Ubi portae templi patentur, est bellum, sed ubi clauduntur, est pax. In regno Numae Pompilii, portae templi semper clauduntur.

After Romulus, Numa Pompilius is king. Egeria, the wife of Numa Pompilius, is the nymph and lives in a cave. The months of January and February are added to the Roman calendar by Numa Pompilius. Religion and the office, Pontifex Maximus, are established by the king. While he is king, the temple of Janus, the goddess of doors, is built. When the gates of the temple are opened, there is war, but when they are closed, there is peace. In the kingdom of Numa Pompilius, the gates of the temple are always closed.
