3rd Conjugation, 3rd-io, 4th Conjugation Verbs Foundation

You’re doing great! Now, let’s review 3rd and 4th Conjugation.

3rd Conjugation:

  • The 1st principal part of the verb ends in -o, and the 2nd principal ends in -ere.
    Vir puerum ad scholam mittit. The man sends the boy to the school.

3rd-io Conjugation:

  • The 1st principal part ends in -io, and its 2nd principal part ends in -ere, so this verb is 3rd -io conjugation.
  • An -i is placed before every ending.
    Puella felem capit. The girl seizes the cat.

4th Conjugation:

  • In the 4th Conjugation, the 1st principal part of the verb ends in -io, and the 2nd principal ends in -ire.
  • Magister discipulum audit. The teacher hears the student.
3rd Conjugation Present and Perfect System
  • The 1st principal part ends in -o and the 2nd principal part ends in -ere.
  • Find the present stem of the verb by removing the -ere from the 2nd principal part/infinitive.
    e.g., mittere = mitt-; then add the appropriate vowel.

Consider the verb mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um—to send

2nd versus 3rd Conjugation

You may have noticed some similarities between the Present tense, 2nd conjugation endings, and the 3rd conjugation, Future tense. Let's look at these forms side-by-side:

2nd Conjugation Present System: doceo, docere, docui, doctus, a, um to teach. Listen to the pronunciation of this conjugated verb.

For a 2nd conjugation verb, "e" = present tense, and "bo/bi/bu" = Future tense.

3rd -io Conjugation
  • The 1st principal part of the verb ends in -io and the 2nd principal ends in -ere.
  • You will see an -i- vowel appear in all persons of the Present system.

  • The 1st principal part ends in -io, and the 2nd principal part ends in -ire.
  • Find the present stem of the verb by removing the -ire from the 2nd principal part/infinitive.
    e.g., audire = aud-
