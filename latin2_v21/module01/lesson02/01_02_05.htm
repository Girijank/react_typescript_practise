1st Conjugation and 2nd Conjugation Verbs Foundation

1st Conjugation:

  • The first principal part of the verb ends in -o and the second principal part ends in -are.
  • Find the present stem of the verb by removing the -re from the 2nd principal part/infinitive.
    e.g.,  amare = ama-
  • Puella agricolam amat. = The girl loves the farmer. (Notice that a Nominative singular subject will use the -t verb ending. Since the word "girl" is there, there is no pronoun "she.")

2nd Conjugation:

  • The first principal part of the verb ends in -eo and the second principal ends in -ere.
  • Find the present stem of the verb by removing the -re from the 2nd principal part/infinitive.
    e.g.,  doceo = doce-
  • Puellae agricolam docent. = The girls teach the farmers. (Notice that a Nominative plural subject will use the -nt verb ending. Since the word "girls" is there, there is no pronoun "they.")

1st Conjugation Present System

amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um = to like, to love

Listen to the pronunciation of this conjugated verb.

Person Present Imperfect Future
1st person singular (I) amo amabam amabo
2nd person singular (you) amas amabas amabis
3rd person singular (he/she/it) amat amabat amabit
1st person plural (we) amamus amabamus amabimus
2nd person plural (you all) amatis amabatis amabitis
3rd person plural (they) amant amabant amabunt
