Verb Guide
Throughout your Latin studies, you will learn each of these forms.
  • Tense: the time at which the verb occurs. Latin has six tenses: Present, Imperfect, Future, Perfect, Pluperfect, Future Perfect
  • Mood: classifications that denote the attitude of the speaker. Latin has three moods: Indicative, Imperative, Subjunctive.
  • Voice: the relationship between the subject and the verb. Latin has two voices: Active, Passive.
  • Conjugation: the inflection of the verb. Latin has four conjugations: 1st conjugation, 2nd conjugation, 3rd/3rd-io, 4th.
Latin Verb Tenses

Verb tenses are placed into two buckets:

Bucket 1: Present System = Present Tense, Imperfect Tense, Future Tense

Bucket 2: Perfect System = Perfect Tense, Pluperfect Tense, Future Perfect Tense

Present System, 1st/2nd Conjugation

Present System: Uses the 2nd principal part of the verb to conjugate and add the stem vowel:

  • 1st Conjugation (-a)
  • 2nd Conjugation (-e)
  • The stem formations look mostly the same as 1st and 2nd conjugations, but 3rd and 4th conjugations are different in the Present and Future tenses.

1st and 2nd Conjugation verbs use these endings:

2nd Singular -s -bas -bis
3rd Singular -t -bat -bit
1st Plural -mus -bamus -bimus
2nd Plural -tis -batis -bitis
3rd Plural -nt -bant -bunt
