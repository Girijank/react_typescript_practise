Juno's Festival

I am a champion of women's rights and empowerment. I comforted women during their marriages and childbirth, and I served as a guardian angel for married women. Every year on March 1st, Roman women marched to the Temple of Juno on the Capitoline Hill to worship me. Feminae mihi dona, oves et boves dant. They also conducted rituals at my temple.

Every year on July 7th, in another festival, the Nonae Caprotinae, or "The Nones of the Wild Fig," women marched to the Campus Martius to make offerings and sacrifices to me in celebration of my protection over them.
