Review Infinitives and Imperatives

Infinitives

Infinitives

The infinitive is a verbal noun. The present infinitive is the second principal part of a verb. When you encounter these, translate them as "to (verb)" (e.g. amare = to love and monere = to warn).

Complementary Infinitive: an infinitive used to complete an action of the sentence

Latin examples:

  • Libros legere amo. I love to read books.
Imperatives

Imperatives

The imperative mood is used to form 2nd person singular or plural commands.

The SINGULAR imperative is formed by dropping the -re from the 2nd principal part.

Examples:

Dictionary Entry Singular Imperative
amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um to love ama (love!)
doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um to teach doce (teach!)
mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um to send mitte (send!)
capio, capere, cepi, captus, -a, -um to seize cape (seize!)
audio, audire, audivi, auditus, -a, -um to hear audi (hear!)

The plural imperative is formed by dropping the -re from the 2nd principal part and adding -te to the stem. Note that in the 3rd conjugation, we change the last -e- in the present stem into an -i prior to adding -te.

Examples:

Dictionary Entry Plural Imperative
amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um to love amate (love!)
doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um to teach docete (teach!)
mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um to send mittite (send!)
capio, capere, cepi, captus, -a, -um to seize capite (seize!)
audio, audire, audivi, auditus, -a, -um to hear audite (hear!)

Latin examples:

  • Romani, laudate Caesarem! Romans, praise Caesar!
  • Da mihi librum! Give the book to me!
Irregular imperatives

Irregular Imperatives

Some verbs have irregular imperative forms that you must memorize.

Dictionary Entry Irregular Singular Imperative
duco, ducere, duxi, ductus, -a, -um to lead duc (lead!)
fero, ferre, tuli, latus, -a, -um to bring fer (bring!)
dico, dicere, dixi, dictus,-a, -um to say dic (say!)
facio, facere, feci, factus, -a, -um to do fac (do!)

Latin examples:

  • Populos duc! Lead the people!
  • Ad agricolas dic! Speak to the farmers!
