Fabula Fabulosa : Apollo and Cassandra

Apollo was always looking for love. Unfortunately, love was not always in the cards for Apollo. Translate this story about what happened to the Trojan princess Cassandra when Apollo pursued her.

Cassandra est filia regis Troiani et sarcerdos Apollinis.
Apollo amat eam et ei donum dat: ea futurum videre potest.

Cassandra is the daughter of the Trojan king and the priestess of Apollo.

Apollo loves her and gives her a gift: she is able to see the future.

Cassandra Apollinem amare recusat et Apollo Cassandrae poenam dat:
Ea futurum cognoscit, sed nemo verba Cassandrae credere potest.

Cassandra refuses to love Apollo, and Apollo gives Cassandra a punishment:

She knows the future, but no one can believe Cassandra’s words.

Vita eius est plena tragediae et doloris.
Cassandra suum patrem, suam matrem, suos fratres et suam patriam in Bello Troiano amisit.

Her life is full of tragedy and sadness.

Cassandra lost her father, her mother, her brothers, and her homeland.

Use this vocabulary to help you translate the sentences in the given passage.

sacerdos, sacerdotis m. (priest)

tragedia, tragediae f. (tragedy)

dolor, doloris m. (pain, grief)

credo, credere, credidi, creditus,-a,-um (to trust, believe)
