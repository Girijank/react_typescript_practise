Fabula Fabulosa: Baby Apollo and the Giant Python

Listen to the story about how Apollo slayed a giant python!

Etiam ego sum pugnator, like my twin sister, Diana. Before I was born, my father, Jupiter, had an affair with my mother, Leto. Juno, of course, was extremely upset, and so she sent down a giant python to chase Leto around. Another giant snake story! So, Jupiter had to turn my mother into a quail to give birth to me and my sister, Diana. After I was born, I wanted to find that python and take revenge on it! Only four days old, I gathered up my bow and golden arrows, quas meus avunculus Vulcanus pro me fecerat, and I went searching for the python. Take that, Mercury; you are not the only strong little baby god! I found the python in his cave and shot my arrows at it. It put up a good fight at first, hissing at me loudly and trying to swallow me, but it was no match for Baby Apollo! I killed it! And it Iet out the loudest scream this side of Parnassus. Then I picked up my lyre and strummed beautiful notes on it as my victory song. I slay all day!

Cogitemus!

After slaying the giant python, Apollo founded the Pythian Games, which were part of the Greek Panhellenic Games that are still celebrated today! The Pythian Games included competitions for art and dance, naturally.
