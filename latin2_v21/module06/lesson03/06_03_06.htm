Future Tense Main “Sensory” Verb in Indirect Statement

Let’s learn how to translate the indirect statement when the main “sensory” verb is in the present tense. You will want to have your infinitive tenses by your side!

Present infinitives occur at the same time as the action of the main verb.

Perfect infinitives occur at the time before the action of the main verb occurs.

Future infinitives occur at the time after the action of the main verb occurs.

If the Main “Sensory” Verb is… And the Infinitive is… Then you Translate the Infinitive as… Sentence Example Main Verb-Infinitive Relationship
Future Tense Present Tense Active Voice: is verbing Dicet virum feminam amare.

He will say that he is loving the woman.

 Same Time
Passive Voice: is being verbed Dicet feminam ab viro amare.

He will say that the woman is being loved by the man.
Future Tense Perfect Tense Active Voice: verbed Dicet virum feminam amavisse.

He will say that man loved the woman.

 Time Before
Passive Voice: has been verbed Dicet feminam ab viro amatam esse.

He will say that the woman has been loved by the man.
Future Tense Future Tense Active Voice: will love Dicet virum feminam amaturum esse.

He will say that the man will love the woman.

 Time After
Passive Voice: will be loved Dicet feminam ab viro amatum iri.

He says that the woman will be loved by the man.

Nota bene:

Notice that this chart uses the same infinitive translations as the present tense main “sensory” verb chart!
