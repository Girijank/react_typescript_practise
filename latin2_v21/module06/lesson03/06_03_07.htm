Translating Indirect Statements: Future Tense Main "Sensory" Verb

Let’s break down how to translate an indirect statement when our main “sensory” verb is in the future tense. You can use these translation steps for indirect statement to guide your process.

Example: Translation Steps
Incolae videbunt [vorticem naves in saxa rumpere].
The inhabitants will see that[the whirlpool is breaking the ships].
  1. Introduced by the "sensory" verb videbunt, the tense of the main verb is future—"The inhabitants will see…"
  2. Insert the English word “that.”
  3. Vorticem is the Accusative subject—"The inhabitants will see that the whirlpool…"
  4. The infinitive rumpere is in the present tense—same time as the main verb—"The inhabitants will see… the whirlpool is breaking…"
  5. The 2nd Accusative is naves.
  6. The other case is: in saxa = against the rocks.

Now let’s look at each time sequencing –same time,time before, and time after.

Present Infinitive: Same Time
Main “Sensory” Verb Tense Latin Sentence English Translation Infinitive Tense + Voice
Future Tense Incolae videbunt vorticem naves rumpere. The inhabitants will see that the whirlpool is breaking the ships. Present Active
Future Tense Incolae videbunt naves ab vortice rumpi. The inhabitants will see that the ships are being broken by the whirlpool. Present Passive
Perfect Infinitive: Time Before
Main “Sensory” Verb Tense Latin Sentence English Translation Infinitive Tense + Voice
Future Tense Incolae videbunt vorticem naves rupisse. The inhabitants will see that the whirlpool was breaking the ships. Perfect Active
Future Tense Incolae videbunt naves ab vortice ruptas esse. The inhabitants will see that the ships were being broken by the whirlpool. Perfect Passive
Future Infinitive: Time After
Main “Sensory” Verb Tense Latin Sentence English Translation Infinitive Tense + Voice
Future Tense Incolae videbunt vorticem naves rupturum esse. The inhabitants will see that the whirlpool will break the ships. Future Active
Future Tense Incolae videbunt naves ab vortice ruptum iri. The inhabitants will see that the ships will be broken by the whirlpool. Future Passive
Example 1
Sentence Latin English
Main independent clause + sensory verb: Nauta audiet [monstrum aves devorare.] The sailor will hear that the monster is devouring the birds.
Main independent clause + sensory verb: Nauta audiet
audiet = future tense		 The sailor will hear
Indirect statement clause: monstrum aves devorare the monster is devouring the birds
Accusative-subject monstrum the monster
Accusative D.O. + other cases/adverbs aves = receives the action of devorare the birds
Infinitive tense, voice, and time devorare = present Active infinitive = same time is devouring
Example 2
Sentence Latin English
Viri sentient [deos omen ad omnes misisse.] The men will realize that the gods have sent an omen to all...
Main independent clause + sensory verb: Viri sentient
sentient = future tense		 The men will realize
Indirect statement clause: deos omen ad omnes misisse the gods have sent an omen to all
Accusative-subject deos the gods
Accusative D.O. + other cases/adverbs omen
other cases: ad omnes = prepositional phrase 		an omen
Infinitive tense, voice, and time misisse = perfect Active infinitive = time before have sent
Example 3
Sentence Latin English
Senator putabit [se magnum ducem futurum esse.] The senator will think that he himself will be a great leader.
Main independent clause + sensory verb: Senator putabit
putabit = future tense		 The senator will think
Indirect statement clause: se magnum ducem futurum esse he himself will be a great leader.
Accusative-subject se he himself
Accusative D.O. + other cases/adverbs Accusative D.O. = magnum ducem = receives the action of esse a great leader
Infinitive tense, voice, and time futurum esse = future Active infinitive = time after will be

Translate these indirect statement sentences into English. Remember to pay attention to the tense of the infinitive!

Poetae dicent rem publicam inter imperium Augusti per mutatam esse.

Poets will say that [the republic was changed during the reign of Augustus]

Mei amici in Croatia vovent suas rates navales nos in itinere nostro constituras esse.

My friends in Croatia will vow that [their naval boats will not stop us on our journey].

Cras gladiator sperabit leonem in eum in certamine non desilere.

Tomorrow the gladiator will hope that [the lion does not jump down onto him in the contest].

Marius, who has a large army, will threaten that [Rome will be fought by his soldiers and weapons].
