If I were a patron in Ancient Rome…

Every patron needs his crew—those artists whom he supports and who produce great works of art for him and the nation! In Rome, Maecenas was the best patron in the biz! He had the greatest writers in his crew: Horace, Vergil, Propertius, Varius, and even Augustus for that matter. He had a keen eye for talent.

It’s important to always surround yourself with the best people and best talent. So, if you were an aspiring patron and knew that you wanted the right crew of artists to showcase to the world and who would help you build clout as an artistic patron, whom would you choose? Here’s your chance to draft a crew composed of artists from both ancient Rome and from the modern day to design one dream patron posse!

My Dream Circle of Artists

It’s patron posse draft day, and I am selecting the perfect artists to be members of my crew. There are a few poets whom I’ve been eyeing around Rome. I’ve heard their speeches in the Forum Romanum; I’ve listened to bards recite their lyrics around town; I’ve read selections of their works at my luxurious villa in Apulia. I’m selecting four artists who are innovative, inspiring, ingenious, and who I, as their patron, will help make household names in Rome.

Gallus has been writing the most transformational love elegies, a new poetic genre that I believe will take Rome by storm. If I get him in my crew, then I can recruit other poets like Varrus, Propertius, and Tibullus who could mimic his style and craft poetry, the likes of which Rome has never seen. Then, we will be our own supportive network, our own private club of the most intellectually bold artists, capable of infusing Greek Alexandrian poetic stylistic nuances into our own individualized and personalized Roman flavor to write poetry the Romans are yearning for. Scio Gallum magnam gratiam apud futuros scriptiones Horatii et Vergilii tenturum esse.

Then there’s Catullus. You see Catullus has me thinking about finding the one—that special lady, like his Lesbia, about whom I’d even compose elegiac couplets. Catullus is most awe-inspiring, but between you and me, I also think he needs some new friends to hang out with.

Then, here’s the dark horse no one sees coming. I’m selecting the biographer Cornelius Nepos to my crew. Romani sentiebant Nepotem modo poeticae Catulli et Galli non scripsisse. However, I need someone to provide in-depth research about the times in which we live, because to me, that’s what will bring gravitas to the verses of Catullus and Gallus. In turn, Nepos will be inspired to be more whimsical in his biographical works. Catullus might even one day dedicate his poems to him.

Now for my future artist pick. I have just the perfect lyricist, musician, performer, and all around ingénue to join my crew—Prince! The majestic purple one, who hails from the future city of Minneapolis, Minnesota, with his funky musical style, heart-piercing lyrics, and mind-mesmerizing sound, will round out our collective artistic rhythm. Known for his performances, Prince will also teach my artists how to have their audiences hanging onto their every word.

I can see it now! My circle of artists will perform at every festival, contest, and country villa. There’s no stopping us! Sperabo me patronum divitissimum esse.
