Step 2:

Write your patron posse report. Utilize your research within previous lessons and the research you conducted on your own. You can use the sample report, “My Dream Circle of Artists” as a creative guide. Be sure to include the following:

  • Write the report in English, in 400–450 words, using the details from your research chart (see step 1).
  • Use three sentences, written in Latin, embedded into your story. See step 3 below for Latin sentence specific guidelines.
  • Give a citation of the sources you used for your research, using the MLA format.

Step 3:

Latin Writing Assignment: Supply the three sentences in Latin from your narrative, along with their English translations.

  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with a present tense main “sensory” verb.
  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with an imperfect/perfect tense main “sensory” verb.
  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with a future tense main “sensory” verb.

Nota bene:

For each of your three Latin sentences, you can use any infinitive tense in your dependent Adjective-infinitive clause; just make sure your translation reflects the correct tense!

Example Sentences:

Sentence 1: Scio Gallum magnam gratiam apud futuros scriptiones Horatii et Vergilii tenturum esse.

➤English: I know that Gallus will have great influence on the future writings of Horace and Vergil.

➤indirect statement with a present tense main “sensory” verb

Sentence 2: Romani sentiebant Nepotem modo poeticae Catulli et Galli non scripsisse.

➤English: The Romans felt that Nepos had not written in the fashion of the poetry of Catullus and Gallus.

➤indirect statement with an imperfect/perfect tense main “sensory” verb

Sentence 3: Sperabo me patronum divitissimum esse.

➤English: I shall hope that I am the richest patron!

➤indirect statement with a future tense main “sensory” verb
