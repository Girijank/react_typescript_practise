Step 3: Write three sentences in Latin, and infuse them into your report. Remember:

You will be writing three sentences in Latin.

  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with a present tense main “sensory” verb.
  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with an imperfect/perfect tense main “sensory” verb.
  • One sentence must contain an indirect statement with a future tense main “sensory” verb..

Your sentences should be simple and short, reflecting your level of Latin proficiency. Provide English translations with these Latin sentences below your report.

➤ Latin sentence 1: Write something that you think is important about one of your artists.

Example: Scio Gallum magnam gratiam apud futuros scriptiones Horatii et Vergilii tenturum esse.

➤ English: I know that Gallus will have great influence on the future writings of Horace and Vergil.

Remember:

to form the indirect statement according to its grammar rules:

  • Write the main sentence first, using your sensory verb as the main verb.
  • Write the adjective-infinitive clause using this formula:
    • subject = Accusative case
    • infinitive = any tense
    • direct object = Accusative case
➤ Latin sentence 2: Write something that the Roman public thought about one of your artists.

Example: Romani sentiebant Nepotem modo poeticae Catulli et Galli non scripsisse.

  • English: The Romans felt that Nepos had not written in the fashion of the poetry of Catullus and Gallus.
 Remember:

When translating your sentence, add the connecting “that” between your main sentence and the adjective-infinitive clause, and then follow your sequence of tense rules of translation.

➤ Latin sentence 3: Write something you hope for as patron to these artists.

Example: Sperabo me patronum divitissimum esse.

  • English: I shall hope that I am the richest patron!
Remember:

The tense of the infinitive in the dependent clause depends on the tense of your sensory verb in the main sentence.

  • present tense infinitive = same time as the main verb
  • perfect tense infinitive = time before the main verb
  • future tense infinitive = time after the main verb
Nota bene: Did you proofread your work and cite your sources? Remember:

Every sentence should use correct punctuation, grammar, and syntax. All research sources should be cited, using the MLA format.
