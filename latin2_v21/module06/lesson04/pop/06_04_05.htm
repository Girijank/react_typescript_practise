How to translate an Indirect Statement Translation Guide:

Step 1: Main Sentence: This has the main “sensory” verb in it. Translate it like any normal Latin sentence.

Step 2:: Insert the English word “that.”

  • Latin will not include a word for “that” in an indirect statement sentence.

Step 3: Now translate the first Accusative you see after the main “sensory” verb.

  • This begins the Accusative-infinitive clause

Step 4: Translate the infinitive (which is normally located at the very end of the entire sentence)

Step 5: Translate the 2nd Accusative (if it exists) you see in the Accusative-infinitive clause.

Step 6: Translate any other case in the Accusative-infinitive clause to complete your translation.
