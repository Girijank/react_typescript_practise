Fabula Fabulosa: “The Willow Wife” (A Japanese Folk Story)

Many cultures shared similar mythological stories. That’s the magic of the world—that we share so many traits, languages, folklore, and origin stories! You know that the Roman Empire expanded into Asia and Africa, and the Asian and African Empires expanded into Europe. So, it’s no wonder that we find the “same” folklore story in various cultures, just with different names and a different twist! Read this Japanese folk story entitled “The Willow Wife,” and think if you see any similarities to the “Apollo and Daphne” story.

In oppido parvo erat pulchra salix. Quotidie Hiroshi, iuvenis puer, prope arborem ambulabat. “Arbor tam pulchra est!” putabat. Olim incolae oppidi salicem caedere cupiebant. Hiroshi erat tristissimus. Incolis clamabat: “Linquite salicem! Curabo arborem.” Itaque multos annos arbor incolumis manebat.

In a small town, there was a beautiful willow tree. Everyday, Hiroshi, a young boy, walked near the tree. “The tree is so beautiful!” he thought.

One day the inhabitants of the town wished to cut down the willow tree. Hiroshi was very sad. He shouted to the inhabitants: “Leave the willow tree alone! I will take care of the tree.” And so, for many years, the tree remained unharmed.

Ubi Hiroshi vir erat, videbat feminam prope salicem. Femina, Kaori nomine, erat formosa et mollis; Hiroshi feminam amat: “Semper te curabo,” dixit. Tum feminam in matrimonium ducebat. Multos annos laete habitabant.

When Hiroshi was a man, he saw a woman near the willow tree. The woman, Kaori by name, was beautiful and gentle; Hiroshi loved the woman: “I will always take care of you!” Then he led the woman into matrimony. They lived happily for many years.

Sed olim imperator iubebat populum omnes arbores donare. Cupiebat magna templa aedificare. Incola arbores caedere incipiebat. Incolae pulchram salicem caedunt. Interim Kaori exclamat “Eheu! Bracchia mea sunt in dolore!” Audiunt fragorem immanem! Subito misera femina vanescit!

But one day, the emperor ordered the people to cut down all the trees. He wished to build great temples. The inhabitants began to cut the trees. The inhabitants cut the beautiful willow tree. Meanwhile, Kaori exclaimed, “Oh no! My arms are in pain!” They heard a huge crash! Suddenly the poor woman vanished!

Hiroshi nunc intellegit. Uxor erat anima salicis. Hiroshi erat tristissimus et semper pulchram salicem, pulchram uxorem in memoria tenebat.

Hiroshi now understands. His wife was the spirit of the willow tree. Hiroshi was very sad and always held the beautiful tree, his beautiful wife, in his memory.
