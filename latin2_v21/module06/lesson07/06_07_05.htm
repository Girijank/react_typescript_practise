Cicero’s Speech Against Cataline!

While consul, Cicero thwarted one of the most egregious assaults on the Roman Republic. This event became known as the Cataline Conspiracies.

The Response

In a speech delivered in the Senate, Cicero would denounce Cataline’s plan and attack Cataline’s character in one of the all-time greatest speeches of mankind! It was so juicy! It is entitled In Catalinam.

Listen to and read below an excerpt from Cicero’s speech against Cataline.

O tempora! O mores! Senatus haec intellegit, consul videt; hic tamen vivit. Vivit? Immo vero etiam in senatum venit, fit publici consili particeps, notat et designat oculis ad caedem unum quemque nostrum. Nos autem, fortes viri, satis facere rei publicae videmur, si istius furorem ac tela vitamus.
Ad mortem te, Catilina, duci iussu consulis iam pridem oportebat, in te conferri pestem quam tu in nos omnis iam diu machinaris.

Oh, the times! Oh, the customs! The Senate understands these things; the consul sees these things. Yet he lives. He lives? For crying out loud, he even comes into the Senate, he comes as a participant in the public decision making, he nods and points out with his eyes for each to chop down every one of us. However, we, brave men, seem to do enough for the Republic if we forbid the madness and arms of that one.

To death, Catiline, it was long since important that you be led by the command of the consul; you ought to have been brought as a plague which you have been devising for a long time onto all of us.
