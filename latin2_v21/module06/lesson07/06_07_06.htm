Roman Oration Infusion

Many of the greatest modern orators have been heavily influenced by Roman and Greek orators. Now that you know what it takes to write a great speech, and you have read a portion of one of Rome’s greatest speeches, let’s visit a speech written by Frances Ellen Watkins, a full-time abolitionist speaker for the Maine Anti-Slavery Society to identify its Roman oratorical characteristics.

Speech Analysis of “Liberty for Slaves.” by Frances Ellen Watkins

Can you identify the Roman oratorical techniques in her speech?

Read through this speech from Frances Ellen Watkins and locate the elements of an effective speech, as identified by the Romans: exordium, narratio, propositio, confirmatio, refutatio, and peroratio.
