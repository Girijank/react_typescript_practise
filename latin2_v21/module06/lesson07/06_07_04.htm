Roman Speeches

The Romans constructed their speeches depending on the speech’s purpose. Let's take a look at how these speeches were put together.

Type of Speeches

There were three types of speeches an orator would give in Rome:

  1. Judicial legal speeches
  2. Deliberative speeches advocating for a course of action
  3. Demonstrative speeches of blame or praise

Places to Conduct Speeches

  1. Roman Forum: After school education, a young citizen would practice their oratorical skills in the courts in the Forum.
  2. Curia: Young citizens would conduct minor speeches in front of the Senate.
  3. Comitia: The Assembly House where Roman youth would give speeches is called a contio.

Speech Construction

Judicial speeches generally followed this pattern:

  1. Exordium: The introduction is first.
  2. Narratio: The speaker gives the facts of the argument.
  3. Propositio: The speaker states what is agreed upon and what is being contested.
  4. Confirmatio: Supportive evidence for the argument is presented.
  5. Refutatio: Argument against the opposition is presented.
  6. Peroratio: Conclusion and emotional appeal to the audience is last.
