Circle of Maecenas Unite!

Do you want to join the M-Circle? Let’s learn more about the poetic members of the circle of Maecenas.

Vergil

After writing the Bucolics, a collection of pastoral poems set in the pristine Roman countryside, I attracted the attention of Maecenas. I then became the most distinguished writer in the “circle of Maecenas,” and Caesar Augustus commissioned me to write the Aeneid! What an honor?? It took me 11 years to write it!! Etiam alia opera such as the Georgics, a collection of poems about agriculture, which I supposedly read at the bedside of a sick Augustus, who used my work to bolster his agricultural policy. My Georgics also contained a dedicatory prayer to Augustus.

Horace

I was an “Augustan poet” who lived from 65 BCE–8 BCE. My father provided me with a very strict education, which later in life prompted me to write in the genre of satire. I also wrote great works such as the Odes, Carmen Saeculare, and Ars Poetica. The poets, Vergil and Varro introduced me to Maecenas, cui magnus amicus factus sum!

Propertius

Aetatem Augustam elegos scripsi. I was supported by Maecenas but did not particularly write favorable lines about Augustus in my later poetry. In my earlier works, I wrote poetry that praised Augustus and his military accomplishments. However, I did not believe in Augustus’s pax Romana agenda of total subjugation of Rome’s enemies, and therefore in Book IV of my elegies, I wrote both pro and anti-sentiments of Augustus. Oops!
