Present Tense Main “Sensory” Verb in Indirect Statement

Let’s learn how to translate the indirect statement when the main “sensory” verb is in the present tense. You will want to have your infinitive tenses by your side!

Present infinitives occur at the same time as the action of the main verb.

Perfect infinitives occur at the time before the action of the main verb occurs.

Future infinitives occur at the time after the action of the main verb occurs.

If the Main “Sensory” Verb is… And the Infinitive is… Then you Translate the Infinitive as… Sentence Example Main Verb-Infinitive Relationship
Present Tense Present Tense Active Voice: is verbing Dicit virum feminam amare.

He says that he is loving the woman.

 Same Time
Passive Voice: is being verbed Dicit feminam ab viro amari.

He says that the woman is being loved by the man.
Present Tense Perfect Tense Active Voice: have/has verbed Dicit virum feminam amavisse.

He says that the man has loved the woman.

 Time Before
Passive Voice: has being verbed Dicit feminam ab viro amatam esse.

He says that the woman has been loved by the man.
Present Tense Future Tense Active Voice: will verb Dicit virum feminam amaturum esse..

He says that the man will love the woman.

 Time After
Passive Voice: will be verbed Dicit feminam ab viro amatum iri..

He says that the woman will be loved by the man.

Nota bene:

Because the Perfect Passive Participle (PPP) acts like an adjective, the Perfect Passive infinitive and the Future Active Infinitive will always match with the Accusative subject of the Accusative-infinitive clause in case, number, and gender.
