Vergil, the Poet

Practice the indirect statement using this passage about the Augustan poet Vergil. You can check your translation using the translation globes.

Hoc anno, iam cognoveras multum de poeta famoso Vergilio et poemate eius, Aeneade.
Antequam fit amicus bonus Imperatori Augusto, iam scripsit Eclogas Georgicaque.

This year, you have already learned much about the famous poet Vergil and his poem the Aeneid.

Before he became good friends to the Emperor Augustus, he had already written the Eclogues and the Georgics.

Eclogae erant poemata de vitis pastorum agricolarumque qui quosque amant et terras suas amiserunt.
Georgica erant poemata quattor librorum de arte colendi.

The Eclogues were poems about the lives of shepherds and farmers who fall in love with each other and lose their lands.

The Georgics was a poem of four books about the art of farming.

Augustus Vergilem rogavit Aeneidem scribere quae gloriam et historiam Romanorum demonstravit.
Vergilius multum poema laboravit sed finire non potuit antequam mortuus est.
Rogavit Aeneidem perditum esse sed Augustus recusavit atque possumus legere hoc opus gloriosum.

Augustus asked Vergil to write the Aeneid, which showed the glory and history of the Romans.

Vergil worked a lot on the poem but was not able to finish it before he died.

He asked for the Aeneid to be destroyed, but Augustus refused, and so we are still able to read this amazing work.
