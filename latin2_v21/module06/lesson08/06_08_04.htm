Oration: Cicero Versus Mark Antony

As a review in Roman oration and translating, let’s translate this speech by Cicero from Cicero’s Philippics 13.4. Cicero and Mark Antony had become enemies. Thus, Cicero used his power of oration to condemn Mark Antony in a series of fourteen speeches called the Philippics. Yes, that Mark Antony! Cicero was later killed as a result of these speeches. For this passage, use your Latin Toolbox and Glossary to guide you.

Philippics 13.4

O fidam dexteram Antoni, qua ille plurimos civis trucidavit, o ratum religiosumque foedus, quod cum Antoniis fecerimus!

Oh, faithful righthand of Antony by which he slaughtered several citizens; oh, the fixed and religious treaty which we made with Antony!

hoc si Marcus violare conabitur, Luci eum sanctitas a scelere revocabit. illis locus si in hac urbe fuerit, ipsi urbi locus non erit.

If Marcus will try to violate this, the sanctity of Lucius will recall him from this crime. If there will be a place for them in this city, there will not be a place for the city itself.

Ora vobis eorum ponite ante oculos et maxime Antoniorum; incessum, aspectum, voltum, spiritum, latera tegentis alios, alios praegredientis amicos.

Place before your eyes, the faces of them and especially of the Antonii; their walk, their look, their expression, their arrogance, some walking by the side of their friends, others walking in front of their friends.

quem vini anhelitum, quas contumelias fore censetis minasque verborum !

What breath reeks of wine, what arrogance and what threat of language do you think there to be!
