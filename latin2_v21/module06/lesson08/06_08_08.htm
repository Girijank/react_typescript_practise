Sulpicia

Translate this passage about the Roman female poet, Sulpicia, born in 40 BCE. You will submit your work to your instructor for a grade. Make sure to use the Translation Help and New Vocabulary below!

Latin Sentences and Questions for Submission

Latin Sentences (Translate from Latin to English.),

  1. Didicimus multos poetas in Roma antiqua scribere.
  2. Paene omnes poetarum erant viri.
  3. Tamen, putamus feminas Romanas poemata etiam scripsisse.
  4. Habemus opera unae poetae feminae, autem, nomine Sulpicia.
  5. Poemata Sulpiciae in scriptis Tibulli inventa erant et inclusa erant.
  6. Dicitur Sulpiciam tempore Augusti vixisse, sed triste scimus paene nihil de vita eius.
  7. Habemus sex poemata scripta amatoris eius, Cerinthi.
  8. De identitate Sulpiciae Cerinthique erant multi rationes; nihil quarum demonstrari possunt.

Grammar and Comprehension Questions (Answer in 1–2 sentences.),

  1. Is the action of scribere happening before, at the same time, or after the action of didicimus in #1?
  2. Is the action of scripsivisse happening before, at the same time or after the action of putamus in #3?
  3. List three Latin sensory verbs found in this passage.
  4. List three things that we know about Sulpicia from this passage.

Short Essay (Answer in 4–5 sentences.):

  1. The passage mentions that Sulpicia lived during the time of Augustus. Name three other poets who lived during the time of Augustus.
  2. Explain how you translated Line 6: Dicitur Sulpiciam tempore Augusti vixisse, sed triste scimus paene nihil de vita eius. In three to four full sentences, describe the translation steps you took to translate this sentence. In your answer, be sure to include how you found and translated the subject(s) and verb(s). If this contains an indirect statement(s), don't forget to discuss the components of the indirect statement!

Translation Help and New Vocabulary

Vocabulary Aid

  • Indirect Statements
  • Remember to look out for sensory verbs! They often denote the beginning of an indirect statement. Also remember that a 3rd person singular sensory verb can be translated with the pronoun “it.” Example: dicitur = It is said.
  • poema, poematis, n.. poem
  • scribo, scribere, scripsi, scriptus, to write
  • Sulpicia, Sulpiciae, f. , Sulpicia (Roman poet from the 1st century BCE)
  • tempore: at the time (Ablative of time “when”)
  • Tibullus, Tibulli, m. Albius Tibullus (Roman poet from the 1st century BCE)
  • includo, includere, inclusi, inclusus, to include
  • tempore Augusti during the time of Augustus
  • vivo, vivere, vixi, victus, to live
  • amator, amatoris, m. lover
  • Cerinthus, Cerinthi, m. Cerinthus (the “lover” in Sulpicia’s poems)
  • volo, volere = to wish, desire
  • ratio, rationis, f. theory
  • identitas, identitatis, f, identity

Translation Checklist

  • Have you identified the tense, voice, person, number, and mood of the verb?
  • Have you identified the case, number, and gender for the nouns and adjectives?
  • Do your English definitions for each of the words in the Latin translation match the definitions found in the toolbox dictionary?
  • Have you double-checked your Latin versus English word order?
    • HINT: In Latin, adjectives follow the nouns they modify, Genitive nouns follow the nouns they possess, and verbs are typically found at the end of the sentence.
  • Did you transfer all punctuation from Latin to English?
