Indirect Statement Construction

In the previous pages, we learned what an indirect statement is. Let’s break it down even further.

An indirect statement is a statement that is being made by a person other than the original speaker. Thus, the original speaker is indirectly making the statement.

English Construction

In English, after verbs of thinking, knowing, hearing, perceiving, etc., if words are not quoted directly (direct statement), an indirect statement clause is often introduced by the word “that.”

For example:

Direct Statement Indirect Statement

He says, "The boy is washing the dishes."

He says that the boy is washing the dishes.

Nota bene:

In this sentence there are two clauses:

"He says" (independent clause because it can stand by itself) and "that the boy is washing the dishes." (dependent clause—makes no sense without the first clause)
