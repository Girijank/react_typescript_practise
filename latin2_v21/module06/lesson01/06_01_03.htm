Indirect Statement

To fully understand the genius of my favorite poets, it is important to know the grammatical structure called the indirect statement. You will see this grammar construct in most types of Roman writing.

Direct Statement:

A direct statement is directly completed by the subject of the sentence. These are your normal, everyday sentences.

Examples:
  • The man loves the woman.
  • The woman is loved by the man.

Indirect Statement:

An indirect statement is a statement that is being made by a person other than the original speaker.

The original speaker is indirectly making the statement.

Direct Statement versus Indirect Statement

See how the two compare and contrast.

Direct Statement Indirect Statement Speaker Difference
The man loves the woman.

Notice how the person speaking is different from the person speaking in the direct statement.

The poet says that the man loves the woman.

DS (Direct Statement) versus IS (Indirect Statement)

  • DS = The man
  • IS = The poet
I am a good student. My teachers recognize that I am a good student.
  • DS = I
  • IS = My teachers
The Latin language is great. FLVS students think that the Latin language is great.
  • DS = The Latin language
  • IS = FLVS students
