Sensory Verbs of Indirect Statement

  • If someone is thinking, saying, telling, showing, hearing (or doing anything that can take place (or sensing) in one’s head), then they can introduce an indirect statement. We call these verbs “sensory verbs.”
  • Think of other words in the same family: say, tell, report, announce, etc. You will notice that the word that naturally follows each one. For example:
    • I hear that...
    • I know that...
    • I feel that…
    • I say that…
  • The Latin sensory verbs that introduce indirect statements are listed in your vocabulary in this lesson. There are others to learn as well. I sense that you can match the sensory verbs to their corresponding pictures!

Apollo’s Aim: Four Principal Parts

It is extremely important that you know all the principal parts of these verbs. The indirect statement hinges on having the correct verb tense of the main verb. Take the time now to ensure that you have the four principal parts firmly in mind before going any further.
