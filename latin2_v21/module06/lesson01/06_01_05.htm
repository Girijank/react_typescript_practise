The Early Poets

Lucretius

Born on the cusp of the first century BCE, Lucretius was a follower of Epicurean philosophy, which teaches individuals how to live life happily in moderation. He wrote the de rerum Natura, a poem of about six books that explains the cosmic nature of life and death and the world of physics.

Tristissimum poema Catulli est de fratre suo, qui, dum in Asia erat, mortuus est. Catullus scripsit de se eunti usque Bithyniam in Troia ubi frater suus sepultus est. In poemate suo dixit de se stanti super cinerem mutam et de se danti lacrimas ut donum sepulchro fratris. Catullus erat unus ex poetis primis Romanis qui de experientiis suis scripserunt et poetas multos futuros inspiravit.

The saddest of the poems of Catullus is about his brother who died while in Asia.

Catullus writes about himself going all the way to up to Bithynia in Troy, where his brother was buried.

In his poem he spoke about himself standing over his brother’s mute ashes and giving his tears as a gift to his brother’s grave.

Catullus was one of the first Roman poets who wrote about their own experiences and inspired many future poets.
