Poet’s Corner

You are very familiar with Vergil’s epic poem the Aeneid, which was written during the age of Augustus. Let’s travel back 100 years before that, when the first Roman poets were inspired to write poetry.

First Roman Poets

➤ Lucius Livius Andronicus from southern Italy had his hand in transcribing the Odyssey into Latin after the 2nd Punic War.

➤ Then, there was Gnaeus Naevius, who wrote epic poetry in the third century BCE.

➤ The Roman poet Ennius, born around 239 BCE, is credited for being one of the first to write in poetic verse while trying to imitate Greek verse.

Straight from the Source!

O Tite tute Tati tibi tanta, turanne, tulisti.
Alteri sic tibi
Amicule, deliciae, num is sum qui mentiar tibi?
 (Ennius, Annals, lines 266–268)

O Titus Tatius, you tyrant, you have brought so much upon yourself.

Thus to another as to yourself

Baby, sweetheart, surely, I wouldn’t lie to you, right? (Ennius, Annals, lines 266–268)

The Path of Roman Poetry

By the end of the second century BCE, amateur groups of poets who regarded poetry as a pastime began to assemble in Rome. This led to a poetic revolution in the first century BCE in which a new group of poets called “novi poetae” or “neoterics” began to meet in groups and model the purpose and expression of their poetry after the third century BCE Greek Alexandrian poets and the Greek poet Callimachus.
