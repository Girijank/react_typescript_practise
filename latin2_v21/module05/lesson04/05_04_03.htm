Participle Practice!

A participle is a verbal adjective that is formed from the Latin verb but declined like a Latin adjective.

We have three participial tenses. Let’s see how the participle tenses look for the verb porto, portare, portavi, portatus,-a,-um = to carry.

  1. present Active: portans, portantis (second p. p.) carrying
  2. perfect Passive: portatus (fourth p.p.), carried
  3. future Active and Passive (In this module, we have only learned the future Active form.): portaturus (fourth p. p.), going to carry

Let’s Focus!

Present Participles

Present participles are formed from the second principal part. Remove the -re (porta-re)

  • Add -ns (for the nominative singular - easy to remember that way!) and -ntis for the genitive singular (portans, portantis). Translate it as "verb-ing".
    • Dux cadens: the falling leader
    • Lupa spirans: the breathing wolf
Caesar milites consilia facientes videt.

Caesar sees the men making plans.

Milites portantes scuta ad Caesarem cucurrerunt

The soldiers carrying the shields rushed towards Caesar.

Perfect Passive Participle

Perfect participles are formed from the fourth principal part (portatus, a, um, and monstratus, a, um) and are translated as "having been…" Examples: having been carried, having been shown.

  • Servus oppressus hopes for freedom. (The oppressed slave hopes for freedom.)
  • Miles liberatus shouted for joy in front of his former master's house. (answer: The freed soldier)
  • The field marshal took pity upon the puerum superatum. (answer: the overpowered boy)
Puella ex aqua portata servata erat.

The girl carried from the water had been saved.

Omen amissum in populo, deos creare maleficium coegit.

The omen having been lost on the people, compelled the gods to create a wrongdoing.

Future Active Participles

Future participles are formed from the fourth principal part Add -ur- in front of the ending. examples: portatURus, a, um; monstratURus, a, um. The translation is "going to…" or "about to…"

  • Lupa spiratura: the wolf about to breathe
  • Puer puellam trans flumen portaturus est: The boy is going to carry the girl across the river.
Mater filiam visura est.

The mother is about to see the daughter.

Sumus visuri Forum

We are going to see the Forum.
