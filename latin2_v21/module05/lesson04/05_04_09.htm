"The Sorrows of Boudica" (Part I)

Listen to and translate this story about the Boudica Rebellion, led by a powerful female queen, which took place in the age of Nero.

The Sorrows of Boudica (Part I): Dolores Boudicae (Part I)

Illis hostibus ad litora proximorum advenientibus, ut puella timebam. (line 1)

Venerant ad Britanniam (nominaverunt nos Britannos sed sunt multi populi in hac insula). (line 2)

His Romanis iter ad terram nostram facientibus, rex Icenorum, maritus meus, Prasutagus, periculum sensit. (line 3)

Ergo pacem cum Romanis fecit. Permittebatur ei regere, adhuc rex. Regina eram. (line 4)

Ubi rex mortuus est, Caesarem et duas filias heredes scripserat. (line 5)

Sed Romani terras et viros et feminas et liberos, servos rapiebant; etiam me et filias meas. (line 6)

With those enemies arriving at the shores of our neighbors, as a girl, I was afraid (line 1).

They had come to Britannia. (they named us Britons, but there are many peoples in this island.) (line 2).

With these Romans marching towards our land, the king of Iceni, my husband, Prasutagus, realized the danger (line 3).

Therefore, he made peace with the Romans It was allowed for him to rule, still the king. I was queen (line 4).

When the king died, he had written Caesar and his two daughters as his heirs (line 5).

But the Romans kept on pillaging our lands and men, women, and children as slaves; they also seized me and my daughters (line 6).
