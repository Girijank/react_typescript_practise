Latin Speaks!

Latin is still the official language of Vatican City, Italy. Right outside this city’s boundaries is a country full of Italian–speaking individuals. This makes me wonder—could an ancient Roman and modern Italian speaker converse with one another today? Let’s try it out!

Enjoy this conversation below between a Roman girl named Marcia who is speaking in Latin, and an Italian girl named Marcella who is speaking in Italian.

Salve, Marcella!

Hello, Marcella!

Un saluto a te, Marcia!

Greetings to you, Marcia!.

Placet mihi occurrere tibi. Video te longa bracchia, manus, et collem habere.

It is pleasing for me to meet you. I see that you have long arms, hands, and neck.

Si, sono molto alto. Le mie braccia, le mani e il collo sono molto lunghi.

Yes, I am very tall. My arms, hands, and neck are very long.

Ego alta non sum. Sed oculos similes habemus. Et nos ambae aerem Romanam spiramus.

I am not tall. Yet we have similar eyes. And we both breathe Roman air!

Si!

Yes!

Diana’s Directive: Practice Makes Perfect

Speaking in Latin is a fun skill set to have. You too can become fluent in speaking Latin! First, join your local Latin Club where you can learn how to speak fluent Latin! Then, pick one day a week and speak only in Latin to your friends and family!

You can also join the National Junior Classical League to converse with other Latinist peers all over the country! It’ll be so much fun to do.
