In Tuo Orbe!

Check out these English phrases you probably know, which are really Latin phrases! Do you recognize these?

Vocabulary Part of Speech Definition Derivative Audio
ad hominem phrase to the individual person “The gladiator swung ad hominem attacks on me before we battled—he said he didn’t like the haircut I just got from a barber in Trajan’s Forum!” --
ad valorem phrase according to value “I calculated my ad valorem tax after I bought my new house— it was definitely according to value.” according to value
ad nauseam phrase to nausea “The presenter spoke about his graphs ad nauseam—we were nearly bored to death!” ad nauseam
de facto phrase from the fact “Although English is the de facto primary language taught in schools today, we believe Latin should be!” de facto
et cetera phrase and other things “My grocery list includes bananas, fruit cups, cheese, sweet potatoes, cranberry juice, collard greens, et cetera—if I named the entire list, it would take up an entire page!” et cetera
modus vivendi phrase way of living “My sister’s modus vivendi is so care-free that I’m surprised she decided to major in mathematics in college—she won’t have any free time now!” modus vivendi
per capita phrase by head “Apples are the number one fruit consumed per capita in the United States—I can count at least 20 people on my block who eat them daily!” per capita
per se phrase by himself/itself “Nerva wasn’t necessarily a great emperor per se—he just seemed great in comparison to his predecessor, the horrible Domitian!” per se
per diem phrase by the day “We asked our parents for a per diem allowance, to which they responded, “Do your daily chores and maybe we’ll agree to this!” per diem
quid pro quo phrase something for someone “The cleaner wrasse fish negotiated a quid pro quo with the shark—“I’ll clean your teeth in exchange for you not eating me!” quid pro quo
status quo phrase the situation in which “Our town’s government maintained the status quo by not proposing new ideas. On account of its stagnancy, I decided to relocate to a more progressive city.” --
