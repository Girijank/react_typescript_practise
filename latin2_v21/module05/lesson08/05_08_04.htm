Longe Vive Linguam Latinam!

The five major languages that we call “Romance” (coming from Rome) are Spanish, Portuguese, French, Italian, and Romanian. There are a ton of other minor languages as well, like Catalan, Aragonese, Ladin, Ladino, etc. These all share a general grammar and a large portion of their vocabulary which comes from Latin. With your work in Latin, for most of these languages, once you learn the conventions for pronunciation, you will be able to recognize lots of words in the modern languages.

Let’s see how these words took root from the original Latin into Romance languages!

Latin: aqua = water
Latin aqua
Spanish aqua
Italian acqua
Romanian apa
French eau
Portuguese agua
Latin: mater = mother
Latin mater
Spanish madre
Italian madre
Romanian mamă
French mère
Portuguese mãe

Take a look at these words from across the globe and see how they have come from Latin words! You’ll see that most of these modern words look very similar to their Latin roots—just perhaps with slight consonant or vowel changes.

Modern Word Modern Language Latin Original
casa (house) Spanish casa, casae f. = house
vater (father) German pater, patris m. = father
laborant (laboratory technician) German labor, laboris m. = laborer
sedia (chair) Italian sedeo, sedere = to sit
villagio (village) Italian villa, villae f. = farmhouse, villa
livre (book) French liber, libri m. = book
fille (girl) French filia, filiae f. = daughter
