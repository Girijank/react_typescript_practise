Iam me captiva, Romani me habent. Primum, tamen, magnum bellum cum Romanis gerebamus. Trinovantibus et aliis ab veteranis Romanis e domibus et agris pulsis, omnes nos, viri et feminae, iuvenes et senes, in Camulodunum descendimus et statuam Victoriae convertitimus et domus et aedificia ursimus et veteranos cecidimus, ut veterani nos. Deinde in Londinium, peiorem oppidum, et Verulamium iter fecimus et incolas e casis egimus.

Now with me as captive, the Romans hold me. However, we waged a great war with the Romans first. With the Tribantes and others having been driven from their homes and farms by the Roman veterans, we all, men and women, young and old, descended into Camulodunum, overturned the statue of Victory, and burned their houses and buildings, and slaughtered the veterans, just as the veterans had done to us. Then we made the journey against London, a rather small town, and Verulamium and drove the inhabitants from their houses.
