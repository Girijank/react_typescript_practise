A Changing Empire

Many more rulers ruled Rome as imperator. There were many more wars, internal civil conflicts, and crises the Romans had endured as well. There were 18 emperors who ruled in the mid-3rd century CE. Invasions by the Germans, revolts in Africa, and instability in Roman leadership will all change the composition of the Roman Empire and its operation.

The glory days of Rome were gone. Rome was now financially unstable, militarily insecure, and politically weak. Then, there was the rise of Christianity, a religion with which the Emperor Diocletian was in conflict...

Diocletian

Reign: 284 CE—305 CE. I am known for:

  • Palace of Diocletian in Croatia
  • “Great Persecution”

Diocletian

I, Diocletian, now Roman emperor, will try to bring stability back into Rome after all of the crises we have endured. Our empire is falling apart because it is too large. Thus, I will split the Empire into East and West regions. I myself will rule the East, and Maximian, the West.Tum, quisque nostrum virum regere praeter nos leget. This will be the first time that the Empire is ruled by four people! A tetrarchy, if you will. All was well, except I did not like the Christians or other minority groups, and so I began a brutal period of persecuting these people. I also stripped Christian soldiers and clergy of their rank and enslaved many of them. People called this the “Great Persecution,” which caused great strife within the Christian church. I eventually abdicated to retire in the Roman provincial town of Split, Croatia.

Let’s see architectural masterpieces that Diocletian built!

Palace of Diocletian, Croatia

Located in the modern city of Split, Croatia, this palace built for Diocletian was constructed in the 4th century CE. This colossal palace, built like a fortress, contained multiple residences for Diocletian’s personal use and military garrison, but it was unfinished at the time of his death. It is now a UNESCO world heritage site.

Mausoleum of Diocletian, Croatia

Retiring in Split, Croatia, Diocletian had this mausoleum built as his resting home. Diocletian died in 312 BCE at the age of 66. His mausoleum is now the cathedral of Saint Domnius and is a UNESCO world heritage site.

Baths of Diocletian (Thermae Diocletiani), Rome

Located in Rome and dedicated in 306 CE, the bath complex was the largest imperial baths structure in the Empire. Spanning over 13 hectares, it contained a frigidarium, caldarium, tepidarium, a theatre, gymnasium, library, gardens, and a 3,500-foot swimming pool. It could house up to 3,000 people at one time. It was made of brick, laid with a stucco outer surface.
