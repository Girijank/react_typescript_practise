Step-by-Step Guide to Translating a Latin Sentence

You now have many tools to translate longer Latin sentences. Here are quick tips to help guide you:

  • If you have an interrogative pronoun or adjective or a question word in the sentence, translate that first.
  • If you have a sentence mixed with an independent and a relative clause, mark off the relative clause with brackets and insert it into the translation of the independent clause, and then translate the independent clause.
  • If you have a sentence with an Ablative Absolute, translate that first.

Step 1: Translate the Nominative Case: the noun. (If there is no Nominative in the sentence, go to step 2.)

Step 2: Translate the Passive Voice verb. (Remember how we translate the Passive Voice.)

Step 3:
  1. If the Verb is in the Active Voice, translate the Accusative Case direct object.
  2. If the Verb is in the Passive Voice, translate the Ablative of Agent next. (There will be no direct object.)

Step 4: Translate the Ablative of Agent: by the noun (if in a Passive Voice sentence).

Step 5: Use your knowledge of the other cases such as the Genitive, Dative, and prepositional phrases, and translate those where they best fit in the sentence.

Step 6: Look for adjectives and participles modifying nouns in every case, and translate them next to the noun they modify. This process is known as “chunking.”
