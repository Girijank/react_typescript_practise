Constantine

Reign: 306 CE–324 CE. I am known for:

  • being the first Christian emperor
  • moving the capital of the Empire to Constantinople
  • the Baths of Constantine
  • the Arch of Constantine

Constantine

I, Constantine, was a revolutionary Roman emperor! In the beginning of my career, I campaigned fiercely in Britain, Africa, Sicily, and Sardinia and gained great support of my military who helped me eventually become imperator. Ut imperator Romae, in Roma habere non volui quod urbs in ruinas ceciderat. So, I went to the old city of Byzantium, decided to rebuild this city with great monuments and architecture, and named the city Constantinople after myself! Fitting! My mother was a devout Christian, and so I had a great understanding of the Christian faith and I recognized Christ as God. I began a Christian church building program, and I started to build basilicas in Rome where the Pope could be seated. I also marked the burial sites of martyrs around the Empire with churches, including the Church of St. Peter next to the Tiber river. My building program helped Rome’s architectural structure be designed with Christ in mind. I was baptized on my deathbed. You should call me Constantine the Great!

Christian Emperors

Following Constantine, many of the Roman emperors practiced the Christian faith and recognized the seat of the Bishop as a powerful leader in Rome. Although Rome was still the major city in the Empire, Constantinople served as the Christian capital of the Roman Empire.
