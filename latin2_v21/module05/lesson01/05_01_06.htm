Translating with Present Active Participles

Follow this guide to help you!

Translation of the present Active participle:

  • Translate by adding -ing to the verb meaning and translate the participle alongside the noun.
  • You will decide whether to translate the participle into English before the noun it modifies or after the word it modifies. You will choose the correct translation based upon what makes the best sense in the context of the sentence.
Example 1

Aves stantes prope forum in ventum volabunt.

  • Stantes comes from the second principal part of “sto, stare, steti, status,-a,-um.”
  • Stantes uses the tense sign -nt.
  • Stantes is translated by "ing."
  • Stantes modifies aves (-es =Nominative, plural, feminine).

The birds standing near the forum will fly into the air.

Example 2

Feminae auribus audientibus carmina canant.

  • Audientibus comes from the second principal part of “audio, audire, audivi, auditus,-a,-um.”
  • Audientibus uses the tense sign -nt.
  • Audientibus is translated by "ing."
  • Audientibus modifies auribus (-is and -ibus =Dative, plural, feminine).

The women are singing the songs for the listening ears.

Example 3

Liberos ambulantes in via vidi.

  • Ambulantes comes from the second principal part of “ambulo, ambulare, ambulavi, ambulatus,-a,-um.”
  • Ambulantes uses the tense sign -nt.
  • Ambulantes is translated by "ing."
  • Ambulantes modifies liberos (-os and -es=Accusative, plural, masculine).

Translation: I saw the children walking on the road.

Example 4

Romulus et Remus a lupa nutrienti amabantur.

  • Nutrienti comes from the second principal part of “nutrio, nutrire, nutrivi, nutritus,-a,-um.”
  • Nutrienti uses the tense sign -nt.
  • Nutrienti is translated by "ing."
  • Nutrienti modifies lupa (-a and -i =Ablative, singular, feminine).

Romulus and Remus were loved by a nourishing wolf.

Straight from the Source!

Qui parentem meum interfecerunt, eos in exilium expuli iudiciis legitimis ultus eorum facinus, et postea bellum inferentis rei publicae vici bis acie. (Augustus, Res Gestae 2)

Those who killed my father, I drove them into exile, having avenged their deed under due process of the law, and afterwards with them waging war with the Republic, I conquered them twice in battle.

Nota bene: inferentis = present Active participle, Dative plural masculine. It functions as a participial noun.

Diana’s Directive: Participles with Direct objects

Sometimes, a participle can take a direct object.

  • Example: vir ducens canem oves videt = The man walking the dog sees the sheep.
    • Vir ducens is in the Nominative Case and canem is the direct object, receiving the action from the ducens participle.
