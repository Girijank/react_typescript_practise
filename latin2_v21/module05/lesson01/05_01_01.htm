Rise of the Roman Empire

The Roman Republic needed to be restored. Rome had expanded its borders all over the Mediterranean, establishing Roman provinces and allies throughout. It had developed a semi-professional army, able to conquer any opposition. It had designed a governmental structure built upon morals, virtues, and social equity for its people.

The Roman Empire. c. 27 BCE–476 CE

For the past 400 years…

Rome had been waging war after war with fellow Italian tribes, African empires, Germanic tribes, and Asian nations. By doing so, it became the “capital” of the world, but it also drained itself of its resources, created an army with more loyalty to its commanders than to Rome itself, relied upon its allies to maintain order in foreign territories, and indirectly encouraged men to be individually ambitious on the warfront and at home on the political front.

For the past 40 years…

Rome had waged wars upon itself. Instead of a collective consciousness built upon a unified call for moral order and virtue, citizens fought for more individual power, wealth, and honor. There was constant infighting. The Senate was operating through incessant corruption amongst its ranks. Men were bribing citizens for political power. Rich financiers were moving into private villas and making the working farmer become their slaves. The gap between the rich and poor had widened terribly. Social equity in Rome was in jeopardy.

Then it was 44 BCE…

Julius Caesar was dead, and the corruption and moral decay within the Senate and amongst the populus Romanus had been exposed. The Roman Republic had died.

For the next 14 years…

The senatus populusque Romanus hoped to have the Republic restored back to its virtuous self with a working Senate, effective laws, and moral leadership. Octavian would rise to this calling and become the “restorer of the Republic.” However, he did not actually restore the Republic, but rather, through a well-calculated, ambitious agenda, he elevated himself as the sole ruler of Rome for the next 40 years and created the Roman Empire.
