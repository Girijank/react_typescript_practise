Present Active Participles in Latin

The participle is formed from a verb and declined like an adjective. Follow this formula:

Present Stem + -nt-
(-ns- for Nominative sg. only) 		+ 3rd Declension endings
porta + portant- + portantis (Genitive singular)
  1. For 1st , 2nd, 3rdconjugation verbs, add -ns to the present stem for the Nominative case singular form only.
    • Then, add -nt + 3rd Declension i-stem endings for all other cases, singular and plural.
  2. For 3rd -io and 4th verbs, add -iens and -ient + 3rd Declension i-stem endings.
  3. The participle must agree with the noun it’s modifying in case, number, and gender.

Present Active Participial
Phrases

Let’s identify and translate present active participial phrases. Notice that the participle is agreeing in case and number with the noun:

Participial Phrase Translation Case, Number, and Gender of Participial Phrase
puer portans the carrying boy; the boy carrying… Nominative sg. m.
senatus constituentis of the deciding senate; of the senate deciding… Genitive sg. m.
piscatoribus sentientibus to/for the feeling fishermen; to/for the fisherman feeling… Dative pl. f.
feminas amantes the loving women/ the women loving… Accusative pl. f.
in carcere castiganti in the punishing prison Ablative sg. m.
