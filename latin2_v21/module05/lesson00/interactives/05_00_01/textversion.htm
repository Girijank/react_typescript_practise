Meet Diana—Text Version

image of Diana with her hunting gear, a deer, and dog Diana, Roman goddess

  • I am often depicted as a young, yet fierce woman—a testimony to the dual attributes of beauty and strength reflected in the women of Rome. You can often identify me by my bow and quiver of arrows, as I am always on the hunt. In Greek mythology, I am known as Artemis. In Etruscan mythology, I am Artume.
  • As the goddess who watches over all women, I am often depicted in association with the moon and its cycles. There are also many stories of me related to the constellations—including the famous Orion's belt, which you will learn more about in the coming lessons.
  • Most images place me in my favored domain—nature. I am also the protector of animals, and I have been known to act with swift vengeance when my familiars are threatened or hurt. Both the dogs of the hunt and prey such as deer are symbols of my realm.
