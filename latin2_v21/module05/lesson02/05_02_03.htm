Fabula Fabulosa: Diana and Actaeon

I am Diana, goddess of the hunt, daughter of Jupiter and Leto. I am a child of nature and love, roaming through the woods with my nymphs. One day, with my nymphs in a grove sacred to me, I decided to bathe in a spring near a grassy bank. But I was startled. I heard my nymphs yelling in high-pitched voices and then saw them beating their chests. They were trying to warn me that a stranger had entered my sacred grove.Id tardius erat.I stood there, undressed, towering over my nymphs and a hunter—Actaeon was his name—who had seen me! Embarrassed and angry, I threw water in his face. Then, I had no choice—I turned him into a deer. Quid facere debui? I couldn’t risk him telling anyone he saw me, the goddess Diana, unclothed.

Now the deer, Actaeon, ran away, but he was chased by his hunting dogs—all three of them! Eum cinxerunt et eum assiluerunt.Then, his fellow hunters gave chase and joined in this hunt. Oh, what have I caused! Actaeon was gravely wounded by his own dogs and fellow hunters. But it is important for mortals to know to never spy on a goddess!

In Tuo Orbe
