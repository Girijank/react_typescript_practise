The Julio–Claudian Imperial Dynasty

'In the time of Augustus, she [Livia] possessed the greatest influence, and she always declared that it was she who had made Tiberius emperor; consequently, she was not satisfied to rule in equal terms with him, but wished to take precedence over him.' (Cassius Dio, Historiae Romanae, book. 57, ch. 12, sec. 3)

Augustus needed an heir to carry on his seat as emperor, but he did not have a son to pass it to. Thus, he turned to his adopted sons, the sons of his wife Livia, to become the next emperors of Rome. This was his succession plan. Each Imperator would adopt the surname Caesar and continue to rule Rome with absolute power.

Tiberius

I, Tiberius, was the Roman emperor from 14 CE–37 CE. I am the son of Tiberius Claudius of the renowned Claudii family. Maximam partem vitae meae egi on foreign military exploits where I was an outstanding general. I also held tribunal power in Rome. I never wanted to be the emperor, but my step-father, Augustus Caesar, made me divorce my wife, Agrippina, marry his daughter, Julia, and become his “next-of-kin” to the emperor’s seat. Augustus adopted me at the age of 40, and mea mater, Livia, erat gravissima about placing me as his successor. I understood the importance of keeping order and stability in Rome, and I was a fine administrator. However, I never developed a good relationship with the Senate nor the people, and I was distrustful of many, especially my nephew and adopted son, Germanicus, whom the people really wanted to rule. Some believe I had him poisoned. His death did give way to my son, Drusus, as my successor. However, I retreated in 23 BCE to the island of Capri to self-isolate with my Praetorian Guard. I sent in my pal, Sejanus, to rule Rome while I was away. But he made a fatal mistake; he had an affair with my son Drusus’s wife, poisoned Drusus, and then married my son’s wife. Then, he announced that he was the Roman emperor. No! So, I went back to Rome, had him accused of treason in the Senate, and had him murdered.

Caligula

I, Caligula, a direct descendent of both Augustus and Livia, and son of Germanicus, at age 24 was the chosen successor to Tiberius following his death. I was a very good emperor at first. I gave power back to the Senate, I built roads, and I threw plenty of games for the entertainment of the people. But, within the year of my ascendency, I fell ill, perhaps to a brain disease, and after that I was nasty, mean, vicious, and terrifying. I killed several of my Praetorian Guards; I would humiliate people in public, or have men killed just because. I then began dressing like the gods while roaming the streets of Rome and then performing as an actor, singer, charioteer—you name it! I even made my sister a goddess. I had become unhinged. Tum omnia haec parva bella in Gallia et Brittania gesseram just for the fun of it. Then, I had gold statues built of me in Rome and throughout the Empire. The people had had enough of me, and so my Praetorian Guard assassinated me. Ahh man!

Claudius

I, Claudius, uncle of Caligula and grandson of Livia, was a great commander. A senatu et mea familia male tractus sum because I had cerebral palsy. Because of my mistreatment, I used to withdraw into my quarters, but I grew to become an excellent emperor. I had successful expeditions in Britain, Judaea, and Germany. I rid Rome of conspirators and useless senators. However, I was always paranoid that someone would try to assassinate me as they did Caligula. I married four times, and my final marriage to my niece Agrippina was my fatal marriage. I suspect that she fatally poisoned me so that her son, Nero, could become emperor. Et tu, Agrippina?
