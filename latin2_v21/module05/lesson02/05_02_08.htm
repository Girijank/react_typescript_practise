Caligula's Story

In the history of Rome, several leaders were known for their mental instability, but one was notorious—Caligula. Caligula lived from 12 CE to 41 CE. He was a Julio–Claudian emperor who ruled Rome from 37 CE to 41 CE. His reign ended when he was assassinated by the Praetorian Guard. He was known for mass accumulation of power, immorality, and corruption. In the history of Rome, he was the most infamous of the Roman imperial line.

It’s imperative that you translate this Latin story about Caligula below.

Caligula fuit imperator Romae. Is novus (strange) homo fuit qui equum suum amavit. Caligula equum suum senatorem facere voluit. Multi Senatores Caligulam maxime non amaverunt. Timens vitas suas, populus a Caligula fugit. Caligula a Praetorio interfectus est. Equus Senator a Caligula non factus est. Nam Caligula, equus est numquam sicut equus nisi etsi est Incitatus.

Caligula was the emperor of Rome. He was a strange man who loved his horse. Caligula wanted to make his horse a senator. Many senators greatly hated Caligula. Fearing for their lives, the people fled from Caligula. Caligula was killed by the Praetorian Guard. The horse did not become a senator by Caligula. For Caligula, a horse is never just a horse, unless the horse is Incitatus.
