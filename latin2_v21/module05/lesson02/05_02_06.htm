Translating with Perfect Passive Participles

Follow this guide to help you!

Translation of the Perfect Passive Participle:

  • Translation: "having been ____ed" (example: "having been carried," "having been given")
    • Since it is a participle in the Passive voice, you will often find the Ablative of Agent paired with it. example: oppidum, occupatum ab hostibus = the town, having been seized by the enemy

Step by Step! Use the arrows to view examples for translating the Present active participle in a sentence!

Example 1

Vinum, bibitum, in olla non remansit.

  • Bibitum comes from the fourth principal part of “bibo, bibere, bibi, bibitus,-a,-um.”
  • Bibitum is translated by "having been drunk."
  • Bibitum modifies vinum (-um =Nominative, singular, neuter). It is agreeing with vinum, which is the subject of the sentence.

Translation: The wine, having been drunk, did not remain in the jar.

Example 2

Senatus legibus creatis spem donaverant.

  • Creatis comes from the fourth principal part of “creo, creare, creavi, creates,-a,-um.”
  • Creatis is translated by "having been created."
  • Creatis modifies legibus (-is, -ibus =Dative, plural, feminine). It is agreeing with legibus,which is the indirect object in the sentence.

Translation: The Senate had given hope for the laws having been created.

Example 3

Sine malis civibus pulsis ex oppido, senatores impetum in se timebant.

  • Pulsis comes from the fourth principal part of “pello, pellere, pepuli, pulsus,-a,-um.”
  • Pulsis is translated by "having been expelled."
  • Pulsis modifies vinum (-is, -ibus =Ablative, plural, masculine). It is in the prepositional phrase “sine maliscivibuspulsis.”

Translation: Without the bad citizens having been expelled out of the town, the senators were fearing an attack on themselves

Exerceamus!

Now let’s put those participial phrases to practice with these sentences.

Senator, a comitibus suis castigatus, urbem reliquit.

The Senator, having been punished by his own comrades, left the city.

Pars pontis, celerius aedificata, in aquam cecidit.

Part of the bridge, having been built too quickly, fell into the water.

Cor eius, uxore eius ruptum, a matre eius multum nutriebatur.

His heart, having been broken by his wife, was being nourished much by his mother.

A puellis laudata, Diana templum servavit.

Having been praised by the girls, Diana protected the temple.

Agrippa, ab Augusto imperium aedificare coactus, erat magnus artifex.

Agrippa, having been compelled by Augustus to build the empire, was a great craftsman.
