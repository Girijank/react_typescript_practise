Perfect Passive Participles in Latin

The participle is formed from a verb and declined like an adjective. Follow this formula:

PPP (4th principal part) + 1st/2nd Declension endings in correct gender, number, case
Nominative singular =portatus,a,-um Genitive singular =portati,-ae,-i

Nota bene : The participle must agree with the noun it’s modifying in case, number, and gender.

Declining a Perfect Passive Participle: All Conjugation Verbs

We will use 1st conjugation verb: porto, portare, portavi, portatus, -a, -um = to carry

All verb conjugations will follow this same pattern.

Singular English Plural English
Nominative portatus,-a,-um the (noun) having been carried portati,-ae,-a the (noun) having been carried
Genitive portati,-ae,-i of the (noun) having been carried portatorum,-arum,-orum of the (noun) having been carried
Dative portato,-ae,-o to/for the (noun) having been carried portatis,-is,-is to/for the (noun) having been carried
Accusative portatum,-am-um the (noun) having been carried portatos,-as,-a the (noun) having been carried
Ablative portato, -a,-o by/ with the (noun) having been carried portatis,-is,-is by/ with the (noun) having been carried
Perfect Passive Participial Phrases

Using the verb “rumpo, rumpere, rupi, ruptus,-a,-um = to break,”use this chart as your guide!

Latin Participial Phrase English Translation Participle Case, Number, Gender Participial Phrase Explanation
lumen raptum the light having been broken Nominative sg. n. Answers: Which light?
It’s that light which has been broken.
luminis rapti of the light having been broken Genitive sg. n. Answers: Which light?
It’s that light which has been broken, but now it’s just broken in the Genitive case. Go figure.
scuto rapto to/for the shield having been broken Dative sg. f Answers: What shield?
It’s the shield which has been broken…probably in one of the many wars Rome fought.
cor raptum the heart having been broken Accusative sg.n. Answers: Which heart?
It’s that heart over there that has been broken…guess it needs some love glue.
a senatore rapto by the senator having been broken Ablative sg. m. Answers: Which senator?
It’s the senator who has been broken…probably in his spirit. Sorry, Senator.
