Emperor Trajan

Reign: c. 98 CE–117 CE. I am known for:

  • receiving letters from the writer Pliny the Younger
  • building Trajan’s Column
  • building Trajan’s Marketplace

I, Trajan, from the Spanish province of Italica, am now the first Roman emperor from a province! After Augustus, I am probably Rome’s best emperor. Ut filius praesidis Romani, momentum militum cognovi, so before I came to Rome to accept my position as emperor, I was conquering Upper Germany for a year. When I arrived in Rome, the people, the Senate, and the army loved me, but I made sure never to be called dominus but rather Optimus Princeps. I increased the size of the army and distributed money and grain to the people. My biggest accomplishment was my success in the Dacian Wars, defeating the people of Transylvania who had troubled Rome since the Republic. From this, we enjoyed huge spoils—money and slaves. The money helped expand our building program. I built Trajan’s Column and Trajan’s Marketplace in Rome. Then, I annexed Dacia and kept going—annexing Arabia, Mesopotamia, Armenia, and the Persian Gulf. I expanded our boundaries far beyond what any other emperor had ever done. Unfortunately, this also led to the people of Egypt, Cyprus, Judaea, and others to revolt against me. However, I consider myself one of Rome’s greatest emperors!

Trajan’s Forum

Designed by his architect, Apollodorus of Damascus, the Emperor’s Forum was a massive complex built to commemorate Trajan’s victories in the Dacian Wars. It included a bronze statue of Trajan, the Basilica Ulpia, Trajan’s Column, Trajan’s Marketplace, a library, and public open spaces. Let’s view the Forum!

Trajan’s Column, Rome

This magnificent marble architectural wonder, originally full of color, still stands in Rome today! Built in 113 CE to mark Trajan’s victories in the Dacian Wars, it stands 38 m tall and contains a spiraling relief of 155 scenes from his campaign. A statue of Trajan originally stood before the column and after his death, his ashes were buried beneath the column.

Trajan’s Marketplace, Rome

One of world’s oldest shopping center, this multi-leveled marketplace had over 150 stores for purchasing food and goods.

Basilica Ulpia, Rome

This was an architectural marvel dedicated to public use as law courts and a place of business. It separated the main courtyard from the temple in the Forum.

Cogavisti?

Trajan gave the title of Augusta to his beloved sister, Marcia. She was the first sister of an emperor to have been granted this title. Marcia travelled with Trajan, helping him make important decisions for the empire throughout the provinces. After her death, she was deified by the Senate.
