Emperor Hadrian

Now let’s learn about one of the best emperors Rome ever had!

Hadrian

Reign c. 117 CE–138 CE. I am known for:

  • building Hadrian’s Wall
  • the Jewish revolts

I, Hadrian, am another one of the good emperors…I think. I loved everything about the Roman Empire and wanted to soak in all of its wonders. So, I traveled extensively throughout the empire, studying it, writing poetry, and meeting troops and people from every walk of life. As emperor, I made sure that we were at peace, that we had better infrastructure, that we expanded our borders, that we trained our military, and that there was a great appreciation for Rome. During my reign, there were perhaps 50–70 million people living in the empire. Life was great—we had antibiotics, refrigeration, heating, and cooling on the streets and in our homes. My biggest blunder was my mishandling of the Jewish revolts, which caused me to retreat into isolation. But, before I died, I made sure to have a succession plan, legens proximas duas aetates imperatorum.

Hadrian’s Wall

Empires in the ancient world were in the business of building walls to protect their interests from invaders. One example is the Great Wall of China. Rome was also in the business of building walls. One of the architectural achievements of the Roman Empire was Hadrian’s Wall. After the expansionist policies of Trajan, Hadrian wanted peace and stability in the empire. Hadrian visited Britain in 122 CE, and he decided to build a 73-mile wall between Britain and Scotland. He wanted the wall to defend Roman interests in Britannia as well as control immigration and trade. Today, Hadrian’s Wall is a UNESCO historic site and can be visited by tourists.

Hadrianus Romam post mortem Traiani rexit. Is pacem in toto imperio habere cupivit. Illo tempore, Hadrianus ad Britanniam iter fecit. Terra visa, is longum murum aedificavit quod Britanniam cum barbaris munire cupivit. Iussit milites sex annos laborare. Hodie murum videre adhuc (still) potestis.

Hadrian ruled Rome after the death of Trajan. He wanted to have peace in all the empire. At that time, Hadrian made a trip to Britannia. With the land having been seen, he built a long wall because he wanted to defend Britannia against barbarians. He ordered soldiers to work for six years. Today you can still see the wall.
