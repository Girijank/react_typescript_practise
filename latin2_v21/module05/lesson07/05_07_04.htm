Emperor Nerva

Whew, we just had a slew of bad emperors ruling Rome! Can we make it? Will we make it? Let’s read about a couple of the good guys now.

Reign: 96 CE–98 CE. I am known for:

  • restoring stability to Rome
  • adopting Trajan

I, Nerva, only ruled Rome for two years as emperor. I was greatly needed in Rome after the horrible Domitian. At first, I was able to stabilize our economy. I was very good to the Senate and to the people. For example, populo pecuniam dedi to buy land, and I instituted a child-support program. Autem, potestatem exercituum tenere non potui, which led to a revolt by the Praetorian Guard. Before my natural death, I adopted a man from southern Spain named Trajan. I gave him the title of Caesar and appointed him as my successor, which was probably my biggest claim to fame.
