New Infinitive Forms

You are very familiar with infinitives! There are still a few forms to learn, and you will have a ball getting to know them. Let’s go!

Present Passive Infinitive

Follow this formula for 1st/2nd /4th Conjugation Verbs:

2nd principal part - -e
 + -i
portare - portant- + portari

Follow this formula for 3rd Conjugation Verbs:

2nd principal part - -ere
 + -i
Regere - reg- + regi

Translation: to be (verb)-ed

Dictionary Entry Singular Imperative
amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um to love amari (to be loved)
doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um to teach doceri (to be taught)
mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um to send mitti (to be sent)
capio, capere, cepi, captus, -a, -um to seize capi (to be seized)
audio, audire, audivi, auditus, -a, -um to hear audiri (to be heard)
Perfect Active Infinitives

Follow this formula for all Verb Conjugations:

3rd principal part - -i
 + -isse
portavi - portav + portavisse

Translation: to have (verb)-ed

Perfect Passive Infinitives

Follow this formula for all Verb Conjugations:

4th principal part + -esse
4th principal part + portatus,-a,-um esse

Translation: to have been verbed

  • The infinitive agrees with the noun it modifies in case, number, and gender.

Future Active Infinitive

Follow this formula for all Verb Conjugations:

4th principal part - -us, -a, -um
 + -urus, -ura, -urum + esse
portatus - portat- + portaturus,-ura, -urum esse

Translation: to be about to (verb)

  • The infinitive agrees with the noun it modifies in case, number, and gender.
Dictionary Entry Singular Imperative
amo, amare, amavi, amatus, -a, -um to love amaturus,-a,-um esse (to be about to love)
doceo, docere, docui, doctus, -a, -um to teach docturus,-a,-um esse (to be about to teach)
mitto, mittere, misi, missus, -a, -um to send missurus,-a,um esse (to be about to send)
capio, capere, cepi, captus, -a, -um to seize capturus, -a,-um esse (to be about to seize)
audio, audire, audivi, auditus, -a, -um to hear auditurus,-a,-um esse (to be about to hear)
Future Passive Infinitive

Follow this formula for all Verb Conjugations:

4th principal part: -um ending only (-um) + -um + iri
portatus - portat- + portatum iri

Translation: to be about to be (verbed)

  • This is non-declinable

The Six Infinitives:

Here is the overall synopsis of all Infinitive forms. Memorize these forms and you will be golden!

porto, portare, portavi, portatus, -a, -um – to carry

moneo, monere, monui, monitus, -a, -um – to warn

rego, regere, rexi, rectus, -a, -um – to rule

sentio, sentire, sensi, sensus, -a, -um – to feel

Active Passive
Present

portare (to carry)

monere (to warn)

regere (to rule)

sentire (to feel)

 Present

portari (to be carried)

moneri (to be warned)

regi (to be ruled)

sentiri (to be felt)
Perfect

portavisse (to have carried)

monuisse (to have warned)

rexisse (to have ruled)

sensisse (to have felt)

 Perfect

portatus, -a, -um esse (to have been carried)

monitus, -a, -um esse (to have been warned)

rectus, -a, -um esse (to have been ruled)

sensus, -a, -um esse (to have been felt)

Future

portaturum esse (to be about to carry)

moniturum esse (to be about to warn)

recturum esse (to be about to rule)

sensurum esse(to be about to feel)

 Future

portatum iri (to be about to be carried)

monitum iri (to be about to be warned)

rectum iri (to be about to be ruled)

sensum iri (to be about to be felt)

Diana’s Directive: More Infinitives

  • You might see our Passive infinitive forms coupled with a Passive form of the verb dico, dicere, dixi, dictus,-a,-um - to say. This is because once you translated “dico” in the passive such as “is said, was said,” then the Passive infinitive fits nicely in the sentence to complete an action.
