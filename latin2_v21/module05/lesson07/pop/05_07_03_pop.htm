Tense

Latin, like English, has six tenses that show the time an action takes place:

Present

Imperfect (simple past)

Future

Perfect

Pluperfect

Future perfect

Person

1st person = I or we

2nd person = you singular, you plural

3rd person = he/ she/ it or they

Number

Singular or Plural

Voice

It shows the relationship between the subject and the verb. Like English, Latin has two voices:

Active:The subject is the doer of the action.

Passive:The subject receives the action.

Mood

Moods are classifications that denote the attitude of the speaker. Latin has three moods:

Indicative: These are statements and simple questions.

Imperative: These are commands.

Subjunctive: These are possibility and wish statements.
