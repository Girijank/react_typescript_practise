What is an Infinitive?

  • The infinitive is a verbal noun.
  • Infinitives do not have a“person.”
  • They are translated as to...

How are Infinitives Used?

1. The Direct Object

  • Terram laborare parat = He prepares to work the land.
  • Hostes contra milites Caesaris oppidum armare cupiverant. = The enemy had wished to arm the town against the soldiers of Caesar.

2. The Subject

  • Errare est humanum. = To err is human.

Nota Bene: The infinitive is considered a neuter when adjectives modify it.

3. The Predicate Nominative

  • Videre est credere. = To see is to believe.

Infinitives with the Verb POSSUM

The verb possum is a compound of sum and shows many of the same irregularities.

  • Its principal parts are possum, posse, potui- (no fourth principal part and no Passive).
  • It is translated as can or be able and is followed by the infinitive.

Diana’s Directive: Can versus Able

Stick with "to be able" because an infinitive naturally follows: “I am able to see.” When using can, an infinitive does not naturally follow in English, and it may leave you wondering.

  • For example: "Possum videre" can be translated either of two ways:

"I am able to see" or "I can see."
