Translating with Future Active Participles

Follow this guide to help you translate with future Active participles!

Translation of the future Active participle:

  • Translate with “about to verb” or “going to verb.”
  • You will decide whether to translate the participle into English before the noun it modifies or after the noun it modifies. You will choose the correct translation based upon what makes best sense in the sentence.
Example 1

Vir portaturus puellam trans flumen natavit

  • Portaturus comes from the fourth principal part of porto, portare, portavi, portatus,-a,-um.
  • Portaturus uses the tense sign – ur.
  • Portaturus modifies vir (-us=Nominative, singular, masculine).

Translation: The man, about to carry the girl, swam across the river.

Example 2

Potestas in Roma a Caligula necaturo Tiberium transmutabitur.

  • Necaturo comes from the fourth principal part of neco, necare, necavi, necatus,-a,-um.
  • Necaturo uses the tense sign – ur.
  • Necaturo modifies Caligula (Ablative, singular, masculine).

Translation: Power in Rome will be changed over by Caligula, who was about to kill Tiberius.

Example 3

In Britannia, milites castra oppugnaturos vidimus.

  • Oppugnaturos comes from the fourth principal part of oppugno, oppugnare, oppugnavi, oppugnatus,-a,-um.
  • Oppugnaturos uses the tense sign – ur.
  • Oppugnaturos modifies milites (-es & -os = Accusative, plural, masculine).

Translation: We saw the soldiers about to attack the camp.

Now let’s put those participial phrases to practice with sentences.

Aetas versura in autumnum meas sorores ambulare in urbem coegerat.

The summer about to turn into autumn had compelled my sisters to walk into the city.

Praetorem laudaturam consulem omnibus cordibus vestris amas.

You love with all your hearts the praetor about to praise the consul.

Milites proelium commissuri praeter ducem suum superbe stetit.

The soldiers about to begin battle stood up proudly in front of their leader.

Illis iudicibus stipendium nostrum constituturis benigtatem monstrabamus.

We were showing kindness to those judges about to decide our pay.

Nos, castra posituri, dormire maxime voluimus.

We, about to pitch camp, wanted especially to sleep
