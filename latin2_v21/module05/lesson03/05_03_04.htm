Future Active Participles

Let’s learn another participial form—the future Active participle, that is!

Future Active Participle English Translation:

  • It denotes action that is about to happen.
  • It is translated as “about to verb” or “going to.”

In English, the future Active participle will only be utilized in an adjectival form.

Future Active Participle as Adjective

When functioning as an adjective, the participle will describe the noun. You will decide whether to translate the participle before the noun it modifies or after the noun it modifies. You will choose the correct translation based upon what makes best sense in the sentence.

Example : Agrippina, about to swim to shore, had been forced out of Rome by her son, Nero.

Participle : about to swim

Participle Explanation: What is Agrippina about to do? She’s about to swim. “about to swim” = future Active participle, modifying “Agrippina”

Future Active Participle Usage

English Sentence

Future Active Participle Identification + Explanation

Function of Participial Phrase
The man about to become emperor was Tiberius. about to become = future active participle, modifying “the man” The man about to become emperor = subject of the sentence
The Praetorian Guard selected Claudius, who was about to serve as emperor. who was about to serve = future active participle, modifying “Claudius” who was about to serve as emperor = direct object of the sentence
