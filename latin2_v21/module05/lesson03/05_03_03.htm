Emperor Nero

Let’s learn about our next emperor, Nero.

Nero

Reign: 54 CE—68 CE. I am known for:

  • the Great Fire of Rome
  • the Domus Aurea
  • the love of Greek culture, art, and music
  • the Boudica Rebellion

I, Nero, at age 17 became emperor of Rome! At first, I was great at it! I followed the lead of my tutors, the Stoic philosopher Seneca and the Guard Burrus, who made great relationships with the Senate on my behalf. Etiam meam Agrippinam ex Roma removere me adiuvaverunt. Then, after five years, I changed for the worse—and there was no coming back for me! I just wanted to have fun without any supervision! So, I poisoned my stepbrother, planned to have my mother assassinated, and made Seneca take his own life. Me amante mores Graecos, I held Greek festivals and performed all over town as a singer and actor. I was great, too! I murdered my wife, Octavia, daughter of Claudius. I perhaps set Rome on fire in 64 CE which nearly destroyed all of Rome. Luckily for me, I could just rebuild Rome into whatever I wanted after that, without those other monuments in my way. So, I built my Domus Aurea, widened the streets of Rome, and rebuilt our infrastructure. I would say the fire gave Rome new life! However, others blamed me for it and as a result, tried to kill me or rebel against me. I could not take the blame anymore and shortly after that, I took my own life.

Cogitavisti?

Nero devised many plans to have his mother assassinated, but she continued to outwit him. First, he planned to have her bedchamber ceiling fall on her as she slept, but that didn’t work. Then, he sent her out on the ocean in a collapsible boat. But she was able to swim to shore. Then, he finally had his men kill her with daggers, which haunted him for the rest of his life.
