Future Active Participles in Latin

The participle is formed from a verb and declined like an adjective. Future participles are formed from the 4th principal part of the verb = PPP. Follow this formula:

4th Principal Part - -us,-a,-um + -urus,-a,-um
portatus,a,um portat- = portaturus,-a,-um
  1. Decline the future Active participle like a 1st/2nd Declension adjective to match with the noun it’s modifying in case, number, and gender.
  2. Translate by the words “going to” or "about to."
Declining a Future Active Participle: All Conjugation Verbs

Follow this chart for future Active participle declining.

Singular English Plural English
Nominative portaturus,-a,-um (noun) about to carry portaturi,-ae,-a (noun) about to carry
Genitive portaturi,-ae,-a of the (noun) about to carry portaturorum,-arum-orum of the (noun) about to carry
Dative portaturo,-ae,-o to/for the (noun) about to carry portaturis,-is,-is to/for the (noun) about to carry
Accusative portaturum,-am-um the (noun) about to carry portaturos,-as,-a the (noun) about to carry
Ablative portaturo,-a,-o by/with the (noun) about to carry portaturis,-is,-is by/with the (noun) about to carry
Future Active Participial Phrases

Let’s identify and translate future active participial phrases. Notice that the participle is agreeing in case and number with the noun:

Latin Participial Phrase English Translation Participle Case, Number, Gender + Explanation
puer portaturus the boy about to carry Nominative sg. m.

Answers: Which boy? Oh, that boy who is about to carry something…
feminae portaturae the women about to carry Nominative pl. f.

Answers: Which women? Yep, those women who are about to carry something…

lumen moturum the light about to move Nominative sg. n.

Answers: Which light? That light that’s about to move…perhaps to dark? Who knows!?
