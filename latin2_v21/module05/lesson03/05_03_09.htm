The Year of Four Emperors

'Vitellius, addicted as he was to luxury and licentiousness, no longer cared for anything else either human or divine … Now, when he was in a position of so great authority, his wantonness only increased, and he was squandering money most of the day and night alike.' (Cassius Dio, Historiae Romanae, boo. 64, ch. 2, sec 1)

With Nero dead, the family line ceased to exist. Nero did not appoint a successor, and thus everyone wanted and fought for the throne. As a result, in 69 CE, Rome had four emperors in one year. They were Galba, Otho, Vitellius, and Vespasian.

We, Galba, Otho, Vitellius, and Vespasian, all ruled Rome as Imperator in 69 CE.

Galba:

  • ruled Rome for seven months total, beginning in June, 68 CE
  • ruled Rome for two weeks in 69 CE (January 1st–15th, CE)

Otho: Ruled c. 69 CE. Known for:

  • ruling Rome for three months

Vitellius: Ruled c. 69 CE. Known for:

  • ruling Rome for seven months

Vespasian: Ruled c. 69 CE. Known for:

  • being last emperor in 69 CE; ruled for one month in December of 69 CE

Galba

I, Galba, a governor of Africa and Spain, marched on Rome to overthrow Nero. When I reached Rome, the Senate declared Nero an enemy of the state, and he later took his own life. I became emperor in June of 68 CE, but only ruled for two weeks in 69 CE. Nunc, sum Imperator, sed meum regnum erat breve because I executed many citizens just because, and I was not very much liked. So, my one-time supporter Otho had me murdered. Otho, how could you!

Otho

I, Otho, am now emperor, but I was constantly fighting for control with the military commander, Vitellius, cuius copiae in Italiam invaserunt. I believed I couldn’t beat Vitellius in war, so I took my own life after ruling Rome for only three months.

Vitellius

Yes, Otho’s out! Finally, I’m in!! I, Vitellius, am now emperor of Rome, but I squandered my opportunity because I was cruel and addicted to power. I was a bit of a tyrant, constantly partying and celebrating the death of my enemy and disregarding the customs of Rome. Ita, populi fidem in me amiserunt, and after seven months, they brought in Vespasian as the new emperor. That brings us to December, 69 CE.

Vespasian

Three strikes—you’re out? Not in my book! Let me rule and get some order established. I, Vespasian, am now emperor and I ruled Rome for 10 years. I brought peace and calm back to the Empire and was well-liked by the people, et Coliseum aedificare incepi!
