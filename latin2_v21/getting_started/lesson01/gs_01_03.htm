Lessons & Assessments: Parts of a Lesson

Beginning

Each lesson begins with an introduction to the lesson's big idea, followed by the note guide to assist you as you work through the lesson. There will also be an opportunity to practice vocabulary you have already learned to refresh your memory.

Middle

Read each lesson carefully to understand all the concepts. Also, be sure to complete the activities. You will practice the concepts and learn from your mistakes, so you can be better prepared before you complete your assessment.

End

Each lesson ends with information about the assessment that you will need to submit for a grade. You will be asked to either complete a quiz or exam or submit an assignment that will be graded by your instructor. Be sure to read any instructions that are provided.

Lessons & Assessments: Submitting Your Work

As you complete each lesson, you will need to submit an assessment. The type of assessment varies for each lesson. Here are some assessment types you will see in this course.

Pretest

At the beginning of each lesson, you will take a pretest of multiple-choice questions related to the content in the module to see what you already know. Don't worry; this diagnostic will have little to no impact on your grade, but it will give you a window into the knowledge you will gain throughout the module. It will also help you understand one component of how you will be assessed in the course.

Quizzes

Many lessons include a multiple-choice quiz with questions. In these types of assessments, you will choose one correct answer from the choices given. Often the question is paired with an image or data graph. To be successful, make sure to read each question and its stimuli carefully, and use your skills to select the best response.

Module Exams

Each module will end with a multiple-choice and matching exam that assesses your understanding. Module exams also contain questions to test your listening comprehension of Latin, so be sure to have speakers or headphones to hear the audio files.

Discussion-Based Assessments

The Discussion-Based Assessment (or DBA) is an opportunity to discuss your skills and learning objectives in each module with your instructor. This isn't a "one size fits all" activity. Each DBA is a specific activity and discussion. It provides opportunities for you to make connections in your own learning across key concepts, ideas, and policies. You can ask questions and demonstrate your growing skills.

Translations

Translations are teacher-graded assignments that allow you to demonstrate your Latin translating skills. For these assignments, you will translate a selection of Latin sentences into your own words in English and answer grammar and comprehension questions based on your translations.

Workshops

Workshops are an opportunity to demonstrate what you have learned. To be successful, read all of the instructions and the rubric to see how you will be graded. All workshops will include a Latin composition element. Workshops often involve multiple elements, so don't forget to save your work often. Templates are also provided to ensure you have everything you need to complete the workshop project.

Segment Exam

The segment exam at the end of the course will assess your understanding of the course as a whole. It will include multiple-choice and matching questions. Like the module exams, these segment exams will include an audio component as well.

Collaboration

Collaboration assessments are peer project opportunities. You will have the opportunity to participate in a complex, multi-step, problem-solving activity and share feedback with at least one other student to benefit from a peer's perspective. There are synchronous and asynchronous opportunities, meaning you do not have to be working with your peers at the exact same time, but you may. However, you must complete the collaboration assessment before taking the segment exam. There are opportunities provided in the collaboration section. Contact your instructor early about collaboration opportunities.
