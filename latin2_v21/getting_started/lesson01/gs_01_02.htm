Course Icons

Road signs let you know what to expect as you drive. Similarly, there will be some symbols or icons found throughout this course. Icons are used in the course to draw your attention to some specific things. Here are icons you may see.

Note Guide

The note guide icon can be found throughout the course as a reminder to access your lesson note guide and take note of important content. The note guide for each lesson can be downloaded on the first page of that lesson, and it should be saved as a study tool throughout the course.

Cogitemus! (Let's think about it!)

The cogitemus icon is used to encourage deeper thought into the lesson content. Questions and practices noted by this icon will further your understanding of Latin and the Roman world.

In Tuo Orbe! (In your world!)

Ever wonder where Latin went after all these years? We've got news for youâ€”it never left! When you see the In Tuo Orbe! icon, you will learn about ways that the Latin language and culture can still be heard and seen in the modern world.

Olympian Narrator Notes

In this Latin course, the Olympians speak! Each module is narrated by a different Roman deity from Mt. Olympus; check this icon to see what they have to say about Latin grammar. Their notes are important tidbits to remember when studying and understanding new concepts.

Translation Globe

Whenever you see this icon, select to reveal the English translation of the Latin word, phrase, or sentence it follows. Remember, when practicing your Latin language skills, it is important to give translation your best shot before checking your answer.

Assessment

When you see this icon, it's time to show off your skills! Read the instructions carefully under this icon to know what you will need to submit to your instructor for that lesson.

Cogitavisti? (Did You Know?)

Check out information under the cogitavisti icon to learn fun, interesting, or weird facts about the Latin language and the Romans that spoke it. Carry on this knowledge into your daily life and hobbies!
