You will be writing your dialogue from the perspectives of a doctor (medicus) and a member of a Republic familia Romana. Choose your patient character type from the following list; then complete the chart below for both dialogue characters. Your characters can be:

  • mater: mother

  • filia: daughter

Step 2: Describe your characters

In English, write out interesting facts about the character. You can be very creative with your description, but make sure you are basing your information on your research. Be sure to include the following:

  • name and role of your characters
  • a Roman Virtue of the interviewee that best describes the person
  • where they live (in the city or in the countryside),including descriptions
  • duties within and outside of the household
  • style of dress/ how they look
  • full sentence answers with a minimum of 100 words each
  • citation of the sources you used for your research, using MLA format

Step 3: Latin Writing Assignment –Medical Emergency Dialog

Imagine that a medical emergency has occurred in the household of your Roman family member. Write a dialogue between your characters, with your medicus/medica asking three questions and your familia Romana character providing three answers in dialogue format. Your questions should be in Latin with at least one interrogative pronoun question and at least one interrogative adjective question, with English translations included. The answers to your doctor's questions should be in English. Be sure to label each line of your dialogue with the name of the person speaking.

Example:

Question 1: Medicus: Quis manum eius cepit? (Doctor: Who took his hand?)

Response 1: Mother: Someone cut off his hand.

Question 2: Medicus: Estne is tutus? (Doctor: Is he safe?)

Response 2: Mother: Yes, the dog is dry now

Question 3: Medicus: Quid? De quo cane dicis? (Doctor: What? About which dog are you speaking?)

Response 3: Mother: Not my dog.

Nota Bene: These sentences have been taken from the story, “The Doctor and the Soldier’s Lost Hand.” Your dialogue should center around a new medical emergency of your choosing.
