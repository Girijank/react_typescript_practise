I am often depicted as a beautiful, yet humble young woman—much like the Vestal Priestesses who join my temple. You can often identify me by my hair, which I always wear tied up or hidden under a veil. Vesta is my Roman name. I am typically just called Mater because of my motherly warmth. In Greek mythology, I am known as Hestia. In Etruscan mythology, I am Ethausva.

As protectress of the home, I am often depicted with the most essential, central part of any Roman household—the hearth. All duties related to the household fall under my domain.

Many images you will find of me show me carrying a torch. This torch is a symbol of my eternal flame, which my Vestal Priestesses are charged with tending and protecting. It was their sacred mission to never let my fire go out.
