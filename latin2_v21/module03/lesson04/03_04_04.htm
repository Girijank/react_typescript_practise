Vestal Power!

Since the Vestals held a high status in society, they were treated very well in Rome and were held in high regard. They all lived together at the Atrium Vestae. They were allowed to attend games and events without a male guardian. Remember the The Twelve Tables? The document stated that Vestal Maidens were not legally bound to any man. The position didn't come without risk. If a Vestal conducted herself poorly, the state would punish her by burying her alive! Not to worry—this was a very rare occasion in Rome because it was bad luck to kill a Vestal Maiden. However, after the Battle of Cannae of the Second Punic War, Rome was so afraid to incur any further bad will against it that the city buried alive two Vestal Maidens as a form of sacrifice

In Tuo Orbe!
