Which which is which?

Have you ever heard of the joke "who's on first"? Sometimes, even in English, pronouns can be comically tricky. Here are some ways to distinguish between all the Latin pronouns that even the Romans would have had a good chuckle about!

Interrogative or Relative?

Here are the three simple rules for distinguishing between interrogative adjectives, interrogative pronouns, and relative pronouns:

  1. If there is no question mark, it is a relative pronoun.
  2. If it has a question mark and the "q" word agrees with a noun (usually next to it), it is an interrogative adjective.
  3. If none of those fit, it is the interrogative pronoun.
