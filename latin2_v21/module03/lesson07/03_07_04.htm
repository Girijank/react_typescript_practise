"The Farmer and the Senator" (Part I)

Roman senator

Roman farmer

Listen to the conversation and translate the dialogue between a farmer who works in the countryside and a senator who has come to the countryside for the first time.

Ego terram rusticam habito. Omni die in agris laboro. Sum senex fessusque, et meam terram colere non volo.

I live in the countryside. Everyday I work in the fields. I am old and tired, and I do not wish to till my land.

Vita est crudelis!.

Life is cruel!

Salve, Agricola! Nonne vita est magna! Ex urbe sordida ad hanc villam in terra rustica movi. Labor meus in Roma erat difficilis!.

Greetings, farmer! Isn’t life great! I moved out of the filthy city to this villa in the countryside. My work in Rome was difficult!

Difficilis? Nonne vita tua in senatu erat non difficilis!

Difficult? Surely, your life in the senate wasn’t difficult!

Visne locum meum in agris substituere?

Do you wish to take my place in the fields?

Cur id dicis? Ecce omnes aves, capros, oves, boves, et equos in agris tuis! Mirabile!

Why are you saying this? Look at all the birds, goats, sheep, cows, and horses in your fields! Wonderful!

Tu es demens! Ego ipse numquam dormio! Ego ipse animalia semper lavo, nutrio, specto. Ei ipsi omnem cibum semper devorant, ex agris excedunt, ea frangunt, et vina olivarum, quibus mercatores ad forum mihi pecuniam dant, semper edunt.

You are crazy! I myself never sleep! I myself am always washing, nourishing, watching the animals. They themselves are always devouring food, leaving the fields, breaking things, and eating the vines of olives, for which merchants pay me at the marketplace.
