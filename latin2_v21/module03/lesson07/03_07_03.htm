Intensive Pronouns

In this lesson, I will introduce you to another important pronoun form that adds fire to any sentence. My kind of pronoun!

Ipse, Ipsa, Ipsum

Intensive pronouns are demonstrative pronouns that put special emphasis on the words they modify. You will learn two forms of intensive pronouns in this lesson.

Ipse is a compound of the pronoun is, ea, id which we have already learned. Remember, you can always review your pronoun forms in your Toolbox!

Look for the intense pronoun placed right next to the noun it modifies.

Translated by the compound -self: myself, herself, themselves, etc.

  • It is used in an intensive fashion.
  • I myself saw…
  • You yourself saw…
  • She herself saw…
  • They themselves saw…

You can also translate ipse, ipsa, ipsum as “very” in a sentence.

  • The very man saw…
  • The very woman saw…
  • The very thing moved…

Sentence Examples:

Ipse pueros vidi. I myself saw the boys. (In this sentence, the “I” would be a male.)
Ipsa pueros vidi. I myself saw the boys. (In this sentence, the “I” would be a female.)
Pueros ipsos vidi. I saw the boys themselves.
Roma ipsa est pulcherrima urbium. Rome herself is the most beautiful of cities.
Filia senatoris ipsius in villa dormiet. The daughter of the very senator will sleep in the villa.

Ad astra per sententias! Translation Practice

Hostis ipse civitatem Romanam post bella Latina habere non cupivit.

The enemy himself did not wish to have Roman citizenship after the Latin Wars.

Finis bellorum Latinorum ipsorum erat in 338 BCE.

The end of the Latin Wars themselves was in 338 BCE.

Rex aures ipsas ad caput suum ligabit.

The king will bind his very ears to his own head.

Vulnus ipsum se non claudetur.

The wound itself will not close by itself.
