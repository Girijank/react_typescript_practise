5th Declension

You’ve made it to the last noun declension! Aren’t you excited? In this lesson, you will learn the 5th Declension. With every declension comes a new set of endings! First review what a declension is in the guide and then let's learn the 5th Declension endings and how to decline a 5th Declension noun.

Let’s roll through our declension identifiers:

Declension Identification Vocabulary Example
1st Declension -ae = Genitive singular ending puella, puellae f. girl
2st Declension -i = Genitive singular ending liber, libri m. book; oppidum, oppidi n. town
3st Declension -is = Genitive singular ending lex, legis f. law; mare, maris n. sea
4st Declension -us = Genitive singular ending portus, portus m. port, harbor; manus, manus f. hand; cornu, cornus n. horn

Let’s listen to the pronunciations!

5th Declension M./F. Endings and Declining
  Fifth Declension Endings dies, diei, m and f.–day res, rei, f.–thing
Case Singular Plural Singular Plural Singular Plural
Nominative -es -es dies dies res res
Genitive -ei -erum diei dierum rei rerum
Dative -ei -ebus diei diebus rei rebus
Accusative -em -es diem dies rem res
Ablative -e -ebus die diebus re rebus

5th Declension Nouns

  • -ei is the Genitive singular ending.
  • It is called the E Declension because there is an E in every case ending.
  • The fifth Declension has mostly feminine nouns.
  • The fifth Declension has no neuter nouns.

Examples:

  • dies, diei m./f. day
  • res, rei f. matter, thing
