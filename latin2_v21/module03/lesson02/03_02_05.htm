All those Declensions!

I am so proud of you. You now know all of the noun declensions! What an accomplishment. You can now open up any Latin dictionary and be able to identify, decline, and translate any noun. This is over 2500 years of information that you can now decipher! Wow!

All you need to remember is that the dictionary entry is the key to telling which declension a noun is and what case endings will be applied to it. So, let’s go back over your noun dictionary entries for each declension:

  1. 1st Declension nouns end in -ae in the Genitive singular. Example: puella, puellae f. girl
  2. 2nd Delension nouns end in -I in the Genitive singular. Example: liber, libri m. book; oppidum, oppidi n. town
  3. 3rd Declension nouns end in -is in the Genitive singular. Example: lex, legis f. law; mare, maris n. sea
  4. 4th Declension nouns end in -us in the Genitive singular. Example: portus, portus m. port, harbor; manus, manus f. hand; cornu, cornus n. horn
  5. 5th Declensionnouns end in -ei in the Genitive singular. Example: dies, diei m./f. day

You will notice that...
