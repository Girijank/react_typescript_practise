Aeneas lost at Sea

Translate this story about the family relationship between Aeneas and Romulus. Use the vocabulary from the first two lessons and the information you learned about the Roman family.

Aeneas in mare amisus est. Is vertice, saxo, a monstro, et ordine rerum malorum propulsatus erat.

Aeneas was lost at sea. He had been driven by a whirlpool, rock, monster, and a series of bad things.

Deinde, is in portu Latii, quod est ubi Romani die futuro habitabit, advenit. Ibi, Romulus Remusque a lupa educentur.

Then, he arrived in the port of Latium, which is where the Romans will live on a future day. There, Romulus and Remus will be raised by a wolf.

Mater sua, tamen, erat Rhea Silvia, virgo Vestalis, et pater suus erat deus Mars.

Their mother, however, was Rhea Silvia, a Vestal Maiden, and their father was the god Mars.

Erat Romulo omen. Romulus, qui prodigium ex avibus caeli legerat, suum fratrem Remum necare constituit.

There was an omen for Romulus. Romulus, who had read a sign out of the birds of the sky, decided to kill his own brother Remus.

Ob eum maleficium, in quo erat mortem Remi,urbs Romae constituta est.

On account of this evil deed, in which there was the death of Remus, the city of Rome was established.
