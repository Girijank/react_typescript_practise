Roman Home Decor

Romans decorated their homes with much ostentation because Roman houses were signs of a person’s social hierarchy. In Cicero’s de Officiis, Cicero states that the dominus of the house should design their home to distinguish his own dignity and then to give attention to persons entering the house.

Wall Paintings

Wealthy Romans decorated their walls with elaborate wall paintings or frescos. Many of these paintings still survive today, such as in the city of Pompeii. The paintings often pictured various images

  • Glass bowls were featured on their painted walls.
  • Still-life images of translucent, white-colored large bowls with fruit were displayed in many of the elite villas and homes in Rome, Campania, Pompeii, and Herculaneum from the late first century BCE through the first century CE.

The Romans also decorated their houses with sculptures—of their family members, of their ancestors, of their gods, and notable Roman leaders. The wealthy arranged their houses with glass objects and decorations to show their wealth and social status
