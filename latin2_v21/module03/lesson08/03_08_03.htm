Prevailing Pronouns!

Your knowledge of pronouns is stellar. I am so proud of all of your hard work. Let’s take a look at them all to give each its proper home in your translations!

The Intensive Pronouns

Ipse, Ipsa, Ipsum

Review the declension chart for practice.

Ipse is a compound of the pronoun which we have already learned. It is translated by the compound -self: myself, himself, themselves, etc. It is used in an intensive fashion: I myself saw...

Sentence examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Pronoun Identification
Res publica Romana ipsa ab Hannibale et urbe Carthaginis paene vastata erat. The Roman Republic itself had been almost destroyed by Hannibal and the city of Carthage. Nominative sg. f.
Virgines Vestam ipsam in templo eius laudant. The maidens praise Vesta herself in her temple. Accusative sg. f.
Domus divis patris ipsius tria cubicula, unum impluvium, et multas picturas in muris habet. The house of the rich father himself has three bedrooms, one impluvium, and many pictures on the walls. Genitive sg. m.

Demonstratives

Iste, Ista, Istud

Review the declension chart for practice.

Translation:

  • That (noun) of yours
  • That (bad noun)

Sentence examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Pronoun Identification
Senatus iste leges bonas rumpet. That evil senate will break the good laws. Nominative sg. m.
Virtutes istae populis communibus spes confirmaverunt. Those virtues of yours have established hope for the common people. Nominative pl. f.
Ex aciei istos rapuistis. You all seized those bad men from the battle line. Accusative pl. m.
Cum istis bibere vos coegeramus. We had compelled you all to drink with those bad men. Ablative pl. m.

The Interrogatives Pronouns

Quis, Quid

Review the declension chart for practice.

The interrogative pronoun is a pronoun that asks the question “who?” or “what?”

Sentence examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Pronoun Identification
Quid in cubiculo illa res est? What is that thing in the room? Nominative sg. n.
Cui fabulam de magna familia Barca narrabo? To whom will I tell the story about the great Barca family? Dative sg. m.
Quid virtutes rei publicae sunt? What are the virtues of the republic? Nominative sg. n.
Quibuscum elephanti trans Alpes passus suos fecit? With whom did the elephants make their steps across the Alps? Ablative pl. m.

The Interrogative Adjectives

Qui, quae, quod

Review the declension chart for practice.

The interrogative adjective asks “which?” or“what?”

Translations: What man? Which book?

Sentence examples:

Latin Sentence English Translation Pronoun Identification
Qui dies est? Which day is it? Nominative sg. m.
In quibus domibus Romanis servi et liberi habitant? In which Roman homes do the slaves and children live? Ablative pl. f.
Quibus pueris Grammaticus linguam latinam docet? To which boys does the teacher teach the Latin Language? Dative pl. m.
Quae proelia Hannibal in secondo bello Punico superavit? Which battles did Hannibal win in the Second Punic War? Accusative pl. n.
