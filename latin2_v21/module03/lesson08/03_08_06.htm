The Soldier and the Craftsman

You have read the story about a soldier who has just returned from war and sees his friend, the craftsman, in the city of Rome. Continue to translate this story. You will submit your translations for the numbered sentences,Grammar and Comprehension Questions, and Short Essays to your instructor for a grade. Make sure to use the Glossary and Useful Tips!

Latin Sentences and Questions for Submission

Latin sentences (translate from Latin to English):

  1. Miles: Narra mihi, quanto Roma mutataest?
  2. Artifex: Nunc in urbe sunt multae insulae, viae, et statuae, multabalnea, multi aquaeductus, et populi ignoti. Etiam Cloaca Maxima est nova!
  3. Miles: Mirabile! Quo est Cloaca Maxima?
  4. Artifex: In urbe nostra et multis urbibus.
  5. Artifex: Ante bella, ego agricola de Etruria eram. Nunc ego artifex sum.
  6. Miles: Quomodo es artifex ipse?
  7. Artifex: Rates et alveos rei publicae aedifico. Quid in bello faciebas?
  8. Miles: Ego ipse hostes ballistis arietibusque pugnaveram.
  9. Artifex: Igitur equales in arte sumus! Per noctem ridebimus et bibemus!

Grammar and Comprehension Questions (answer in 1–2 sentences):

  1. List the four question words in this passage.
  2. In line 1, what tense, person, voice, and number is mutataest in line 1?
  3. In line 9, what case, number, and gender is equales, and what word is it agreeing with?
  4. In line 9, what verb does ridebimus come from? (provide all four principal parts from the dictionary entry)

Short Essay (answer in 3–4 sentences):

  1. In three to four full sentences, describe the translation steps you took to translate Line 7: Artifex: rates et alveos rei publicae aedifico. Quid in bello faciebas? In your answer, be sure to include how you found and translated the subject(s) and verb(s) of each sentence component. Then, tell us the cases of the nouns, and why they are in these cases. Don't forget to identify the tense, voice, and number of your verb!
  2. The craftsman names several technological advancements within Rome.
    1. List at least three items in English that the craftsman names as being part of the growth of the Roman Republic.
    2. Then, explain the Cloaca Maxima and how important it was to the technological expansion of the Roman Republic.

Vocabulary Aid

  • insula, insulae, f. apartment
  • mirabile wonderful!
  • Cloaca Maxima, Cloacae Maximae, f.
  • Respublica, respublicae f. the Republic
  • ratis, ratis, f. ship, boat
  • alveum, alvei, n. hollowed-out boat
  • ballista, ballistae, f. ballista (large military engine for throwing stones and missiles)
  • aries, arietis, m. battering ram

Translation Checklist

  • Have you identified the tense, voice, person, number, and mood of the verb?
  • Have you identified the case, number, and gender for the nouns and adjectives?
  • Do your English definitions for each of the words in the Latin translation match the definitions found in the toolbox dictionary?
  • Have you double-checked your Latin vs. English word order?
    • HINT: In Latin, adjectives follow the nouns they modify, genitive nouns follow the nouns they possess, and verbs are typically found at the end of the sentence.
  • Did you transfer all punctuation from Latin to English?
