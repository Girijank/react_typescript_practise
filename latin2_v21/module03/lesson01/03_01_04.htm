4th Declension

In this lesson, you will learn a new noun declension—the 4th Declension. Woohoo! Let’s first take a trip down memory lane with our 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Declensions. In preparation, select the noun guide to ensure you remember how a noun functions in a sentence.

Remember these oldies but goodies? Well, it will warm my dear Vestal heart if you go to your notes and find all of your 1st, 2nd, and 3rd declension nouns while you recall the following:

Declension Identification Vocabulary Example
1st Declension -ae = Genitive singular ending puella, puella f. girl
2nd Declension -i= Genitive singular ending liber, libri m. book; oppidum, oppidi n. town
3rd Declension -is = Genitive singular ending lex, legis f. law; mare, maris n. sea

4th Declension Nouns

  • -us = Genitive singular ending

Examples:

  • census, census m.– census
  • portus, portus m.– port, harbor
  • manus, manus f. –hand
  • cornu, cornus n.– horn

With every declension comes a new set of endings! Let’s learn the 4th Declension endings and how to decline a 4th Declension noun.

4th Declension M./F. Endings and Declining

Listen to the pronunciation of these new endings, following along on the chart:

  Fourth Declension M. and F. Endings manus, -us, f. - hand
Case Singular Plural Singular Plural
Nominative -us -us manus manus
Genitive -us -uum manus manuum
Dative -ui -ibus manui manibus
Accusative -um -us manum manus
Ablative -u -ibus manu manibus

Fourth Declension nouns are mostly masculine. However;

  • There are two common feminine nouns: domus, domus f. = home and manus, manus f. = hand
4th Declension Neuter Endings and Declining

There are a handful of neuter 4th Declension nouns. Let’s check out the neuter endings and then listen to the pronunciation of the neuter endings and a declined neuter noun.

  Fourth Declension N. Endings cornu, -us, n. - horn
Case Singular Plural Singular Plural
Nominative -u -ua cornu cornua
Genitive -us -uum cornus cornuum
Dative -u -ibus cornu cornibus
Accusative -u -ua cornu cornua
Ablative -u -ibus cornu cornibus

Can you hear where these are different from the masculine and feminine forms?

Fourth Declension nouns are mostly masculine. However:

  • There are two common neuter nouns: genu, genus n. = knee and cornu, cornus n. = horn

Vesta’s Advice: 4th Declension Endings

  • I call 4th Declension the “-u-“ Declension because there is a “u” in almost every case form.
