Roman Virtue

Romans conducted their lives according to a collection of virtues and values. These virtutes were part of a belief system instilled in Roman people at an early age. They were words, ideals, phrases, and a collective mindset that the Romans utilized in all aspects of their daily lives.

Roman Virtues

Select each of the virtues below to learn what they meant to the ancient Romans.

Virtus

Virtue; the courage or virtue a person possesses that brings great acclaim to them

Auctoritas

Spiritual authority; bestowed by the Senate upon a leader; the senses of ultimate authority

Clementia

Mercy; mildness and gentleness

Dignitas

Dignity; a sense of personal pride and self-worth

Honestas

Respectibility; the images that one presents as a respectable member of society

Pietas

Piety; the quality of being pious in respect to one’s religion, faith, and country

Humanitas

Character; the refinement and sense of culture in an individual

Industria

Industrious; the act of working hard in your profession

Veritas

Truth; having honesty when dealing with others

Prudens

Sensible; showing wisdom in one’s actions

These are some of the virtues that helped establish a national narrative for the Roman people. They would use these words to guide them in life and also to aid them in moving up the cursus honorum. The Romans would write their laws and literature, conduct business and warfare, and set a national tone based upon the virtues. As a result, this value system helped the Romans develop such a strong nationalistic pride that they believed they were an unstoppable people destined for greatness and capable of conquering the entire world!
