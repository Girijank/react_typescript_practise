Interrogative Pronouns

You have learned several pronoun forms, which you can review under Pronouns in your Toolbox. In this lesson, you will learn the interrogative pronoun.

English Formation:

The interrogative pronoun is a pronoun that asks the question “who?” or “what?”

  • The “who?” will refer to a masculine/feminine word.
    Example: Who walks to the park? Who teaches Latin? Who reads the lesson?
  • The “what?” will refer to a neuter word.
    Example: What is the answer to the problem? What is the meaning of life? What is the time?

Latin Formation

  • The interrogative pronoun is declined in the masculine/feminine and neuter forms.
  • The plural Latin forms are exactly the same as the relative pronoun plural forms.
  • It is important to memorize the interrogative pronoun forms.

Sentence Usage

Interrogative pronouns will be written at the beginning of the sentence. They will be in the case, number, and gender that they need to be, according to the context of the sentence. Here are some examples. Use these as your guide!

CASE Sentence Interrogative Pronoun Gender and Function of Interrogative Pronoun
Nominative Who walks to the forum? Who = qui Masculine subject of the sentence
Nominative What is the answer to the problem? What = quid Neuter subject of the sentence
Genitive Whose book is this? Whose = cuius Possession (masculine or feminine)
Genitive Of what will the science project be made? Of what = cuius Neuter possession
Dative For whom are you baking the cake? For whom? = cui Indirect object (masculine or feminine)
Dative To what does the Vestal Priestess pray? To what = cui Neuter indirect object
Accusative Whom will you fight in the war? Whom = quos Masculine direct object
Accusative What does the gymnast hear? What = quid Neuter direct object
Ablative With whom do the soldiers fight? With whom = quibuscum Prepositional phrase (masculine or feminine)
Ablative By whom will the story be shared? By whom = a quibus Prepositional phrase (masculine or feminine)

Latin Sentences

  1. Qui ad forum ambulat? Who walks to the forest? (qui= Nominative sg. m)
  2. Quid ad terram naves propulsat? What propels the ships to the land? (quid = Nominative sg. n)
  3. Cuius liber lego? Whose book am I reading? (cuius= Genitive sg. m)
  4. Cui virgo Vestalis laudat? To what does the Vestal Maiden pray? (cui = Dative sg. n.)
  5. Quos in bello pugnabitis? Whom will you fight in war? (quos = Accusative pl. m.)
  6. Quibuscum liberi fabulam legent? With whom will the children read the story? (quibuscum = Ablative pl. m.)

Vesta’s Advice: Ablative Preposition + Interrogative Pronoun

You will see any of the prepositions (ab/de/ex/cum) which take the Ablative Case written with the Latin interrogative pronoun forms.

Example:

  • ab quo / quibus= by whom/what?
  • de quo/ quibus = from whom/what?
  • ex quo/ quibus = from whom/what?
  • quocum/ quibuscum = with whom/what?
