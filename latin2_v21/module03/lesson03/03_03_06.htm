The Vestal Priestesses

My Vestals would swear this oath when selected to be one of my Vestal Priestesses. Let’s listen!

“Sacerdotem vestalem quae sacra faciat quae ius siet sacerdotem vestalem facere pro populo Romano Quiritium utei quae optima lege fovit ita te amata capio.”

“I take you, beloved one, as the Vestal Priestess who will perform the sacred duties which the law has for a Vestal Priestess on behalf of the Roman people, just as which it is under the law for her to perform the best terms.” FABIUS PICTOR, PRESERVED BY AULUS GELLIUS BOOK I

Having a daughter selected as a Vestal Priestess was highly coveted by a Roman noble family. Vestal Priestesses were revered for their modesty and duty to the Roman state. The Pontifex Maximus would select twenty girls of high esteem between the ages of 6 to 10 to possibly become a Vestal Priestess. Selection to the cult of six was then done by a lottery.

The girls would then live with the other Vestal Priestesses for a period of thirty years. The chief priestess was called the Vestalis Maxima This is how their duties went:

  • First ten years: Learn how to conduct the duties of the position.
  • Next ten years: Perform their religious duties, including tending to the fire, leading multiple religious ceremonies (including watching over the Vestalia festival and Lupercalia festival), and performing other duties such as, sweeping the temple steps or pardoning criminals. They were even entrusted with wills for safe-keeping—even the wills of the Roman Emperors.
  • Last ten years: Educate and train the newly selected priestesses.
