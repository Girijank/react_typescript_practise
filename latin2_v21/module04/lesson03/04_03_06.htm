The Grass Crown Award

A medal, a new car, money? No! A Grass Crown! This was what a Roman general wanted the most after his victory! Can you believe it! The Grass Crown (Corona graminea/Corona obsidionalis) was the highest award a Roman general could receive for his military service during the Republic. It was awarded to a leader who had saved an entire legion of an army.

What was it made of?

The crown was made out of wildflowers, grass, and wheat from the land on which the commander saved his army.

Who presented it?

It was presented to a commander by his soldiers. It could also be presented by the Senate and the Roman people to an outstanding commander.

Pliny the Elder describes the Grass Crown in Book XXII of his Naturalis Historia as a crown of the highest honor, only awarded to a most exceptional commander to honor his bravery in times of extreme desperation. The crown was so revered because it was given to a commander on the spot of a battle. His soldiers would gather up grass from the ground and present the crown to him.
