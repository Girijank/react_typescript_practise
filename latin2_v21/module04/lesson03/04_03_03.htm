"Women Warriors" (Part II)

You now know that women were also warriors in battle! Let’s learn who some of them were specifically in this passage. This passage will help you master your comparative and superlative adjectives, which are in bold type.

Narrabo vobis de creberrimis feminis, quae illud bellum gerebant.
Erat Iuturna, soror Turni, quae iram et furorem in suum fratrem propter Iunonem spirabat et cogebat. Etiam, Camilla, maxima et saevissima bellatrix, a Turno recepta est et scuto armata est. Camilla celerrime currebat; celerius ventis; tam celeriter ut, cum per summa frumenta volaret, tenerrimis frumentis non noceret, et, ubi per medium mare iter faceret, pedes mare numquam tangeret. Camilla maiorem numerum Troianorum, sicut frumentum, inter fecerat; tum Arruns, ubi Camilla Chloreum oppugnabat, telum, quod per cor Camilla evolavit, iecit.

I will tell you about these numerous women, who waged that war.
There was Juturna, sister of Turnus, who breathed and brought anger and madness into her brother on account of Juno. Also, Camilla, the greatest and fiercest warrior-woman, was received by Turnus and armed with a shield.
Camilla used to run most quickly; (she ran) more quickly than the winds; (she ran) so quickly that, when she flew through the tops of the crops, she didn’t harm the very tender crops, and when she made the journey through the middle of the sea, her feet never touched the sea.
Camilla had killed a rather large number of Trojans, just as if grain; then Arruns, when Camilla was attacking Chloreus, hurled his spear, which flew through the heart of Camilla.
