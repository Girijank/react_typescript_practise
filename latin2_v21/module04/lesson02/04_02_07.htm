Unique Superlatives: –er, -lis Adjectives

Adjectives that have a base that end in “-er or -lis” have their own unique superlative formation.

Adjectives ending in “-er”

The superlative of all adjectives ending in -er is formed using this formula:

Nominative sg. m. of adjective - ending + -rimus, -a,-um

Examples:

Positive Degree Superlative Degree
miser, misera, miserum miserrimus, miserrima, miserrimum = most wretched
pulcher, pulchra, pulchrum pulcherrimus, pulcherrima, pulcherrimum = most beautiful

I like to think of this superlative formation as the “doubling of the R formation.” Double up! Listen to the pronunciation of these adjectives.

Pronunciation
Positive Comparative Superlative
liber, libera, liberum ‐ free liberior, liberius ‐ more free, freer liberrimus,-a,-um ‐ most free
sacer, sacra, sacrum ‐ sacred, holy sacrior, sacrius ‐ more sacred, more holy, holier sacerrimus,-a,-um ‐ most sacred, most holy, holiest
celer, celeris, celere ‐ swift celerior, celerius ‐ swifter celerrimus,-a,-um ‐ swiftest
Adjectives Ending in “-lis”

base of adjective - ending + -limus, -a,-um

Positive Degree Superlative Degree
facilis, facile facillimus, facillima, facillimum = easiest
difficilis, difficile difficillimus, difficillima, difficillimum = most difficult

I like to think of this superlative formation as the “doubling of the L formation.” Double up!

Pronunciation
There are five adjectives which end in “-lis” that will use this formula. They are:
Positive Comparative Superlative
facilis, facile ‐ easy facilior, facilius ‐ easier facillimus,-a,-um ‐ easiest
difficilis, difficile ‐ difficult difficilior, difficilius ‐ more difficult difficillimus,-a,-um ‐ most difficult
similis, simile ‐ like similior, similius ‐ more like simillimus,-a,-um ‐ most like
dissimilis, dissimile ‐ unlike dissimilior, dissimilius ‐ more unlike dissimillimus,-a,-um ‐ most unlike
humilis, humile ‐ humble humilior, humilius ‐ more humble humillimus,-a,-um ‐ most humble
