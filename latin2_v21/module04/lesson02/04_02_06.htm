"Women Warriors" (Part I)

Even though we learned that the Roman army only contained men, women ‐ not as part of the Roman army ‐‐ did go to battle, participate in the wars, and were fierce leaders in many wars. We will learn about many of those women in the translation exercise.

Omnes de Aenea fabulas legerunt.
Sed multi de me, Lavinia, coniunx Italica Aeneae, non legerunt.
Paris et Troiani bellum cum Menalao et Graecis de Helena gesserant. Tamen, Turnus et alii Italici bellum cum Aenea et Troianis et aliis Italis de me gerebant.
Dum Troiani in Italia bellum gerebant, multi viri pugnabant; sed, paucae feminae non timebant sed eae iuvabant..

Everyone has read stories about Aeneas.
But many have not read about me, Lavinia, the Italian wife of Aeneas.
Paris and the Trojans had waged a war against Menalaus and the Greeks about Helen. However, Turnus and some Italians waged a war against Aeneas and the Trojans and other Italians about me.
While the Trojans waged the war in Italy, many men fought; however, a few women didn’t fear, but they helped.
