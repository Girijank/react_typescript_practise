Army Divisions

The army contained four main divisions: legionaries, auxiliaries, allied soldiers, and mercenaries. Let’s learn about these ranks and unit divisions

Legions

Legions

They were the fighting power of the army who were the elite soldiers. The structure of the legions changed over time throughout the Republic.

  • They were the best fighters, and only strong Roman citizens over the age of 17 were allowed.
  • Men sign up for 25 years of service. They were given money and farmland in retirement and lived in military towns called colonia.
  • A cavalry of 300 men accompanied each unit.
  • A legion = ten cohorts of 480 men.
    • Cohort = six centuries of 80 men.
  • Century = ten contubernia of 8 men.
Auxiliaries

Auxiliaries

The Roman auxilia were essential aids to the Roman army. In fact, because only about 10% of the army’s population were actually Roman citizens, Rome needed non-Roman citizens to aid its legions. The Roman auxilia were non-Roman citizens who fought in the front lines and hailed from all over Roman provinces and territories. They were deployed as specialist units—archers, slingers, spear-throwers, or whatever the fighting style of their native land was. They were normally stationed in their native land to ward off any uprisings in Roman territories. This was a psychological methodology the Romans applied to its allied troops to control its far-reaching army. The auxiliaries were

  • not Romans
  • paid a third of a legionary’s wage, but still a steady income of 750 sestertii
  • given full Roman citizenship after 25 years of service
  • contained in two main groups: the auxilia alae and the auxilia cohortes
Allied Troops

Allied Troops

When Rome conquered tribes, it often forced, through a treaty (foederati), the tribe to become a part of its Allied army. This meant that these tribes were required to fight with Rome when called upon. Oftentimes when Rome was not able to defeat a tribe, it instead offered the tribe land and the promise to fight alongside them against any future threats. The allies would fight under their own commander.

Mercenaries

Mercenaries

The Romans hired mercenary troops to help them fight in battles, although the Romans did not like to do this. Rome only engaged in mercenary hiring a few times in the Republic. One time was during the Punic Wars when it hired the Celts to fight on its behalf. The Celts would later betray the Romans and fight alongside the Carthaginians instead.
