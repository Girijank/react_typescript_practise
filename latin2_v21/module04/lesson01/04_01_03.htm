The Roman Army

I admire any person who fights in a war, so let’s learn about the Roman army and its brave soldiers!

As Rome continued to conquer new territories, it needed a sizable army to help win wars. Thus, Rome developed an army that became unmatched in the ancient world. In the Roman Republic, the Roman army had upwards of 1 million men fighting for it!

Who were the first soldiers?

  • The first soldiers were a ragamuffin crew of mostly small land farmers.
  • Roman allies made up half of the army.

Why join the military?

  • Many wanted to be a part of something larger than themselves, a shared coniuratio that inspired soldiers to fight with bravery.
  • Voting rights: Becoming part of the military would grant aristocratic males and poor farmers the ability to vote in Rome.
  • Wealth, power, social status: After war, men were granted money and land.
  • Men constantly signed up for duty, either because they were forced to, but most likely because they felt supported, protected, and honored to fight on behalf of Rome.
  • The military also took an oath, sacramentum, to the Roman state.

A Soldier’s Strength

  • A Roman soldier would be required to march 20 miles a day, swim across rivers, build Roman camps, train daily if not in battle, build forts, build aqueducts, build bridges, and fight in fierce hand-to-hand combat.
  • If a soldier were caught falling asleep on duty, he could be sentenced to death!

The Army’s Purpose

  • Roman Expansionism: Rome did not have enough administrators or praetors to oversee its expansion. So, Rome utilized its army to maintain rule over all of its conquered territories. Therefore, it needed to constantly recruit members to join the army to maintain order.
  • Rome wanted to conquer new lands and people.
  • It wanted to serve as administrators of new lands.
  • Rome desired to operate as the face of the Roman Republic abroad.
