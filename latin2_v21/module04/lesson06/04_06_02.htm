Speaking Practice

Listen to each section of the chat below. Then, record yourself repeating them to hear and compare your pronunciation. Once you have that chant, repeat the process!

The more you speak Latin out loud, the more comfortable you will feel, pronouncing and discussing the vocabulary and phrases you learn with your instructor. Speak loudly and proudly, soldier!

Roman War Chant I

Roman soldiers chanted this marching song on their way to battle! We’ve broken up the chant into its stanzas; but remember the Romans would have chanted it in its totality as they marched.

Ecce, Caesar nunc triumphat, quī subēgit Galliam, Civiumque multitūdō celebrat victōriam.

See Caesar now rides in triumph, he who overthrew Gaul, and a crowd of citizens celebrates his victory.

Gāius Iūlius Caesar noster, imperātor, pontifex, Prīmum praetor, deinde cōnsul, nunc dictātor, moxque rēx.

Our Gaius Julius Caesar, conqueror, priest, first praetor, then consul, now dictator and soon king.

Ēn! –Victōrēs prōcēdentēs, laetī flōribus novīs, Magnā praedā sunt potītī et captivīs plūrimīs.

The happy people, with fresh flowers, going a head in victories, great loot has been gotten, and a lot of captives.

Exsultantēs magnā vōcē, “Iōtriumphe!” concinunt, Dum aurātum ante currum victa oppida ferunt.

The celebrators shout together, "Hey triumph!" in a great voice, while they bring before the gilded track the beaten towns.

Lēgiōnēs Viam Sacram tōtam complent strepitū. Capitōlīnumque collem scandit Caesar in currū.

Legions fill up the sacred way with a big racket, and Caesar in a chariot climbs up the Capitoline hill.

Ō sōl pulcher, Ō laudande! – Caesarem recēpimus, Et corōnā triumphālī honōrātum vīdimus.
– J. C. Robertson (1934)

O beautiful sun, o worthy–of–praise! We have received Caesar and we have seen him honored with a triumphal crown.
– J. C. Robertson (1934)

Roman War Chant II

Here’s a war chant created just for you! Notice all of the comparative and superlative forms. We think the Romans would have loved chanting it.

Milites fortes urbis nostrum,
Milites fortes urbis nostrum,
Este audaces et pugnate ferociter!
Este audaces et pugnate ferociter!
Celerrime, ferocissime, et simillime,
Iustissime, tacitissime, et facillime,
Prorsus iter facite, pugnate, vincite
Dum hostes nostri cadit gravius!
– Marcia Anderson (2021)

Brave soldiers of our city,
Brave soldiers of our city,
Be bold and fight courageously!
Be bold and fight courageously!
Most swiftly, fiercely, and similarly,
Very justly, silently, and easily,
March on, fight on, win on Till our enemy falls rather heavily!
– Marcia Anderson (2021)
