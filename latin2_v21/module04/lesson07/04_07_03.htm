The First Civil War: 88 BCE–31 BCE

Now that you know who started the First Civil War, let’s learn more about the war itself! As you read, think about whose side you are on and why. Then think about the comparisons between the Roman Civil War and the American Civil War.

I am Cinna

I, Lucius Cornelius Cinna, am on Marius’s side, even though it might not have seemed like it at first. Sulla had first supported me for consulship in 87 BCE, only because he wanted to use me as a pawn against Marius. But I always supported the agenda of a “novus homo,” quod in periculum me posuit. I abhorred Sulla’s “marching on Rome.” Well, the Senate then stripped me of my consulship and my Roman citizenship, and so I went into exile. I teamed up with Marius when he was in self-exile in Africa. We formed an army and conducted our own “March on Rome”!

First Civil War

I wish Rome had never gone to war against Rome, but here we are. Sulla marches on Rome first. Then, Marius and Cinna march on Rome. Marching on Rome is an outright disgrace and an act of war! Marius and Cinna defeated Sulla and together became co-consuls in 86 BCE. But Marius would die only a couple weeks later, and thus poor Cinna could not stand up to Sulla’s men who were still in Rome. Cinna e Roma expulsa est. Sulla ad Romam revenit, filium Marii in itinere, et potestatem rei publicae recuperavit! Sulla was now on fire. The Senate declared him Dictator. Sulla packed the Senate house with his supporters. He killed Marius’s men who were living abroad. Then, he restored the Republic to his liking.The war had ended in 82 BCE. Two marches on Rome—who would have ever thought!
