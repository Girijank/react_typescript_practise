Mythology as Propaganda

The Romans used their mythological stories of fierce gods and noble heroes as an emotional and psychological tactic for instilling a mentality of heroism into their people and soldiers. These stories helped build a sense of invincibility into the mindset of all sections of Roman life, including into:

  • the Roman army and its commanders
  • the senators and assemblymen
  • the wives at home
  • the children training for war
  • the allies of Rome

Mythology helped propel a collective narrative so much that, within Rome, the Romans believed their city was destined to be great.
