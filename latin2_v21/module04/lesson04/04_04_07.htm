"Women Warriors" (Part IV)

In the previous “Women Warriors” reading, you learned that Camilla was one of the few valiant women who fought against Aeneas and his allies. She died in battle by Arruns’s spear. Read and translate the passage below to learn of the aftermath of her death in battle. You will submit your translation of this passage and answers to the following questions to your instructor. Make sure to use the glossary for help with tricky vocabulary!

Latin Sentences and Questions for Submission

Latin Sentences Translate each line into English.

  1. Diana, dea, mortem Camillae spectavit et illa deaerat iratissima quod Camillam carissime amavit.
  2. Igitur Opis, quam Diana iustior armaverat et cui honorem donaverat, de Capitolina desiluit et Arruntem interfecit.
  3. Iuno et Minerva ipsae, quae Troianos non amaverunt, pro Turno acerrrime pugnaverunt.
  4. Denique, pius Aeneas, qui audacissimus erat, Turnum ferocem interfecit. Aeneas rex Latii factus est.
  5. Post mortem Aeneae, ego, Lavinia, regina factus sum, quod Ascanius, qui filius eius erat, novior erat.

  3. List one superlative adjective from this passage. What case and number is it? What word does it modify?
  4. List two superlative adverbs from this passage.

Vocabulary Aid

  • Diana, Dianae f. Diana (the goddess of the hunt)
  • Camilla, Camillae f. Camilla (Volscian warrior maiden)
  • igitur, therefore
  • Opis, Opis f. Opis (goddess of fertility and the earth)
  • armo, armare, armavi, armatus, to arm
  • Capitolina, Capitolinae f. Capitaline Hill
  • desilio, desilire, desilui, desultus, to jump
  • Arruns, Arruntis m. Arruns (a Trojan ally)
  • Iuno, Iunonis f. Juno (goddess of marriage)
  • Minerva, Minervae f. Minerva (goddess of wisdom and battle strategy)
  • Troianus, Troiani m. Trojan
  • Turnus, Turni m. Turnus (king of the Rutuli)
  • denique finally, at last
  • Aeneas, Aeneae m. Aeneas (Trojan hero)
  • Latium, Latii n. Latium
  • Lavinia, Laviniae f. Lavinia (Aeneas’ wife)
  • Ascanius, Ascanii m. Ascanius (Aeneas’ son, also known as Iulus)
  • quod can mean “because”
  • novus, nova, novum new, young, strange

Translation Checklist

  • Have you identified the tense, voice, person, number, and mood of the verb?
  • Have you identified the case, number, and gender for the nouns and adjectives?
  • Do your English definitions for each of the words in the Latin translation match the definitions found in the toolbox dictionary?
  • Have you double-checked your Latin versus English word order?
    • HINT: In Latin, adjectives follow the nouns they modify, genitive nouns follow the nouns they possess, and verbs are typically found at the end of the sentence.
  • Did you transfer all punctuation from Latin to English?
