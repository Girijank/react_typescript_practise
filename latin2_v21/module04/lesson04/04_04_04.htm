We’re almost at the conclusion of our story—can’t wait to see how it ends! But before you read the continuation of this passage, here’s a reminder of how to translate Latin sentences.

Ubi Ascanius maior iuventus erat, rex factus est. Mox, autem, Ascanius e Lavinio urbem novam, Albam Longam statuere cessit. Ubi Ascanius cessit, ego iterum regina faciebar. Mille annos nihil de me scribebatur; sed etiam hodie Vergilius et Livius mihi vitam et lumen fabulae meae dant.

When Ascanius was an older youth, he was made king. Soon, however, Ascanius left from Lavinium to establish a new city, Alba Longa. When Ascanius left, I again was made queen. For a thousand years, nothing was written about me; but even today Vergil and Livy give me life and light to my story.
