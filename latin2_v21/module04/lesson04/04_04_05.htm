Quam + Superlative

You are familiar with how the adverb “quam” is used in the Ablative of Comparison. “Quam” will also be paired with superlative adjectives. Let’s learn how it works here!

In English, we indicate that someone or something possesses the highest degree of quality by translating a phrase as “as X as possible." For example:

  • I walked as quickly as possible to the market.
  • The student wrote as superbly as possible on his term paper.
  • The woman laughed as loudly as possible during the comedic play.
  • The Romans were as brave as possible in their army.

Latin Construction

In Latin, we do the same to indicate someone or something possesses the highest degree of quality by translating a phrase as “as X as possible." In Latin, the construction looks as follows:

quam+ superlative adjective or adverb

For example:

Latin English
quam altissimus as tall as possible
quam largissimus as large as possible
quam celerrime as quickly as possible
quam largissime as largely as possible
Quam + Superlative Adjective Usage

Quam + Superlative Adjective Usage: "as [adjective] as possible"

The adjective will agree with its modified noun still in case, number, and gender.

Latin Sentence English Translation Quam + Superlative Adjective Identification & Explanation
Ventus quam acerrimus fuit. The wind was as sharp as possible. quam acerrimus.acerrimus = Nominative sg. m. to agree with ventus
Femina quam pulcherrima fuit. The woman was as beautiful as possible. quam pulcherrima.pulcherrima = Nominative sg. f. to agree with femina
Ferrum quam durissimum fuit. The iron was as hard as possible. quam durissimum. durissimum = Nominative sg. n. to agree with ferrum
Quam + Superlative Adverb Usage

Quam + Superlative Adverb Usage: "as [adverb] as possible"

The adverb will simply be in the superlative form.

Latin Sentence English Translation Quam + Superlative Adjective Identification & Explanation
Spartacus et servi quam fortissime pugnaverant. Spartacus and the slaves had fought as bravely as possible. quam fortissime
Marius a militibus suis quam audacissime ladabatur.. Marius was loved by his soldiers as boldly as possible. quam audacissime
Discipuli a magistro eorum quam superbissime castigati erant. The students had been reproved by their teacher as snobbishly as possible. quam superbissime

Ad Astra per Sententias!

Translate these sentences into English.

Bellum quam facillime vincemus.

We will conquer in the war as easily as possible.

Mars immortalis a patre, Iove, quam ferocissime castigatus erat.

The immortal Mars had been chastened by his father, Jove, as ferociously as possible.

Contra Romanos, Iugurtha quam fortissimus erat.

Against the Romans, Jugurtha was as brave as possible.

Post mortem patris eius, Mithridates se ducem quam celerrime statuerat.

After his father’s death, Mithridates had established himself as leader as quickly as possible.
