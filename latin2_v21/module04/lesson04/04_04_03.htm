Marius, the Ultimate Roman Soldier

Probably the most famous Roman soldier, Gaius Marius, was born around 157 BCE into a plebeian family and rose up in military ranks. He ultimately reached the highest position in Rome—consul. Click on the slide to learn more about him!

Marius, the most famous Roman soldier ever!

  • He fought in the Punic Wars, Spanish Wars, Jugurthine Wars, and First Civil War.
  • He rose up from the position of novus homo to Commander of the Roman army.

Army Reformer:

  • Under Marius, men now volunteered for the army.
  • The state paid for the soldiers’ gear now.
  • He gave his men land in North Africa.
  • He created the new three cohort fighting stance.

Marius's Mules:

He turned his men into human mules because he made them carry all of their equipment, food, tools, supplies, and tents on their backs. This actually eliminated the need for a baggage train following the marching army.

Love you, Marius:

His men were forever devoted to him. They pledged (sacramentum) themselves to him, which changed the soldiers’ mindset to be more loyal to their commanders than to the Roman state itself. Uh oh, this would become a problem for Rome in the future!
