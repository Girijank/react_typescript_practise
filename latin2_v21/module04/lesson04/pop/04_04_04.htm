Translating Latin Sentences!

You have added more tools to translate longer, more complex Latin sentences. Here are quick tips to help guide you:

Step 1: If you have an interrogative pronoun or adjective or question word in the sentence, translate that first.

Step 2: If you have a sentence mixed with an independent and relative clause, mark off with brackets the relative clause and insert it into the translation of the independent clause, and translate the independent clause.

Step 3: “Chunk” words together. See adjective-noun pairs in a sentence when you scan a sentence and become familiar with this.

Step 4: Translate the Nominative Case: the noun. (If there is no Nominative in the sentence, go to step 2.)

Step 5: Translate the Passive Voice verb. (Remember how we translate the Passive tenses.)

Step 6: a.) If the verb is in the Active Voice, translate the Accusative Case direct object.
i.) Translate the adverb after the verb.
b.) If the verb is a Passive Voice, translate the Ablative of Agent next. (There will be no direct object.)
ii.) Translate the adverb after the verb.

Step 7: Translate the Ablative of Agent by the noun (if in a Passive Voice sentence).

Step 8: Use your knowledge of the other cases such as the Genitive, Dative, and prepositional phrases, and translate those where they best fit in the sentence.
