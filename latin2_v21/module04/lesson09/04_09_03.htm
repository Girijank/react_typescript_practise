"Pastor et Tonsor (Part I)"

Shepherd

Barber

Listen to this conversation and translate this story about a shepherd and barber who have just heard the news the Julius Caesar was killed. Look out for comparative and superlative adjective and adverb forms!

Salve, tonsor. De agris in urbem fortem dictatorem Caesarem videre veni.

Hello, barber. I have come from the fields into the city to see the brave dictator, Caesar.

Salve, pastor. Id est magnum. Sed, ambobus auribus meis clamores in mediis viis audio.

Hello, shepherd. That is great. But I hear with both of my ears shouts in the middle of the streets.

Ante ad te adveni, maximam avem in caelo prope theatrum vidi.

Before I came to you, I saw the greatest bird in the sky near the theatre.

Quid ea fecit?

What did it do?

Ea minorem avem edit.

It ate a smaller bird.

Tum multos viros ferro in manibus eorum super corpus Caesaris vidi.

Then I saw many men with iron in their hands above the body of Caesar.

Tacitissime sto.

Most silently am I standing.

Servi corpus eius celerius rapuerunt et eum ante maiorem turbam populi statim vexerunt.

Slaves rather quickly seized his body and immediately carried it before a rather large crowd.
