Caesar's Tale

Caesar was my kind of soldier. Tough, fierce, war-hungry! Yet, I am afraid he became too powerful…was he trying to compete with me? He just might be the reason why the Roman Republic came to an end. Too much ambition, too much power can be detrimental to the greater good.

If everyone thinks I am too powerful, then let me tell you my own story... I won a great victory against the Gauls in the Gallic Wars. Yes, I should have been granted a Triumph for this. Upon my return to Rome, I immediately set out to enact major reforms for my beloved Roman people. I offered land to my military veterans as I had promised them in the Gallic Wars. I looked over the urban poor and gave grain to them, and also, I sent them to live happily on our Roman colonies of North Africa, Greece, and Anatolia. I consulted the best astronomers and mathematicians to create a new calendar that would calculate the days of the year much better than what we had. I limited the number of terms a governor could hold because I believe that no one should have that much power.Etiam numerum virorum in Senatu auxi.

All of my reforms should have pleased everyone. I even increased the number of jobs in the city, built a public library, cleaned up the dirt and grime in our city. I was doing what was best for the populus Romanus. In 44 BCE, I appointed myself dictator perpetuo. Some say that I now thought of myself as Rex Romae. But remember when Mark Antony tried to crown me with a laurel wreath in the Lupercalia festival? I pushed him away and said only Jupiter could wear a crown of that esteem. It was my fellow Senators who named me Imperator of the next ten years, who crafted an ivory statue of me, who renamed the month of Quinctilus as July in my honor! Who could possibly say that the people didn’t love me, Julius Caesar!
