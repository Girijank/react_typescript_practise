The Assassination of Julius Caesar

The Roman Senate was growing nervous that their power and control in the city was waning. They also could no longer put up with Caesar’s arrogance and feared he would be the downfall of the Republic, so they plotted to assassinate him.

The Plot

There were four conspirators who led the assassination plot: Gaius Trebonius, Decimus Albinus, Gaius Linginus, and Marcus Brutus. Et tu Brute? The men met in secret and planned to kill Caesar during a session of the Senate on March 15th, 44 BCE at the Theater of Pompey.

The Omens

Caesar should not have gone to the Senate on this day. There were plenty of warnings sent to him from the gods. His wife, Calpurnia, had a dream the night before of him bleeding to death in her arms. A soothsayer told him he would be in danger on the Ides of March. In the Theater of Pompey, a bird flew up high and was swiftly devoured by a larger bird. Caesar ignored all of the omens and went to the Senate meeting anyway.

Et tu, Brute?

As he walked into the Senate house, a man by the name of Casca dealt the first blow with his short dagger. Then, the other conspirators began to attack Caesar, striking him 23 times. Caesar lay dead, in front of the statue of Pompey. In shock, the Senators ran away in horror. Caesar’s body was lifted by slaves and carried to his home. The conspirators fled, and they all met their demise in the near future. Rome was in a state of panic, and its leader, its dictator perpetuo, was dead.
