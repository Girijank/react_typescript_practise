Listen to the continuation of this story, practicing your Latin translation along the way. Oh, the suspense!

Estne Caesar mortuus? Mihi responde!

Is Caesar dead? Answer me!

Miserrime, ita.

Most sadly, yes.

Tanta ignomia! Res publica nostra amisa est.

Such a disgrace! Our Republic has been lost.

Iudex asinos, qui regem Caesarem nostrum crudelius necaverunt, castigabit.

A judge will punish the blockheads who rather cruelly killed our king Caesar.

Rex? Caesar rex noster non erat.

King? Caesar was not our king.

Fortasse paulum?

Perhaps a little?

Ne! Is acriorem mentem tenuit sed cognitatio eius erat mala.

No! He possessed a rather sharp mind, but his thinking was wrong.

Caesar erat maior quam Regulus, Coriolanus, itaque Spartacus in mea mente.

Caesar was greater than Regulus, Coriolanus, and also Spartacus in my opinion.

Ob eum, res publica nostra non tutissima facta erat.

On account of him, our Republic had become not the safest.

Es iustus.

You are right.

Ante Roma ipsa in peiorem civitatem cadit, tuum utillimum aes, argentum, ferrum, et lactem rape, itaque urbem pro meliore vita celeriterre linque!

Before Rome itself falls into a worse state, seize your most useful copper, silver, iron, and milk, and quickly abandon the city for a better life!

Ita, vale!

Yes, see ya’!
