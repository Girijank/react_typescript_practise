Comparative and Superlative of Adjectives:

  • For regular adjectives, the comparative is formed by adding -ior (m. and f.), -ius (n.) to the stem/base of the positive (feminine Nominative singular minus the ending).
  • For regular adjectives, the superlative is formed by adding -issimus,-a, -umor -rrimus,-a,-umor -llimus,-a,-um to the base of the positive.
  • Remember to memorize the irregular adverbs comparative and superlative forms.

Hints for translating:

  • The comparative may often be translated ...-er, more ..., too ...
    The superlative may be translated ...-est, most ..., very...
Positive Comparative Superlative
alte – highly altius – more highly altissime – most highly
fortiter – bravely fortius – more bravely fortissime – most bravely

Comparative and Superlative of Adverbs:

  • The comparison of regular adverbs is the comparative neuter singular form of its comparative adjective form.
  • The superlative of regular adverbs uses the base of the superlative adjective form + -e.
  • Remember to memorize the irregular adverbs comparative and superlative forms.

Hints for translating:

  • The comparative may often be translated: more---ly.
  • The superlative may be translated: very---ly.
