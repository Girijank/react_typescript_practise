A Roman Soldier's Story

As you now know, Rome was always engaged in some type of war. It relied heavily on its army for expansionism, power, and control of the Mediterranean. Its soldiers were its lifeblood. But what did the soldiers actually feel about fighting in so many wars?

Telling the tale of a Roman soldier—now that’s a story! So, here’s your chance. We’re transporting you back in time to the age of the Roman Republic. We’ve plopped you down ontothe battlefield of one of Rome’s wars. You’re a soldier now! So, it's time for you to tell your story.

My Disastrous Time in the Samnite War

My goodness, I have landed in the city of Caudium in southern Italy and from the looks of it, I’m in the middle of the Samnite War in 321 BCE! Those men over there dressed in mismatched uniforms, carrying daggers and wearing bronze shields, are Roman soldiers, I believe. Yes, they must be, because I see them launching stones of fire from an onager towards those men over yonder dressed in sparkling armor and wearing decorative bracelets and jewelry. I also see two deep, narrow passes with a watered grassy plain in between a mountain chain. I think I read about this in book nine of Livy’s ab Urbe Condita. I’m in the midst of the battle at the Caudine Forks between the Romans and the Samnites!

The Romans are calling me over Miles, nobiscum fortissime pugna et veni!” Somehow, I understand their Latin! I grab a gladius and begin attacking the Samnites. I recognize my Roman leaders, the consuls Spurius Posthumius Albinus and T. Veturius Calvinusas, fighting alongside me. Etiam, ferociores peditus, equitatus, velites, et hastates Romanos video. I also see that the Samnites are more comfortable with this hilly terrain than we are. We are losing!

I can’t keep up. I have become entrapped within the Caudine Forks, and the Samnites have us surrounded. I hear their leader, Herennius, say that he wants to kill all of us! Luckily, Herrenius’s son, Pontius, offered us an alternative to death. He allowed us to walk under the yoke of the Caudine Forks as a surrender to the Samnite army. But we had to remove our clothing, our paludamenta, and lay down our arms when we did it. Id erat crudellimum! I thought fighting for Rome would be glorious, but not if I’m in one of the few battles in which Rome was embarrassingly defeated! Please return me back home!

Latin Sentence Translation:

  1. Miles, nobiscum fortissime pugna et veni!
  2. Etiam, ferociores peditus, equitatus, velites, et hastates Romanos video.
  3. Id erat crudellimum!
