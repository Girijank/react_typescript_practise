Step 2:

Write your war story narrative. Utilize your research within previous lessons and the research you conducted on your own. You can use the sample war story, “My Disastrous Time in the Samnite War” as a creative guide. Be sure to include the following:

  • Tell a narrative story in English, in a minimum of 250 words, using the details from your research chart (see step 1).
  • Use three sentences written in Latin, embedded into your story. See step 3, below, for Latin sentence specific guidelines.
  • Give a citation of the sources you used for your research, using MLA format .

Step 3:

Latin Writing Assignment: Supply the three sentences in Latin from your narrative, along with their English translations.

  • One sentence must contain a comparative adjective.
  • One sentence must contain a superlative adjective.
  • One sentence must contain either a comparative or superlative adverb.

Example: “My Disastrous Time in the Samnite War”

Sentence 1: “Miles, nobis cum fortissime pugna et veni!” = superlative adverb

Sentence 2: Etiam, ferociores peditus, equitatus, velites, et hastates Romanos video. = comparative adjective

Sentence 3: Id erat crudellimum! = superlative adjective

Mars' Missive: Composing with Comparatives and Superlatives in Latin

Writing sentences in English versus Latin are two very different processes! Visit Composing using Comparatives and Superlatives in Latin in your Toolbox for a step-by-step guide.

When writing in Latin, one challenge many scholars face is the declining of Roman names. Since names are nouns and can be used as such in a sentence, make sure they have the correct case endings! You can visit Latin Name Declining in your Toolbox for a refresher.
